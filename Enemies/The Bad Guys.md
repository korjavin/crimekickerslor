# The Bad Guys — GastroDefense Inc.

The antagonist force the Crime Kickers face is the corporation that built them:
**GastroDefense Inc.**, which started in commercial food safety and pivoted into
automated law enforcement without changing any of its middle management. It built
Project AEGIS-4 (see [[Origin & Concept]]) and, eighteen months after the fracture,
comes looking for its missing prototype.

> The full title of the [[Shadow Kickers (2D Platformer)]] game is literally
> *"Crime Kickers vs The Bad Guys."* GastroDefense is the canon identity behind that
> generic label.

## What GastroDefense is — and isn't

GastroDefense is **soulless, not menacing.** It is evil by quarterly report, not by
monologue. It never sends an emissary; it sends a form. The threat is always a workflow
resuming, a deadline elapsing, a department being notified, a firmware update bolting new
behavior onto a fleet that was never validated for it. OCP from RoboCop is the reference.
There is no dark lord, and there is no boss fight in the usual sense — the closest thing to
a final adversary is a successor product line and a deleted document field.

## The recall — what it actually is

The recall is **not a vendetta.** A law-enforcement client filed a contract performance
notice (**GD-C-4419**) citing GastroDefense's failure to deliver the completed AEGIS-4
prototype. The recall is a compliance step against a liability; the corporation would prefer
to close the file. The four are pursued by a *system*, not a hunter. (Revealed in
[[S01E11 — Closed Loop]].)

Decoded in [[S01E09 — Variance Report]], *"RETURN TO ORIGIN FACILITY"* means
**re-integration = un-make**, irreversible, with no consent field:
- **W-01 ([[Windman]]) and P-03 ([[Primm]])** — "specification outputs," to be reintegrated
  into the prototype (which un-makes all four selves; reintegrated only as a pair).
- **P-02 ([[Pho-boman]]) and T-04 ([[Tiebi]])** — "non-specification outputs," for disposal
  under GD-Standard-7 / GD-Standard-12.

## Apparatus (defined across the arc)

- **The Retrieval Division** — a department with a budget that had not processed a ticket
  since Q3 of the prior fiscal year. Executes the recovery protocol (captures Primm in
  [[S01E10 — Half-Life]] with standard steel containment that incidentally constrains his
  field — procedure, not cunning).
- **The drone net** — the **GD-SURVEY-ENTRÉE … GD-SURVEY-DESSERT** field-survey fleet,
  originally food-safety inspection units, given an unvalidated asset-recovery firmware
  update. **Blind to Pho-boman**, who carries no incident-signature.
- **The recall specification** — *PROJECT AEGIS-4 — UNIT RECALL AND REINTEGRATION
  SPECIFICATION (REV. 7)*, with consent listed as *"[Field not applicable]."* The original
  **Rev. 1 contained a consent field** (Appendix C, "Voluntary Compliance Protocol"),
  removed in Rev. 3 before completion; Appendix C was never written. (Surfaced in
  [[S01E17 — Cascade Failure]].)
- **AEGIS-5** — the successor prototype program. **A specification, not a character;**
  communicates only by form and hardware interrupt. It depends on AEGIS-4 reintegration data
  to finalize its own design, so any recovery failure auto-escalates to it.
- **The law-enforcement client** — visible only as documents (the performance notice; the
  E19 injunction). Their enforcement mechanism beyond the injunction is a beyond-20 engine.

## The AEGIS lineage

"AEGIS-4" was always a count. **AEGIS-1, -2, -3** existed, fractured, and scattered before.
At least one prior reintegration was completed, and one prior non-specification output was
disposed of under GD-Standard-7 (nature unspecified). An AEGIS-3 primary-fragment signal is
still "warm" at a disused facility in the city — never approached on-page. (Decoded in
[[S01E13 — Field Notes]] and [[S01E16 — Prior Art]].)

## Foot soldiers (source material)

The on-the-ground enemies of the games sit downstream of this corporate apparatus:
- **Shadow Kickers (2D platformer):** small red square enemies on patrol routes; killed by
  stomp, dash ([[Primm]]), projectile, or stunned by wind ([[Windman]]). No named bosses.
- **Stealth Kickers (tactical stealth):** guards with vision cones and alert states
  (UNAWARE → SUSPICIOUS → ALERTED) defending an enemy base.
