# Origin & Concept

The top-level premise of the **Crime Kickers** universe. Everything here is drawn
from the approved registry (`crimekickers-init.md`) and is therefore **canon**.
See [[Canon & Sources]] for how this relates to the games.

## The big idea

> Two of the four heroes were **component parts of a single fractured cyborg
> robot**. A third was **born of the same incident** as a separate emergent entity.
> The fourth was a **human bystander transformed by the incident**. As a result of
> the fracture and the incident, all four gained independence and unique properties.

The cyborg, **Project AEGIS-4**, was a prototype for an automated law-enforcement
unit built by **[[The Bad Guys|GastroDefense Inc.]]** It carried two
integrated faculties that became independent on fracture:

- **[[Windman]]** — control of air and wind.
- **[[Primm]]** — gravity, short-range teleportation, phasing.

During the fracture incident (see [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]]), two
additional beings came into existence:

- **[[Pho-boman]]** — a soup-based entity. **Not** a cyborg fragment; born in
  the same moment from broth, heat, and the prototype's empty helmet acting as
  a cradle. He joins the others as a peer.
- **[[Tiebi]]** — a **former soldier** and security officer at GastroDefense Inc.,
  on duty on-site when the incident occurred. The incident transformed him through
  a **supernatural mechanism**, granting volumetric scaling (size-and-shape
  transformation) ability. He is the **only Crime Kicker with a human past**. He is
  not a cyborg fragment.

The fracture itself was a **lucky/random event** — a Lunch Lady's spilled
cauldron of pho meeting an unsealed assembly, not sabotage and not design.

## The team

Having become independent, the four act **together** under the shared name
**Crime Kickers**. The team identity is canon; the internal dynamics, leadership,
and goals are still open for authoring. See [[The Crime Kickers]].

## Tone

Across all source material the universe leans **comedic / absurd-heroic** rather
than grim: ordinary settings (a mall, everyday vehicles, mundane objects) collide
with outsized powers and casual collateral damage. The presentation framing used by
the hub is mock-official — "case files," "field manual," "dossiers," "surveillance
reels" — a tactical/utilitarian veneer over what is essentially slapstick.
