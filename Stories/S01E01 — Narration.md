# S01E01 — Narration (Voiceover)

Spoken-word narration voiceover for [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]], written for
ElevenLabs text-to-speech to play as background narration over a slideshow / motion-
comic of the 5 panels (see [[S01E01 — Panel Prompts]]) and the print [[S01E01 — Captions]].
Written for the ear. Deadpan dry-corporate-comedy voice. ~218 words, ~87 seconds at
150 wpm. Canon-accurate to the 2026-06-14 ratifications.

## Clean script (paste into ElevenLabs)

> Everything in this block is spoken text only — no labels, nothing that shouldn't be
> voiced. Paste as-is.

GastroDefense Inc. had been building Project AEGIS-4 for eleven months.

The executive gave it eleven minutes. Skip harmonization — all of it — Friday demo, done. The lead engineer nodded. The junior engineer hugged the helmet and said nothing.

Lunch hour. The lab was empty except for him — still there, mid-sentence, leaning close to a headless chassis that couldn't hear a word. Behind him, the wrong cart hit the right cable. A cauldron tipped. And a long, glistening arc of pho broth — noodles trailing like comet-tails, one green sprig of cilantro spinning perfectly in the air — sailed across a beige fluorescent laboratory toward an open visor, while nobody watched.

For one impossible heartbeat, it was whole.

Then the seams found it — three ways at once — and the chassis cracked into three, and the helmet, full of soup now, glowing, detached. And from the broth inside it, something else was born. Not split. Born.

Four new beings. Four different ceilings above them. Each one waking up — if that's the word — with no past, no name, and no idea there had ever been anything else.

The Lunch Lady looked into the cauldron. Said one word. Moved on.

They walked out in four directions and did not look back — one into the wind, one still in the soup, one through a wall, one so small he rode out on a lapel pin without the lapel noticing. The corporation filed the paperwork before the steam had cleared.

Project AEGIS-4 — Status: Recalled.

They didn't know that yet. They were still learning to breathe.

## ElevenLabs delivery notes

- **Voice character:** male or androgynous, dry, mid-to-low register. A documentary
  narrator who has stopped caring about the outcome, but not yet about the sentence.
  Unhurried, flat affect that only occasionally lets something through — on "something
  else was born. Not split. Born." and "They were still learning to breathe."
- **Pacing:** let the paragraph breaks be real pauses (≥ a half-beat). "Project
  AEGIS-4 — Status: Recalled." should land like a rubber stamp — clean, final, just a
  little wrong.
- **Settings:** marginally slower than conversational; the prose carries the drama, not
  the voice. If available, expressiveness ~30–40%, stability ~70%. The deadpan is the
  performance.
- **Voice archetypes to audition:** "George," "Harry," "Arnold," or any voice tagged
  documentary / corporate / narrator with a dry, low-affect character. Avoid
  enthusiastic / warm / young.
