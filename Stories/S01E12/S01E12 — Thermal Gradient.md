# S01E12 — Thermal Gradient

> **Logline:** The one the sensors can't see walks into the building that wants them all, leaves a trail of evidence only a nickel sticker can read, and comes back holding the clock.

---

## Title

**"Thermal Gradient"** — the temperature differential between two zones that drives a measurable flow of heat. Here it is literal (Pho-boman at 94°C moving through cold-storage adjacencies, leaving evidence wherever cheap materials meet his heat) and structural: information moves from inside to outside, from the corporation's database to the team's working knowledge, through the only channel the drone net cannot block. The gradient is also the episode's visual engine: the expensive system stays blind while a nickel label marks his entire route. He is the medium by which the difference between what the building knows and what the three outside need resolves — and the medium is hot.

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page**. The solo-infiltration register is quieter than the four-body convergences of E08 and the corporate-parallel structure of E09; five panels is the correct budget for a solo run with one handoff. The episode has two visual registers that never share a panel: the **outside** (three figures at a street-level remove from GastroDefense HQ, night, waiting) and the **inside** (warm institutional corridors, cold-storage adjacencies, a transfer cradle and a staging terminal). Pho-boman carries both registers in his body — reddish-orange warmth moving through a building of cold fluorescent white, leaving evidence he cannot erase.

The visual space is **GastroDefense HQ** and its immediate exterior — not the district substation from E09 but the main operational building, larger, more institutional, with a food-safety drone fleet perimeter and a loading-dock approach. The building does not monologue. Its expensive systems find nothing. Its cheap materials tell the whole story.

The **broth level** is the episode's tension meter: warm and vigorous in P1 (resolve); visible glow deepening through P2–P3 (the cold draining him as he goes deeper); noticeably lower by the time he takes the cold steel tag in P4 (the cost); still lower in P5 (the handoff, the visible price). The **belly-dragon** is his reaction-shot: shivers faintly near cold, stills when he registers Primm, recoils slightly when he takes the cold object. It never literally animates; it reads through folds and camera angle.

Captions are the dry case-file narrator throughout. Dialogue is minimal. The comedy register is operational — the expensive system cannot find him; a five-cent cold-chain sticker has been marking his route since he crossed the forecourt.

## Panels

### Panel 1 — Perimeter Read

**Image:** Exterior, night. The three figures at a street-level remove from GastroDefense HQ — a wide, low-angle shot that frames the building's approach zone: a loading-dock forecourt, a chain-link perimeter fence, institutional lighting from wall-mounted floods, cold vapor drifting from an industrial vent on the building's lower face. The building is large and administrative in the institutional style — cinder-block and glass-panel upper floors, the loading dock a wide roll-door on the lower face, drone-parking housings mounted at the roof edge.

Two GD-SURVEY drones are visible in the mid-air above the forecourt, running their sweep in opposite directions — the overlap of their patrol arcs forms a visible criss-cross above the approach zone. Their sensor arrays point downward. Callsigns on housing: *GD-SURVEY-PLAT-01* and *GD-SURVEY-DESSERT-02.* They are conducting their standard combined ambient-temperature-plus-asset-recovery sweep.

The three figures are on the far side of the fence, in shadow: Windman slightly forward, fan open, blue pressure ghost-lines mapping the building's mass profile and the drone arcs through the air — he has been reading the sweep pattern for eleven minutes. Tiebi to his left, beret straight, one hand at his side, the other gesturing forward to trace a corridor layout in the air — the layout he carried in memory from two years of badge-in mornings. Pho-boman between them, small and spoon-shaped against the fence mesh, noodle-brows in the focused-attention configuration, looking at the gap between the two drone arcs. Broth warm and vigorous in the visor — deep reddish-orange, visibly bubbling. Dragon-belly forward. The cold vapor from the building's lower vent is at the right of the frame: the only thing in the forecourt that is colder than Pho-boman.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The building had a perimeter drone sweep on an eleven-minute rotation.*
- CAPTION (below): *One of them knew the interior floor plan. One of them could read the sweep pattern in the air.*
- CAPTION (below): *One of them the sweep could not find.*
- TIEBI (flat, tracing the air): *Sublevel access is through the freight junction. Cold storage is on your left going down. Stay right.*

**Why it lands:** The establishing panel delivers the operational problem and its solution in a single frame without exposition. Tiebi's line is a navigation brief, not a speech — and "stay right" is both an instruction and a concession to Pho-boman's cold vulnerability without making it into drama. The broth is vigorous here: that reading matters more once it begins to drop. The cold vapor from the lower vent is the first thermal-gradient note — the building already advertising what it runs on.

### Panel 2 — The Trail

**Image:** Split-register panel, but the right half now shows the facilities management system — not the drone operations grid.

**Left half:** Pho-boman, mid-forecourt, walking at Pho-boman speed — the small deliberate waddling gait, each step placed on the warmer side of the approach. He has passed through the gap between the drone arcs. The arcs are visible above him in faint ghost-line sensor-sweep register, passing over him and continuing their rotation on the other side without annotating him. He is visible to the reader. The drones do not annotate him.

At the edge of the forecourt wall, barely in frame, a detail: a small cold-chain monitoring sticker — the five-cent temperature-excursion indicator mounted to building infrastructure at waist height, part of the food-safety compliance grid that runs throughout the facility. The sticker, as Pho-boman passes within range, has turned **permanently crimson**. A small icon on it: *TEMPERATURE EXCURSION — DISCARD.* The sticker does not sound an alarm. It is a sticker. It has changed color. That is all it does.

His noodle-brows are slightly raised, asymmetric — the pre-conclusion configuration. The reddish-orange broth glow is the only warm color on this half.

**Right half:** Not the drone grid. The GastroDefense **facilities management console** — a different system entirely, cheaper-looking, smaller fonts, a grid of building-zone status indicators in institutional green. One zone indicator in the grid is amber: *ZONE: CORRIDOR-FORECOURT-PERIMETER / COLD-CHAIN STATUS: EXCURSION DETECTED / READING: 94.0°C (THRESHOLD: 8°C) / STICKER ID: CCS-F3-07 — STATUS: CRIMSON / ACTION REQUIRED: CLEANUP WORK ORDER ISSUED / ORDER: CWO-0441 / ASSIGNED: NIGHT-SHIFT MAINTENANCE / STATUS: DISPATCHED.*

Below the amber entry, in small plain text: *ASSET-RECOVERY CROSS-REFERENCE: NOT APPLICABLE (COLD-CHAIN EXCURSION — SEE FOOD-SAFETY QUEUE). DRONE NET: NOT NOTIFIED.*

The drone net is not notified. The work order is dispatched. A janitor with a mop is now walking toward the trail.

- LEFT CAPTION (below): *It found nothing.*
- LEFT CAPTION (below, smallest): *The cold-chain sticker found something and turned red.*

### Panel 3 — The Gradient

**Image:** Interior. A wide corridor on sublevel 3 — bare concrete, cable conduit, cold strip lighting. The air is perceptibly colder than the forecourt. Deeper inside, the building runs colder.

Pho-boman is past the T-junction, having already committed to the right branch; the cold-storage door is in the far background at the left edge of the frame — he is not near it, but it is in the same frame. His body shows the cost: the broth level in the visor is **visibly lower** than in P1 — still warm, still reddish-orange, but the vigorous bubbling has quieted to a slower, more deliberate roll. The belly-dragon's folds are pulled slightly inward — the body's thermal response to the ambient cold around him.

He has stopped, mid-corridor, a few paces from the freight-bay entrance. His noodle-brows are in a new configuration: one slightly higher than the other, held — the configuration of a being who has registered something unexpected in a direction he was not planning to look. He is facing the freight-bay entrance (and the transfer cradle beyond it) but his attention has shifted. His scent-field, operating through the ambient chemistry of the sublevel 3 air, has found something it recognizes: a specific signature — familiar, the scent of one of the others — coming from inside the steel-frame containment unit at the far end of the freight bay. The unit is visible at the edge of the frame, heavy and dark, its mass implicit. Pho-boman does not approach it. He cannot open it. Approaching it means going deeper into the cold, and the janitor is somewhere behind him.

He stays where he is. He faces the freight bay. He does not move toward the unit. After a moment — unmarked in the panel, held in the broth-stillness — he turns toward the transfer cradle and the routing tag.

In the background of the corridor, out of focus: the slow approach of a janitorial cart, its handle visible around the far corner, the human figure behind it barely readable. It has not seen him. It is following a work order.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The building got colder the deeper he went.*
- CAPTION (below): *He registered it by the second sub-level — the broth pulled inward, the way it did near the cold-storage door.*
- CAPTION (below): *He found Primm on his way to the cradle.*
- CAPTION (below): *He kept walking.*

**Why it lands:** The panel is doing three things simultaneously, and none of them crowds the others. The broth-level drop is the thermal-gradient made visible — the reader can see the cold winning without a caption naming it. The dragon-fold is the body's quiet distress signal. The scent-find and walk-away is the episode's emotional heart: "He found Primm on his way to the cradle. / He kept walking." is the plainest possible way to write the hardest thing in the episode. The captions do not say it cost him; they do not need to. The janitor in the background corner is the active tension note — not close enough to be a crisis, close enough to be a clock. All three threads (cold cost, heart, pursuer) are present in a single panel at the same moment they would naturally converge.

The belly-dragon's folds pull inward more sharply. His noodle-brows are level and held — concentrated, committed.

The staging-area terminal glows in the background. The CATERING-004A cross-reference is on that screen. It is not on the tag. Pho-boman is not looking at the terminal; he has the tag. The reader sees both.

In the corridor entrance behind him: the janitorial cart is now in the frame. The night-janitor is visible — a figure in a maintenance uniform, mop extended, looking down at the work order on his handheld screen, reading the next zone flag. He has not looked up. He has eyes. **Why it lands:** The caption staircase is the episode's sharpest sequence, upgraded from the terminal-read version. The janitor with his eyes down is the purest expression of the episode's tone: voiceless, indifferent, a workflow — and he is ten paces away. The tension peak is a man looking at a handheld screen.

### Panel 5 — Outside, With a Tag

**Image:** Exterior, night. The angle has inverted from P1 — now from inside the forecourt looking out toward the fence and the dark street. Pho-boman is through the fence and crossing the final meter of forecourt shadow back to where Windman and Tiebi wait. He is walking at Pho-boman speed, but the gait is subtly slower than the forecourt approach in P2 — the cold has taken something.

The three figures come into a loose cluster at the fence line. Windman is exactly as left: fan slightly open — a small involuntary deployment, not aimed, not firing — bandaged face angled toward Pho-boman. Pressure ghost-lines residual and quiet, having tracked Pho-boman's warm mass through the building's sublevels from outside. He has a general shape of the route; he needs the specific. Tiebi is to his right, beret straight, mustard-gold shirt catching the peripheral flood-light. The expression of a man who has been standing still for eleven minutes on the outside of a building he used to have a badge for.

Pho-boman **sets the routing tag on the ground** between them — the small cold steel rectangle on the cracked concrete. He does not speak. He does not need to: Tiebi crouches, reads the stamp, reads the clock. Windman's fan opens that small involuntary degree as he processes what Tiebi's body language is telling him.

Then Pho-boman does one thing more. The belly-dragon — which has been pulled inward since sublevel 3, the folds of distress from the cold — releases slightly, unfolds a degree, and one fold of the embroidery points loosely toward the building. Not literally animated; the camera angle and the fold catch the light in a particular way. Tiebi looks at it. He understands: alive. Still there. The dragon's folds carry the one thing the tag cannot.

Pho-boman's broth is low and quiet in the visor — the vigorous bubbling of P1 is gone; the surface is shallow and still, the level noticeably reduced. He has spent the warmth on the job.

At the upper corner of the panel, barely visible at the building's roofline: the underside of GD-SURVEY-PLAT-01 completing its next arc. Callsign annotation at smallest legible size: *GD-SURVEY-PLAT-01 — SWEEP PASS: GRID-F / ANOMALY FLAGS: 0 / STATUS: ROUTINE.* Somewhere in the building behind the camera, a janitor has arrived at the freight bay with a mop and a work order, and found an empty room and a missing routing tag. The work order will be closed as completed. The discrepancy will go into the facilities queue. These things take time.

## Differentiation / what's new

The diaspora issues (E02–E05) each put one hero alone with one problem of self-discovery. E08 put all four together on one problem of convergence. E12 puts one hero alone inside an enemy structure — the first time the series runs a solo-inside-the-apparatus beat.

What is new here:
- **The liability-as-asset inversion, made visual.** Pho-boman is invisible to the expensive system and flagged red by the cheap one. Both facts are true simultaneously. The series has not previously put this contradiction in the same frame.
- **The broth-level as tension meter.** Pho-boman's visor has always shown his state; E12 is the first episode to track it across five panels as a measurable resource that depletes. The reader sees P1's vigorous full broth against P5's quiet shallow surface and does the accounting.
- **The belly-dragon as emotional performance.** His reaction-shot, carried through folds and camera angle, is the episode's substitute for dialogue and facial expression. The dragon-fold-release in P5 is the episode's most emotional moment, and it is communicated through textile folds.
- **The walk-away.** He finds Primm by scent and keeps walking. The series has not previously given Pho-boman a moment that costly. The captions do not say it cost him. That is the correct register.
- **The silent handoff.** His longest previous report was four sentences in the previous draft; here he sets down a piece of cold steel and lets Tiebi read. The mouthless hero delivers the most important intelligence of the arc without opening a visor that has no mouth. The belly-dragon does the rest.
- **The janitor thread.** The episode's single active tension thread — not a hunt, a workflow. The distinction is load-bearing: everything in GastroDefense is indifferent. The janitor mops because the work order says to mop.

- The 72-hour clock is running. E13 opens below 48 hours.
