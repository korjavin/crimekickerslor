# S01E12 — Narration (Voiceover)

Spoken-word narration voiceover for [[S01E12 — Thermal Gradient]], written for ElevenLabs
text-to-speech to play as background narration over a slideshow / motion-comic of the five
panels (see [[S01E12 — Panel Prompts]]). Written for the ear. Dry case-file narrator — the
same voice family as prior episodes. This is the solo-infiltration episode: the one the
sensors cannot see walks into GastroDefense HQ, finds where Primm is held and the clock, and
walks back out. The narrator tracks one cold fact at a time, in the same flat tone the
building runs on. ~210 words, ~90 seconds at 150 wpm.

Note: E12 has one line of **dialogue** — Tiebi's navigation brief in Panel 1 ("Sublevel
access is through the freight junction. Cold storage is on your left going down. Stay
right.") — which is on-page lettering, not the narration track; do not voice it. Pho-boman is
**silent and has no mouth**; he never speaks. No SFX fires in this episode (he uses no
power). The in-panel institutional text — the cold-chain sticker, the facilities console and
its work order, the cold steel routing tag, the CATERING-004A field, the drone roofline
annotation — is in-panel lettering; do not voice it as narration.

## Clean script (paste into ElevenLabs)

> Everything in this block is spoken text only — no labels, nothing that shouldn't be
> voiced. Paste as-is.

The building had a perimeter drone sweep on an eleven-minute rotation.

One of the three knew its floor plan. One could read the sweep in the air.

One of them the sweep could not find.

He walked in through the gap between two drone arcs. They passed over him and found nothing — the expensive system, working exactly as designed.

A five-cent sticker on the wall found him instead. It turned red. It did not sound an alarm; it is a sticker. But a work order was issued, and a man with a mop began walking toward the trail.

The building got colder the deeper he went. The broth in his helmet pulled inward.

On his way to the cradle, he caught a scent he knew. Primm. Alive, behind steel, a few meters away.

He could not open the cage. He kept walking.

The routing tag was clipped to the transfer cradle. He took it. It was cold, and carrying it cost him, and the janitor had not looked up yet.

Outside, he set the tag on the ground. He did not speak — he had no mouth, and he did not need one. Tiebi read it. Sublevel three. The chamber. Seventy-two hours. Hold active.

Then the dragon on his belly unfolded a little, toward the building. Alive. Still there.

The dragon told them the rest.

Seventy-two hours was a number they could work with.

## Runtime

- **Word count:** ~215 words.
- **Runtime:** ~85 s at 150 wpm; ~92 s at 140 wpm. With the tuned pauses — the held beat
  on "He kept walking." and the staccato "Sublevel three. The chamber. Seventy-two hours." —
  the track plays nearer **~95–100 s**.
- "He kept walking." carries the episode's emotional weight and its longest mid-track break.
  Deliver it flat, not sad — it is a fact, and the fact is the cost. Do not shorten the break.
- "Seventy-two hours was a number they could work with." is the last line. The closing break
  is absent by design — the track ends on that phrase, the clock started, no air after.

## ElevenLabs delivery notes

- **Voice character:** the same dry, mid-to-low documentary narrator as [[S01E01 — Narration]]
  through [[S01E11 — Narration]] — deadpan, unhurried, flat affect. The dryness is uniform.
- **The inversion ("the expensive system, working exactly as designed" / "a five-cent sticker
  found him instead"):** the episode's central joke, delivered completely flat. The comedy is
  that both systems are working correctly; the voice must not signal the irony — the words do.
- **"He kept walking." — the heart:** the hardest beat in the episode. Pho-boman finds the
  teammate he came for and walks past because the plan requires it. Deliver it flat and let
  the break sit. It is **not** sad-acted; the cost lives in the plainness.
- **"It was cold, and carrying it cost him":** flat. The cost is physical (a 94°C being
  carrying cold steel) and the narration states it like an inventory line, not a lament.
- **"He did not speak — he had no mouth":** a dry, deadpan fact about a mouthless hero
  delivering the most important intelligence of the arc without a word. No wink.
- **"Sublevel three. The chamber. Seventy-two hours. Hold active.":** four clipped facts, the
  way you'd read a tag — short, even, no rising drama. This is Tiebi reading, rendered in the
  narrator's voice; the on-page tag text itself is not separately voiced.
- **What not to voice:** Tiebi's Panel 1 navigation brief (speech balloon); and all in-panel
  institutional text — the cold-chain sticker, the facilities console / work order, the cold
  steel routing tag, the CATERING-004A field, the drone roofline annotation. None of these
  appear in the clean script above. No SFX fires in this episode.
- **Pacing:** the two slowest moments are "He kept walking." (Panel 3 — the walk-away) and the
  final line (Panel 5 — the clock). Let both sit. Everything else moves at the series'
  standard unhurried pace.
- **Settings:** expressiveness ~30–40%, stability ~70%. Deadpan is the performance.
- **Voice archetypes to audition:** match the voice chosen for prior episodes — "George,"
  "Harry," "Arnold," or series-established equivalent. The narrator is one person across the
  series. Avoid enthusiastic / warm / alarmed / dramatic.
