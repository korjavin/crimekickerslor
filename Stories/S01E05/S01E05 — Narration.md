# S01E05 — Narration (Voiceover)

Spoken-word narration voiceover for [[S01E05 — Hold Your Own Weight]], written for
ElevenLabs text-to-speech to play as background narration over a slideshow / motion-
comic of the five panels (see [[S01E05 — Panel Prompts]]) and the print [[S01E05 — Captions]].
Written for the ear. Dry case-file narrator — the same family as prior diaspora episodes
but **drier and more diagnostic**: the comedy of a being who looks like a finished
superhero and cannot stay on a floor. ~255 words, ~102 seconds at 150 wpm.

## Clean script (paste into ElevenLabs)

> Everything in this block is spoken text only — no labels, nothing that shouldn't be
> voiced. Paste as-is.

He had been awake for approximately one second.

He was already somewhere he had not chosen to be. Both of these things were going to remain true for a while.

He reached for the ceiling rail. The ceiling rail was not interested. He tried the railing. The railing had the same opinion. The floor below that was completely consistent in its views.

He had been trying to stop phasing. He was not, it turned out, in control of the phasing. Something else had been on. Something else had been on the whole time.

He stopped trying to grab the world. He turned his own weight back on. The floor caught him. It turned out floors do that.

He stood in a sub-floor service corridor with the light bending faintly at arm's reach — the gravity field, pulling back to where it belonged, no longer running full-tilt through his own body. He had been falling for some floors. He had looked, during all of it, exactly like a superhero making a controlled entrance. He was aware of this. He was not going to bring it up.

He descended the stairwell — one boot on a step, the other landing below it, the twin blades steady on his back, the mini-orbs in tight orbit, the purple coat moving with him toward the street. He did not look up at the floors he had passed through.

Primm.

## Runtime

- **Word count:** ~255 words.
- **Runtime:** ~102 s at 150 wpm; ~109 s at a more deliberate 140 wpm. With the tuned
  pauses and the two dry landings ("It turned out floors do that." and "Primm.") it plays
  nearer **~108–112 s** — the longest of the diaspora narrations, because Panel 2's five
  accumulating failures and Panel 4's dual-beat (eureka + dignity-preservation) both
  need room.
- If you need it under 90 s, the Panel-4 extended passage ("He stood in a sub-floor
  service corridor…He was not going to bring it up.") is texture and can be cut — the
  four-caption eureka carries the beat on its own.

## ElevenLabs delivery notes

- **Voice character:** same dry, mid-to-low documentary narrator as [[S01E01 — Narration]]
  and [[S01E02 — Narration]] — but **more precise and more compressed** than the prior
  diaspora voices. Where E04's narrator is warm and slow (a being with no urgency), E05's
  narrator is efficient and clipped: the comedy lands through accumulation and timing, not
  through warmth. Flat affect throughout, with one controlled exception on "He was not going
  to bring it up." — a dry beat that can carry the faintest note of professional sympathy.
  Keep continuity by auditioning the **same voice** used for prior episodes.
- **Panel 2 pacing:** the five-item failure list should accelerate very slightly with each
  entry — ceiling rail, railing, floor — not a rush, but the slight tightening of a narrator
  who is listing things that keep happening in exactly the same way. "The floor below that
  was completely consistent in its views." should land with a half-beat of extra air before
  it, marking the shift from slapstick to the building having opinions.
- **"It turned out floors do that."** is the issue's comic thesis — delivered flat,
  completely without triumph, as a bureaucratic observation. Do not let the voice warm or
  lift here. The comedy is the total absence of any sense of achievement.
- **"Primm." is a quiet offhand note.** Same delivery as "Windman." (E02), "T." (E03), and
  "Pho-boman." (E04) — the case-file observing what is already true, almost under its
  breath. No lift, no announcement. He does not need the ceremony. He is already walking.
- **Settings:** marginally slower than conversational; expressiveness ~30–40%, stability
  ~70%. The deadpan carries the performance. Do not rush Panel 2 — pace the list so each
  refusal lands before the next one begins.
- **Voice archetypes to audition:** the dry documentary / low-affect narrator family
  ("George," "Harry," "Arnold" or similar) — match whatever was chosen for S01E01 through
  S01E04 so the show's narrator stays one person. Avoid enthusiastic / warm-heroic /
  young-energetic.
