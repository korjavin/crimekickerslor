# S01E06 — Panel Prompts

Image-generation prompts and episode-specific visual canon for
[[S01E06 — Two-Body Problem]] (the first reunion issue — Windman + Tiebi). Built by
the **panel-prompt-smith** agent.

> Each prompt is **self-contained** — image models have no memory, so every prompt
> restates the art style, both characters, and the props in full. The global art style
> and recurring descriptors live in [[Visual Style Guide]]; reuse the wordings below
> **verbatim** across panels for consistency.

This issue is **one comic page resolved across six images** (P1–P6). It is the series'
**first relational episode**: two heroes in one frame, the first real dialogue, the
first ending that closes on more than one figure. The space is tight and specific — a
**single dark city block at night**, one streetlamp, one incident. **Both Windman and
Tiebi are present** (Windman from P1, Tiebi from P2); the only other figures are an
unnamed hooded **runner** (P3–P5) and a barely-glimpsed **person left behind** (P3,
background). No other hero, no team name. The seam: this is a **free-standing Act III
opener** — **P1** re-establishes [[S01E02 — Feel the Room]]'s pressure-map perception
(Windman, weeks later, drifting the city after his E02 street-level exit), **P2** picks
up [[S01E03 — Momentum Not Included]]'s Tiebi (weeks later, moving through the city
after his E03 deck exit), and **P5** pays forward [[S01E03 — Momentum Not Included]]
Panel 5's "sprint first, then grow" eureka with the **STOMP** SFX deployed in the field
for the first time. **P6 inverts** the solo-exit ending of every diaspora issue (E02
P5B, E03 P6, E05 P5): two figures, one street, same direction, neither named as a team.

## Episode visual canon

### Windman — appearance (verbatim from [[Windman]])

[[Windman]]'s character page is the single source of truth for his look. Reuse this
descriptor block **verbatim** in every panel he appears in (scaled to camera distance —
full block in close/medium shots, signature-color cue only in far shots):

> a humanoid man, male, 180 cm tall, moderately stout with a slightly loose but
> stable physique, normal proportions, not heavily muscled; short natural-brown
> chestnut hair; a calm, soft-featured slightly-rounded face with no beard or
> mustache. Across his eyes, a thick cloth EYE BANDAGE that completely covers both
> eyes — top portion blue, lower portion (above the eyebrows) red — fastened near the
> ears, fitting tightly to the face, leaving NO visible eyes. He wears a fitted blue
> T-shirt (not tight), sleeveless or short sleeves to mid-shoulder, bearing a single
> large white letter "W" monogram on the chest. He wears brown knee-length straight-cut
> slightly-baggy shorts, and fully-black flat-soled snug boots with no decoration.

### Windman's fan (verbatim from [[Windman]] / [[S01E02 — Panel Prompts]])

Reuse this exact wording every time the fan appears. Track its **open/closed state**
per panel (the fan-state timeline below is a continuity guard):

> a small handheld folding fan held in his RIGHT hand, brown wood-textured handle about
> 25 cm long, three rounded slightly-transparent blades about 10 cm each colored green,
> blue, and red, evenly spaced around a central hub

- **P1:** open in the right hand, blades spread, held low at his side, not aimed.
- **P2:** still open at his side; his head has turned toward the new signal.
- **P3:** open, swinging BACK in preparation as he pivots toward the street.
- **P4:** open, completing one deliberate lateral compression sweep.
- **P5:** folded shut at his side as he walks calmly toward the lamp's pool.
- **P6:** folded shut in the right hand, not aimed.

### Tiebi — appearance (verbatim from [[Tiebi]], with mustard accent rendered)

[[Tiebi]]'s character page is the single source of truth for his look. Reuse this
descriptor block **verbatim** in every panel he appears in (scaled to camera distance
and to his current size — full block at full height; the same identity cues, never
abbreviated, at micro scale):

> a humanoid man, male, ~175–180 cm tall at normal size, medium build, slightly lean
> but healthy, realistic human proportions; an ordinary human head, a neutral and
> friendly medium-skin-toned face, no facial hair, normal expressive eyes (eyes clearly
> visible, NOT covered or blindfolded); his signature feature is a classic French beret,
> slightly tilted, dark navy, snug and soft with a small nub at the top center; he wears
> a simple fitted shirt/tunic in MUSTARD-GOLD (his signature accent color), plain dark
> trousers, and simple flat dark shoes.

- **Mustard accent:** his shirt/tunic is **MUSTARD-GOLD**, his signature warm color —
  one of the two warm notes on the dark block (the other being the streetlamp's pool).
  Reuse the exact phrase **"shirt/tunic in MUSTARD-GOLD"** every time.
- **Identity-across-scales rule (the two non-negotiable cues):** at every scale, the two
  cues that make him read as the SAME person are the **dark-navy tilted French beret**
  and the **mustard-gold shirt**. They must be present and legible at postage-stamp
  scale (P4 micro-sprint) exactly as at full height (P2, P3, P5 roll, P6).
- **Beret-state guard:** the beret is at its **proper slight tilt** in every panel except
  the immediate aftermath of the roll in P5 (knocked, but **somehow still on**), and is
  **straightened back to its proper tilt** in P6.

### Scale-rendering register (verbatim from [[S01E03 — Panel Prompts]])

> Tiebi is the project's size-shifter, so the same character must read as the SAME
> person at **postage-stamp / micro scale** and at **full human height** within one
> issue. Two rules:

- **Micro scale (world-as-giant from his POV):** when Tiebi is postage-stamp-sized, do
  NOT shrink the scene — render the world AROUND him at giant scale so HE reads as tiny
  by contrast (here, the gap between two parked cars rendered as a giant concrete canyon,
  the curb a cliff, the asphalt receding like a plaza). He stays a perfectly formed,
  perfectly proportioned, fully human miniature (NOT a chibi, NOT a child, NOT distorted)
  — just very small. His **dark-navy tilted beret** and **mustard-gold shirt** stay
  legible even at this scale.
- **Full scale (normal staging):** when Tiebi is full-sized (~175–180 cm), stage him as
  an ordinary-height man in a normal night-street environment, same beret and same
  mustard shirt, no scale tricks. The *contrast* between the two registers across panels
  is the storytelling.

### Airmaster pressure-perception overlay (verbatim from [[Visual Style Guide]] / [[S01E02 — Panel Prompts]])

The canon render of [[Windman]]'s blind pressure-perception — how he "feels" structure,
objects, and bodies through displaced air without seeing them. Reuse this block
**verbatim** in every panel that uses it (P1, P2, P3, P4; absent by P5–P6 as the
incident closes). Always label which elements are the **lit camera layer** (what the
audience sees) versus this **ghost-line overlay** (what Windman perceives), and keep
Windman himself a normally-rendered lit figure (the overlay is the block he reads, never
him):

> a "pressure-map / ghost-line" perception overlay rendered in a DISTINCT visual
> register, clearly separate from the lit, visible scene: tighter finer ghost-line
> linework, slightly desaturated and tonally cooler, semi-transparent. In this overlay,
> objects are NOT drawn as normal lit visible objects — instead they appear as
> air-displacement silhouettes and concentric pressure-rings / contour-lines that trace
> their shape in moving air, like a blind man's sonar drawn in wind. This overlay is
> "what HE perceives," layered over or behind "what the camera and audience see"; the
> two layers must read as clearly different so the model does not simply draw a normal
> lit scene.

**The Tiebi-anomaly flutter (episode-specific, P2 + P4).** The one new visual problem of
the issue: how the pressure-overlay reads a size-shifter as an anomaly. When Tiebi is in
the overlay, his ghost-lines do NOT read as the stable dense mass-shadow of a parked car
nor as the steady warm blip of a normal body — they read as **a shape that does not
hold**: the pressure-contours around his figure flutter, collapse and expand slightly as
his body makes unconscious micro-calibrations of its size, never settling into one fixed
mass-signature. Render this as the ghost-line contours around Tiebi **rippling and
unstable** (not random noise — a coherent silhouette whose scale keeps subtly
breathing), clearly different from the steady ghost-shapes of the cars and walls around
him.

### Ghost-image / size-contrast technique (verbatim from [[Visual Style Guide]] / [[S01E03 — Panel Prompts]])

Used in **P4** to show the micro-sprint and the start of the carried momentum, and in
**P5** for the sprint-into-roll arrival. Rendered as two clearly-staged instances of the
same character within one panel (one slightly desaturated "earlier" instance, one
full-color "later/now" instance), NOT a blurry double-exposure and NOT a whole-frame
motion blur — so it stays inside the clean hand-inked flat-color look. Reuse this block
**verbatim** wherever it applies:

> a SINGLE-PANEL "ghost-image" sequence showing one continuous action as TWO
> clearly-staged instances of the SAME character in one frame, both cleanly drawn in the
> flat cel style (NOT a blurred double-exposure, NOT a whole-frame motion blur): an
> EARLIER instance rendered slightly DESATURATED and tonally cooler (the "before" beat),
> and a LATER instance rendered in FULL saturated color (the "now" beat), the two read
> left-to-right as one motion across the panel. Keep the character's identity cues
> identical in both instances so they read as the same person; vary only their SIZE and
> pose to show the physics.

### The night block / setting & palette (episode-specific)

The whole issue occupies **one narrow city block at night** — the stage. Reuse this
descriptor for the block surfaces in every panel:

> a narrow city block at night — dark blue-grey buildings rising on both sides, a row of
> parked cars along the curb, cracked asphalt and a concrete sidewalk, a single cast-iron
> streetlamp casting one warm yellow pool of light on the pavement, the rest of the block
> in deep cool shadow

- **Palette (dark-and-lonely tone):** dark blue-grey night — deep cool shadow, the block
  almost monochrome, with the **one warm streetlamp pool** the single warm pocket of the
  frame. This is the deliberate inverse of the diaspora issues' institutional
  fluorescent: warm pooled lamplight, not flat cold overhead light.
- **The "one warm pool" rule:** the only warm/saturated notes anywhere are **the
  streetlamp's yellow pool of light**, **Tiebi's mustard-gold shirt**, and **the
  green/blue/red blades of Windman's fan** (a small accent where the lamp catches them).
  Everything else stays dark blue-grey. Always add the negative "no warm or saturated
  lighting across the block — only the streetlamp's pool, the mustard shirt, and the fan
  blades carry warm color."

### The runner (episode-specific — P3–P5)

A body in motion, never a face, never a name, never a line. Reuse this wording wherever
the runner appears:

> a hooded figure moving fast, face hidden in shadow under the hood, dark nondescript
> clothing, clutching something small and taken (a bag strap or a phone, unclear),
> rendered as a body in motion and NOT as an identifiable face or named villain

### The person left behind (episode-specific — P3 background only)

> at extreme background distance, barely in frame, a single ordinary pedestrian
> stumbling to a halt with one hand out for balance — not chasing, not a focused
> character, no clear face, present only as the context the runner just left behind

### Per-panel continuity, reveal & palette timeline (guard)

Use as a checklist so no panel renders the wrong figure-count, beat, scale, or palette.
The block is **dark blue-grey night**; the **streetlamp pool, the mustard shirt, and the
fan blades** are the only warm notes.

- **P1 — Pressure Map, Singular — WINDMAN ALONE, PRESSURE-OVERLAY.** High-and-side view
  of the quiet block. Windman stands still in the shadow BETWEEN two parked cars
  (deliberately NOT under the lamp), fan OPEN low at his side, bandaged face turned
  slightly away, chin lifted — listening with his whole body, at ease in the dark.
  Pressure-overlay active: parked cars as dense mass-shadows, walls as hard reflective

  register. Quiet, lonely.

  block, same angle. Tiebi, full size, walks at the FAR end at the edge of the
  streetlamp's pool, normal pace, NOT aware of Windman. In the foreground between the
  cars, Windman's head has TURNED toward him, fan still open. Pressure-overlay: Tiebi's
  ghost-lines FLUTTER — the anomaly flutter (a shape that does not hold), clearly
  different from the steady car/wall ghost-shapes. They have not spoken; Tiebi does not
  know Windman exists. Low-key tonal shift.
- **P3 — Incident — THREE FIGURES + BACKGROUND, GEAR-SHIFT.** The streetlamp's pool
  bright and sudden. The hooded RUNNER clears a stumbling person (background, far) and
  heads down the center of the block toward the viewer, clutching something taken. In the
  foreground Windman is already PIVOTING toward the street, fan swinging BACK in
  preparation. At the far side, Tiebi has STOPPED, has seen the runner, has NOT seen
  Windman — already eyeing the gap between two parked cars, calculating. Both have seen
  the same thing; neither knows the other has. Pressure-overlay can still read faintly.
  The block comes alive.
- **P4 — Sensor and Actuator — TWO ACTIONS AT ONCE, GHOST-IMAGE (micro) + PRESSURE-RINGS.**
  Wide-angle, mid-action. The runner is center-frame, still moving, unaware of either.
  RIGHT side: Windman's fan completes a deliberate lateral sweep — controlled concentric
  pressure-rings (ghost-line register) rolling across the pavement, catching the runner
  from the SIDE (a shove/stagger, NOT a strike); the runner's foot stutters, trajectory
  bends. LEFT side: Tiebi has gone MICRO — a postage-stamp mustard-gold smear sprinting
  flat-out in the gap between two parked cars, beret a moving blur, carrying a full-sized
  start-up's momentum into a tiny frame (ghost-image: desaturated micro-sprint instance).
  Both present in one frame; they have not spoken. The most kinetic panel.
- **P5 — Sprint First, Then Grow — ARRIVAL, GHOST-IMAGE (full), STOMP, WINDMAN APPROACHING.**
  The runner has staggered from the gust and is BRACING (caught himself, not down, lost a
  second). In the gap to his left, AT FULL SIZE, mid-forward-roll: Tiebi arrives exactly
  at the point the stagger opened — the sprint's momentum now the full-mass body's
  momentum (E03 P5's method paid forward). The roll catches the runner's LEGS and brings
  both down; the taken object skids across the pavement. Tiebi ends sitting, slightly
  winded, beret somehow STILL ON. SFX **STOMP—** integrated into the arrival. In the
  background, Windman walks toward the lamp's pool from the shadows, fan FOLDED shut, at a
  pace that says he already knows where the scene is without looking at it.
- **P6 — Two-Body Problem — TWO FIGURES, SAME DIRECTION, OVERHEAD REGISTER (hand-off /
  inversion).** Aftermath. The block quiet again, runner gone (off-panel), taken object
  recovered (off-panel). The streetlamp's pool, and TWO figures at its edge: Windman
  (fan folded in right hand, not aimed) and Tiebi (beret STRAIGHTENED back to its proper
  tilt, brushing grit off his trousers). They stand a half-step apart, both facing the
  SAME direction — outward, down the street, AWAY from where the runner came. Neither
  looks at the other. Overhead-and-slightly-behind register (same family as E03 P6 / E01
  P5). The first series panel ending on two of the four in frame. No pressure-overlay.
  Quiet, dry. Two short flat lines of dialogue + the driest narrator button. The inversion
  of every prior solo exit.

**Hard continuity guards (every panel):** the only heroes are **Windman and Tiebi** — no

cape, no flight. The only non-hero figures in the whole issue are the **hooded runner**
