# S01E05 — Captions

Print-ready narration captions for [[S01E05 — Hold Your Own Weight]] — to letter onto the
comic page beside each picture. **16 captions plus one SFX** across five panels. Voice is
dry case-file narrator, deadpan-strategic: the same register as the series but drier and
more diagnostic, the comedy of a being who looks like a finished superhero and cannot stay
on a floor. This issue is caption-driven — Panel 2 carries five captions (the accumulation
of failures), Panels 3 and 4 each carry four, Panel 1 carries three, Panel 5 carries one.
Panel 3 also carries one quiet SFX. Pairs with the art in [[S01E05 — Panel Prompts]].

## Captions

**Panel 1 — Halfway Out**
> He had been awake for approximately one second.
>
> He was already somewhere he had not chosen to be.
>
> Both of these things were going to remain true for a while.

**Panel 2 — Every Floor Has an Opinion**
> He reached for the ceiling rail.
>
> The ceiling rail was not interested.
>
> He tried the railing.
>
> The railing had the same opinion.
>
> The floor below that was completely consistent in its views.

**Panel 3 — Wrong Dial**
*(SFX, subtle, integrated into the light-bend distortion around his hand: "DRIFT—")*
> He had been trying to stop phasing.
>
> He was not, it turned out, in control of the phasing.
>
> Something else had been on.
>
> Something else had been on the whole time.

**Panel 4 — Turn It On**
> He stopped trying to grab the world.
>
> He turned his own weight back on.
>
> The floor caught him.
>
> It turned out floors do that.

**Panel 5 — PRIMM**
> Primm.

## Lettering notes

- **Panel 2 carries the episode's highest caption density: five captions, staircase rhythm,
  accumulating downward.** Each caption is a new floor and a new refusal; the last one
  tips from slapstick into the building having opinions. Keep all five in the panel — they
  are the beat.
- **Panel 3 SFX: *DRIFT—*** This is the canonical first deployment of Primm's gravity-field
  sound. It is a quiet SFX — not a crash, a hum — integrated into the visible light-bend
  distortion around his hand and forearm, not boxed or labeled. Letter it subtle; it belongs
  to the air, not the action.
- **Panel 3 captions: two matched pairs.** "He had been trying to stop phasing / He was not,
  it turned out, in control of the phasing" — then "Something else had been on / Something
  else had been on the whole time." The double repetition of "Something else had been on"
  is intentional and load-bearing; do not condense or merge.
- **Panel 4 before/after:** the panel is a split image — left half slightly desaturated
  (before, hovering above the floor), right half full color (after, grounded). The four
  captions run across the full panel as a single sequence; they are not split left/right
  per half-image. Place them in a single caption-track at the base or side.
- **Panel 5 caption: smallest and most offhand line in the issue.** Bottom-right, dry-
  corporate register. The name arrives by maintenance artifact (a primer can label); the
  caption observes what is already true. Same treatment as E02's *"Windman."*, E03's
  *"T."*, and E04's *"Pho-boman."* — let it whisper.
- **Non-caption in-panel lettering** (locked in [[S01E05 — Panel Prompts]], not part of
  the narration track): Panel 5 — the dented primer can label reads **PRIMM. UNDERCOAT FOR
  METAL SURFACES. APPLY BEFORE PAINTING.** This is background art text, not a caption box;
  it should be legible but not prominent — easy to miss on first read.
- **Voice continuity:** "approximately one second" in Panel 1 runs the same numeric-deadpan
  formula as E02 ("approximately four minutes"), E03 ("approximately six minutes"), and E04
  ("approximately no minutes"). The formula is the show's signature; keep it intact.
- **Deliberate silence — do not fill Panel 5 beyond the single caption.** He is descending
  the stairwell with perfect composure; a second caption would editorialize. One word is
  enough.
