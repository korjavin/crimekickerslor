# S01E09 — Panel Prompts

Image-generation prompts and episode-specific visual canon for
[[S01E09 — Variance Report]] (Act IV opener — the first voluntary four-body
configuration; the corporation names them and schedules their retrieval while they
read a document). Built by the **panel-prompt-smith** agent.

> Each prompt is **self-contained** — image models have no memory, so every prompt
> restates the art style, every character on screen, and every prop in full. The global
> art style and recurring descriptors live in [[Visual Style Guide]]; reuse the wordings
> below **verbatim** across panels for consistency.

This issue is **one comic page resolved across six images** (P1–P6). It is the most
layout-complex episode in the series: its formal spine is the **parallel-register** —
the four heroes occupy a **warm-palette half** while GastroDefense Inc.'s screens,
documents, drone feeds, and logos occupy a **cold institutional half**, sharing the
same panel without any labeled divider. **Panels 2 and 5 are explicit split-register.**
All four heroes are present from Panel 1 (the series' first voluntary four). The
corporation **never appears as a person** — it is present only as screens, drones,
documents, and logos. The space shifts across the episode: a **night street** (P1), a
**GastroDefense district substation interior** (P2–P5: institutional fluorescent,
cinder-block painted white, a row of wall-mounted terminals, a refrigerated corridor),
and an exterior service **alley** (P6). Tiebi knows this building — he was a
GastroDefense security officer; he has the access code, the routes, and the side door.

### The parallel-register / split-register grammar (episode-specific)

The episode's primary formal device. Every panel is divided — sometimes implicitly,
sometimes as an explicit split — into a **warm physical half** (the heroes, in their
signature colors and in warm pooled or refrigerator-cool naturalistic light) and a
**cold institutional half** (GastroDefense screens, documents, drone feeds, and logos,
in flat cold blue-white/grey fluorescent). **There is no drawn divider line, no border,
no labeled split** — the reader knows which half is which by tone and palette alone.

- **Explicit split-register (P2, P5):** stage the heroes on the **left, warm-palette
  half** and the corporation's screens on the **right, cold-palette half**, the two
  halves abutting with no border between them, distinguished only by palette and content.
- **Implicit register (P1, P3, P4, P6):** the heroes are the warm subject and a
  GastroDefense screen / drone / document sits in the same frame as the cold counterpart,
  facing the reader. The warm and cold tones co-exist in one continuous image.
- **Palette rule:** the warm half carries the only saturated color — Windman's
  green/blue/red fan blades, Tiebi's mustard-gold shirt, Pho-boman's reddish-orange
  broth-glow and red-on-blue belly-dragon, Primm's faint-blue orbs and cyan visor. The
  cold half is flat institutional blue-white / grey / fluorescent, desaturated, no
  warmth. Always add the negative "no warm or saturated color in the institutional /
  screen half — only the heroes carry warm color."

### The corporation as device, never as person (hard guard, every panel)

GastroDefense Inc. is present in every panel and **never appears as a human being**. It
is rendered **only** as: lit terminal screens with legible institutional text, document
pages on screen, drone-operations grid feeds, food-survey drones (machines), and the
corporate logo. **No executives, no staff, no security guards, no faces, no figures, no
silhouettes of people on the corporate side** — the menace is entirely bureaucratic and
machine-mediated. Always negative-guard: "no GastroDefense human / person / executive /
staff / guard anywhere; the corporation appears only as screens, documents, drones, and
logos."

### The house SFX-callout format & the four blank SFX boxes (episode-specific — P4)

The series places **SFX callouts** — bold hand-lettered comic sound-effect words
(HWUF, DRIFT, STOMP, SLURP) — in the visual margins/corners of kinetic panels, in a
consistent house style: bold comic SFX lettering, optionally in a light callout box with
a thin-to-medium border, positioned at the panel's corners/margins.

- **Panel 4 renders FOUR blank SFX boxes**, one in each of the panel's four margins/
  corners, in that exact house callout format — **same border weight and same corner/
  margin position grammar as an action-panel SFX callout — but COMPLETELY EMPTY inside:
  no word, no letter, no glyph, no fill, no placeholder.** Four empty bordered callout
  boxes where the reader has been trained to expect HWUF / DRIFT / STOMP / SLURP. The
  emptiness is the effect: the four heroes stand still and read. No other panel in this
  issue uses SFX boxes.
