# S01E09 — Variance Report

> **Logline:** A GastroDefense logistics ticket, a fleet of food-safety drones, and one parental-leave escalation chain later — the corporation's database has a name for the four of them before any of them do. The four find a terminal and read what was always on file about what they are. The system schedules retrieval. This is within spec.

---

## Title

**"Variance Report"** — the logistics document that triggers the escalation chain when the recall truck fails to arrive. A variance report is a deviation from expected parameters filed as a matter of procedure; it is not an alarm. It simply notes that something did not match the forecast, identifies the gap, and initiates the next step in the workflow. Here it also names the existential condition: W-01 and P-03 are specification outputs, within spec; P-02 and T-04 are non-specification outputs, variance. The corporation does not have a word for the problem. It has a form. The form has a SLA. The title sits in the house family — "Four-Body Problem" / "Specific Heat" / "Momentum Not Included" — without reaching for it.

---

## Continuity / placement

This is **Issue 9, the opening of Act IV ("Antagonist Reveal").** It follows [[S01E08 — Four-Body Problem]] (the full four-way convergence; the truck stopped; the recall notice on the truck's side panel; GastroDefense's fourteen field-survey drones in the district) and feeds forward to [[S01E10]] (the finale — retrieval executed, Primm captured, the un-make dilemma detonated, the name inherited).

**E08 closed** on four figures facing the same direction, truck stopped behind them, the recall notice visible to the reader and unread by any hero, the final caption noting that GastroDefense Inc. operated fourteen field-survey drones in the district. The corporation had not yet noticed its truck did not arrive. It will.

E09 opens on the four as a **loose voluntary four for the first time**: they did not collide into this episode by following separate signals toward the same object. They are choosing, awkwardly, to act together — still no shared language, still no common frame, but the same direction and a problem nobody has articulated. This is a different register from every prior pairing: it is not accidental. It is not comfortable. It is happening anyway.

The **parallel-register structure** is this episode's primary formal signature: heroes' scenes and corporation's administrative process share every page, sometimes in the same panel. The audience watches the net form in real time while the four move through the city unaware. Comedy and dread sit in the same caption staircase.

The **un-make twist** — the reveal that "RETURN TO ORIGIN FACILITY" means re-integration, not detention — lands when the four find a terminal and read a recall specification document. It is delivered in passive voice. There is no consent field. This is where E09 ends: not with a confrontation, not with a capture, but with a GastroDefense system screen updating to: *ANOMALY CORRELATION COMPLETE — 4 UNITS — SCHEDULING RETRIEVAL.*

> **Seam test for any future edit:** does this episode open with all four acting voluntarily together (not an accident), deliver the un-make reveal through reading a corporate document (not through an antagonist speaking), plant the CRIME KICKERS label in GastroDefense institutional type visible to the reader but not named by any hero, and close on the system scheduling retrieval without executing it? If a hero is captured, if GastroDefense speaks, if the name is adopted by the team, or if the un-make twist arrives from an antagonist's mouth, the Act IV opening beat is broken.

## Format

This episode is a **6-panel comic page** (one page; six panels, the full E08 budget, justified by the structural ambition — the parallel-register mechanic requires one panel to establish the corporation's clock, and the un-make reveal requires room to hold). Six panels is the correct budget for an episode whose subject is two stories happening simultaneously, neither aware of the other, arriving at the same destination from opposite directions.

The visual space shifts across the episode: it opens on a **night street** (the same city, the same dark palette as E08's close), moves into a **GastroDefense district substation** (industrial interior, institutional fluorescent, Tiebi's old building), and closes on an **exterior threshold** — the four outside, the GastroDefense screen visible through the window glass behind them, the night around them.

The **parallel-register** technique: where E08 Panel 2 used a split-register for four simultaneous vignettes of heroes doing their own math, here the split-register is used for the two parallel storylines — heroes and corporation — sharing the same panel space without sharing knowledge. Each panel that uses split-register is structured: heroes occupy the physical, warm-palette half; corporation's documents and drone feeds occupy the institutional, cold-palette half. The split is never labeled. The reader knows which is which by tone.

The **caption staircase** carries the corporation's same-night auto-escalation as a running arithmetic: the elapsed-time figure appears in the first corporation-side caption and is updated by each subsequent corporation beat — the clock the audience can read and the heroes cannot.

Captions are the dry case-file narrator throughout. Dialogue is minimal. The corporation never speaks; it only files.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The truck had stopped. The four of them had been standing near each other since.*
- CAPTION (below): *This was new.*
- CAPTION (below, smallest): *Eleven blocks north, a GastroDefense logistics variance ticket had just been opened. Auto-escalation threshold: 45 minutes.*

The audience immediately holds two clocks: the heroes' awkward new configuration, and the 45-minute countdown they cannot see.

Section 4.4's "[Field not applicable]" is the cold heart of the episode delivered as a database field with brackets. It needs no explanation. It needs no commentary.

### Panel 6 — SCHEDULING RETRIEVAL

**Image:** Exterior. Night, the district substation behind the four figures. They have come out through the side door — not the main entrance, a side door Tiebi knew about — and they are standing on the narrow service alley between the substation and the building behind it, facing out toward the street. The geometry is the house grammar: four figures, same direction. ## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- **The CRIME KICKERS label:** GastroDefense's exception-correlation module auto-generated the label "CRIME KICKERS" from the incident log field notes for the E08 truck stop — specifically from the notation "kicking-type force observed (Unit T-04)" — and filed it as the operational designation for the four anomalies in Grid Square 4-C. This label exists in GastroDefense's database. The heroes did not see the screen it was on. They do not know it exists. The corporation has named them.
- **The disposal asymmetry in full:** the heroes have now read Section 4 of the recall specification. They know what "RETURN TO ORIGIN FACILITY" means for all four of them: Windman (W-01) and Primm (P-03) are to be reintegrated — un-made as separate beings, reassembled into the prototype. Pho-boman (P-02) and Tiebi (T-04) are to be disposed of under GD-Standard-7 and GD-Standard-12 respectively. The audience and the heroes share this knowledge from Panel 4 onward. What the heroes do not yet know is that retrieval has been scheduled — the screen update in Panel 6 happens behind them.
- **Pho-boman's invisibility:** the drones' sensor sweep in this episode — including GD-SURVEY-PLAT-02's pass over the substation in Panel 3 — registered four thermal signatures. One of these was a 94°C broth-temperature reading that the exception-reporting module immediately routed to food-safety compliance because it is technically not an asset-recovery anomaly (Pho-boman has no incident-signature). The asset-recovery cross-reference generated by Panel 3's sweep will, when it completes, flag three signatures against W-01 / P-03 / T-04 and return a null for the fourth. The corporation's primary location method cannot find Pho-boman. He found this interesting. He does not yet know he found it.
- **The Retrieval Division ticket:** it has been acknowledged. It is assigned. The Retrieval Division is a department with a budget that has not processed a ticket since Q3 of the previous fiscal year. This is not a warning. It is a workflow resuming. E10 is the workflow executing.
- **GD-SURVEY-DESSERT-04's loop:** it is on iteration 217 and rising. It has been attempting to file a temperature-compliance certificate for a being without a food-establishment registration number for thirty-one minutes and counting. It will continue. The loop is not within spec. The drone does not know this.

## Tone targets

- **Panel 1:** wide and dry, the episode's key reset — four figures, same direction, voluntary; drone in the corner as scenery; the SLA clock planted in the final caption. The humor is in the contrast between the heroes' awkward new configuration and the tiny corporate action happening simultaneously in the corner. Neither is aware of the other. Palette: night, the same dark city as E08, now four colors present but subdued — the team is not in action mode.
- **Panel 2:** the split-register panel that establishes the parallel-register structure. Left: Tiebi's knowledge making the building accessible without announcement. Right: the logistics ticket, the drone feeds, the satisfaction-survey loop, the automated correlation beginning. Comedy in the bureaucratic texture — the escalation field with two struck-through names in the wrong font; the drone reporting Pho-boman to food-safety compliance. Dread in the automated correlation in the smallest institutional type at the bottom of the corporate screen. Neither the heroes nor the captions point at either. The audience holds both.
- **Panel 3:** the interior establishes the substation register — institutional fluorescent, Tiebi navigating from memory, the CRIME KICKERS label on the terminal screen facing away. The comedy is in Tiebi's flat navigation ("The terminals are on the right"), the caption's note about three missed security reviews, and the satisfaction-survey drone outside the window filing escalation on an uncooperative subject. The dread is in the drone's flag in the queue and the label no hero sees.
- **Panel 4:** the quietest panel in the episode. Four figures at a terminal. The four blank SFX boxes make the silence a visual event — the series' kinetic heartbeat running on empty. The document does everything. The caption staircases down to its smallest: "Section 4.4 was one line." The humor is in the document's bureaucratic completeness — the consent field with "[Field not applicable]" is funnier and colder than any dramatic reveal, because the corporation did not forget consent; consent was never a design parameter; the field was never built. The dread is total and is delivered in a corporate font.
- **Panel 5:** the split-register resumes after the still of Panel 4 — heroes moving, corporation escalating. The "within spec / now an emergency" caption pair completes its arc. Drone comedy (iteration 217, the food-safety loop) and institutional comedy (the Retrieval Division's automated welcome note) in the corporate half. The heroes' silence on the left is named and stopped: "They did not discuss what they had read. / The document had been specific." The narrator names the fact; the document already gave the reason.
- **Panel 6:** the close. Four figures, same direction, alley compression. The warm and the cold in the same frame: the four facing the street (forward, alive, in the city), the belly-dragon's reddish glow at eye-line, the screen behind them updating with "SCHEDULING RETRIEVAL" (dread, institutional, unhurried). "Within spec" as the final beat. The drone count is nine. The audience has been counting.

## Differentiation / what's new

The diaspora issues (E02–E05) were each one body learning what it is, alone.

The reunion issues (E06–E07) were each two bodies discovering they are not alone — and not knowing what to do with that.

E08 was the convergence: four bodies, one problem, no coordination, the driest possible statement that it worked.

E09 is the first episode where the team is **active before the problem**: they are together before the episode's central event (the terminal, the document), not assembled by the event. This is the series' first voluntary four-body configuration, and it is as awkward and undeclared as everything else in the series, which is exactly correct.

What is new here:
- **The parallel-register as a full structural device.** E08 used a split-register in one panel for four diagnostic vignettes; E09 uses the split-register in two panels and the whole-episode parallel structure as the episode's spine. The net forms in the corporate half of every frame while the heroes move through the physical half.
- **The ticking clock.** The same-night auto-escalation is the first time the series has a corporate countdown — a clock that the corporation does not dramatize, the audience can read, and the heroes cannot see. The "within spec / now an emergency" arc across two captions is the dry joke version of a ticking-bomb structure.
- **The un-make reveal through reading.** The heroes do not learn what the recall means from an antagonist, from an exposition scene, from a dramatic speech. They find a terminal and they read a document. The document is internally consistent. The corporation is not wrong by its own framework. The reveal is a bureaucratic document in a corporate font, and it is the coldest thing in the series.
- **The consent field.** "[Field not applicable]" is the series' sharpest single line. It arrives in the same panel as the reintegration procedure, in the same document, in the same font. The corporation did not forget consent. Consent was never a parameter. This is different from malice. The document does not understand that it is different from malice. This is the comedy and the horror of GastroDefense Inc.
- **The four blank SFX boxes.** Panel 4 is the only panel in the series where the SFX callout format appears and contains nothing. The boxes are present, in the house format, in the house positions. They are empty. The reader has been trained to expect HWUF / DRIFT / STOMP / SLURP in those positions; the trained expectation hits the emptiness before the reader can articulate why. The silence is not noted by the narrator. The narrator does not need to note it.
- **The CRIME KICKERS plant.** The corporation auto-generates the label from incident-log data — "kicking-type force (Unit T-04)" — and files it as an operational designation. The heroes do not see it. The audience does. The name is in the corporation's database before any hero says it. The inheritance mechanic, reserved for E10, has been seeded.
- **The Retrieval Division.** "This division has not processed a ticket since Q3 of the previous fiscal year. Welcome back." A department resuming function. No alarm. A workflow.
