# S01E01 — Some Assembly Required

> **Logline:** A half-built corporate prototype gets the wrong soup poured
> into its open visor, splits into four people, and walks out the door in
> four different directions without anyone noticing.

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page** (one page, one spread; expand to 6
only if Panel 3 needs its own splash). Each panel is one image with tight
captions / dialogue. Iconography over blocking.

> **Visual production:** image-generation prompts for each panel live in
> [[S01E01 — Panel Prompts]] (built by the panel-prompt-smith agent against the
> [[Visual Style Guide]]).

## Working title

Primary: **"Some Assembly Required"** — the assembly-line gag *and* the
season-long meaning: the *team* is what still needs assembling.

Alternates: "Project AEGIS-4" · "Recall Notice" · "Issue Zero" ·
"The Soup Tasted Funny"

## Cast (this issue)

- **The Prototype** — *Project AEGIS-4.* Headless on the assembly bench in
  Panel 1; whole and alive for one heartbeat in Panel 3; cracks into **two
  fragments** (Windman, Primm) at the moment of fracture. The helmet,
  filling with broth in the same instant, becomes the cradle for a third
  separate being — Pho-boman — who is **born** rather than split.
- **The Lead Engineer.** Tired. Cares about deadlines, not the unit.
- **The Junior Engineer.** Cares about the unit. The only human who sees the
  split (recurring; see [[Open Questions & Contradictions]]).
- **The Executive.** One scene, one decision, wears the GastroDefense lapel
  pin Tiebi later rides out of the building.
- **The Lunch Lady.** GastroDefense food-safety division, wrong floor, right
  ladle. Says one word the whole issue (recurring legend; see
  [[Open Questions & Contradictions]]).

## Panels

### Panel 1 — Quarterly Targets

**Image:** Wide-angle of a sterile lab on the 17th floor of GastroDefense
Inc. On the workbench: a headless humanoid chassis with a multicolored hand
fan zip-tied to its right wrist, twin katanas crossed on its back. A small
**red dragon decal** is visible on the flat torso plate. Beside the chassis,
separately, sits a **massive diving helmet with a transparent visor** — empty,
dark, waiting. A bored Executive jabs a finger at a clipboard; the Lead
Engineer nods; the Junior Engineer hugs the helmet protectively. Poster on
the wall: *"Hot meals, cold justice."*

**Caption / dialogue:**
- EXEC: *"Friday demo. Skip harmonization."*
- LEAD: *"…all of it?"*
- EXEC: *"All of it."*

**Why it lands:** Establishes GastroDefense, the power-systems already
bolted on, and *why* the prototype will shatter. The unattached helmet is
the cocked gun for Panel 2. The chassis carries only two faculty weapons:
Windman's fan and Primm's katanas. Tiebi is a human security officer
elsewhere in the building — not a faculty, not hardware on this bench.

---

### Panel 2 — Wrong Floor, Right Soup

**Image:** Lunch hour. The lab is empty except for the Junior, mid-whisper
to the chassis. Behind him, oblivious, the **Lunch Lady** wheels her cart
through the door — wrong floor, right ladle. A wheel catches a cable and
her steaming cauldron of pho tips. A glistening arc of reddish-orange
broth, noodles trailing like comet-tails, sails through the air directly
toward the **open transparent visor** of the diving helmet on the bench. A
green sprig of cilantro spins mid-air. Time freezes.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- JUNIOR (whisper): *"You're gonna do gre—"*
- SFX: *SLOOOSH—*

**Why it lands:** The iconic image of the show — broth into visor. This is
the panel that ends up on the trade-paperback cover. The Lunch Lady is
wordless and unfazed.

---

### Panel 3 — Assembly Complete (Then Not)

> *Splash panel. Expand to a full page if budget allows — this is the only
> image in the entire series that shows the cyborg whole.*

**Image:** The helmet, now full of glowing broth, slams itself onto the
chassis. For one impossible instant the prototype is **whole and alive:**
the torso plate **inflates outward** as broth pressurizes the body, warping
the red dragon decal into the canonical round-bellied dragon emblem; the
fan snaps open in the right hand; the katanas unsheathe themselves on the
back; blue light bends around the body. **Then two things happen at once.**
Two white seams of light crack down the chassis — vertical down the torso,
horizontal across the shoulders — and the **two cyborg faculties** separate
into distinct figures (Windman, Primm). *Simultaneously*, the broth-filled
helmet **detaches and glows** — noodles braiding into eyes and cheekbones
behind the visor as a **third, separate being is born** from the soup inside
(Pho-boman). Three figures where there was one — plus Tiebi, transformed
elsewhere in the building. Steam everywhere. The Junior dives behind a cart.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- SFX: *KRA-SLURP-KSSHHH*
- JUNIOR: *"oh no oh no oh no—"*

**Why it lands:** The image carries the show's full origin in one panel.
The belly is *created* by the soup-fill; Pho-boman's canonical spoon-shape
is born here, in front of us — and crucially, he is born *separately* from
the cyborg, not split off from it. Also the only panel where the audience
ever sees AEGIS-4 whole. One heartbeat, gone.

---

### Panel 4 — Four Ways Out

**Image:** A four-quadrant grid (one panel, four windows). Each quadrant
is a tight close-up of one of the four waking, unnamed. For Windman and
Primm, equipment-defining features **manifest with the wake-up** — not
bolted on, they *crystallize* from the split (Pho-boman's helmet is
pre-built; Tiebi's beret is his own, already worn):

- **Top-left — a man on a rooftop three blocks away.** Eyes already wrapped
  in a **blue-and-red bandage** that looks half-finished, as if it formed
  mid-flight. A flag on the next building snaps to attention. He breathes
  in.
- **Top-right — inside a steaming cauldron on the Lunch Lady's rolling
  cart.** A small round helmet bobs in broth, two noodles curling into
  startled eyes behind the visor, a green sprig of cilantro surfacing as a
  nose. The Lunch Lady's hand enters frame with a ladle.
- **Bottom-left — a purple-coated figure halfway through a corridor
  wall.** Gasping, **phasing free.** The suit is fully formed, cyan visor
  glowing, twin blades sheathed on his back, mini-orbs already orbiting
  his waist.
- **Bottom-right — a postage-stamp-sized man clinging to the Executive's
  lapel pin in the elevator.** A tiny **dark-navy beret** perched perfectly on
  his head — his personal beret, worn in memory of his military service, still
  on his head as it was before the incident. The Executive's hand fills most
  of the frame, oblivious. The man stares at his own tiny hand.
  *(The audience clocks it: GastroDefense already carries one of them out
  of the building.)*
  *(He knows exactly who he is — ex-soldier, security officer — but has no
  framework yet for what he has become: a postage-stamp-sized man on a lapel
  pin. The disorientation is about what happened to him, not about who he is.)*

**Caption / dialogue:**
- LUNCH LADY (top-right): *"Settle."*
- *(no other dialogue)*

**Why it lands:** Delivers the four canon silhouettes in a single image.
The **iconic equipment manifests at split-time** as a lucky/random
crystallization for Windman (eye bandage) and Primm (suit + visor) — not
engineered by GastroDefense. Tiebi's beret is the exception: a personal
military memento he was wearing as a human before the incident. Each of the
four windows captures a different register: Windman and Primm are brand new
(creations of the fracture, no past, no self); Pho-boman is brand new
(born of the broth, minutes old); Tiebi knows exactly who he is — a soldier,
a security officer — but has no framework yet for what he has become.

The Lunch Lady's single word lands here.

---

### Panel 5 — None of Them Look Back

**Image:** High overhead shot. The GastroDefense Inc. campus at center,
four streets radiating outward like compass spokes. A tiny figure walks
down each street — the windward man on one, the rolling cart on another,
the purple coat descending a stairwell to the street, the small man crossing
a parking garage. None of them face the building. **None of them face each
other.** Small inset circle, lower-right: the empty assembly bench. A single
pho noodle sits on it, gently steaming, beside the poster *"Hot meals, cold
justice."*

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION (boxed, dry, corporate-cold): *Project AEGIS-4 — Status: Recalled.*

**Why it lands:** The dramatic-irony engine for the season. They were one,
they are now four, and the corporation already considers the unit "Recalled"
— which the audience reads as a promise. The four streets foreshadow the
diaspora arc (Episodes 2–5, one per direction).

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- Two of them (Windman, Primm) came from one being; Pho-boman was born of the
  same accident; Tiebi was a human transformed by it.
- Windman and Primm were made by GastroDefense Inc. Pho-boman was made possible
  by it. Tiebi was employed by it.
- GastroDefense considers the units "Recalled" — they will be coming.
- They are walking *away* from each other.

## What the heroes don't yet have

- **Names** — earned in the diaspora issues.
- **Knowledge of their powers** — discovered the hard way, one per issue.
- **The team identity** — *Crime Kickers* is **not** introduced this issue.
  Saved for the season finale.
- **A past at all** (Windman, Primm, Pho-boman) — they are creations of the
  fracture/incident, minutes old, no human history, just bodies they didn't
  ask for. (Tiebi has a human past; what he lacks is a framework for what
  the incident made him.)
- **A sense of what they've become** (Tiebi) — he has a human past, knows who
  he was, but has no framework yet for what the incident did to him.

## Tone targets

- Panels 1–2: dry corporate comedy. Beige fluorescent palette.
- Panel 3: cosmic-physical comedy. Steam, light, color saturation up.
- Panel 4: quiet, almost lonely. Four small portraits in a grid.
- Panel 5: melancholic and ironic. Wide and cold. The show's heart shows
  for the first time.
