# S01E04 — Panel Prompts

Image-generation prompts and episode-specific visual canon for
[[S01E04 — What Is a Ladle For]] (Pho-boman's origin). Built by the
**panel-prompt-smith** agent.

> Each prompt is **self-contained** — image models have no memory, so every prompt
> restates the art style, the character, and the props in full. The global art style
> and recurring descriptors live in [[Visual Style Guide]]; reuse the wordings below
> **verbatim** across panels for consistency.

This issue is **one comic page resolved across five images** (P1–P5). It is
**almost stationary** — a single GastroDefense service corridor and a loading bay.
**Pho-boman is alone** with the **Lunch Lady** the entire page — no other hero
appears. The Lunch Lady is **wordless throughout** (her one Season-1 word, "Settle,"
was spent in E01 Panel 4). The seam: **P1 continues**
[[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 4's top-right quadrant (the cauldron
wake-up), and **P5 hands off into** [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 5's
overhead compass-spoke shot (the cart in motion toward one of the four streets).

## Episode visual canon

### Pho-boman — appearance (verbatim from [[Pho-boman]] Appearance (detailed))

[[Pho-boman]]'s character page is the single source of truth for his look. He is
the **only hero** in the issue. Reuse this descriptor block **verbatim** in every
panel — scaled to how much of him the camera reveals (see the reveal timeline below):

> a small round spoon-shaped being, the smallest hero at roughly 140–150 cm tall,
> with a large round belly, short legs nearly hidden under the belly's curve, and
> short but noticeably muscular arms; his head is shaped like a deep-sea diving
> helmet — a wide metal dome with a wide transparent front visor; the helmet is
> filled with reddish-orange pho broth (with bright-yellow highlights, a warm glow)
> that visibly sloshes and bubbles; his face forms inside the helmet out of the
> soup's ingredients — eyes, eyebrows and cheekbones made of slightly twisted
> noodles, and a nose made of a bright-green sprig of cilantro poking above the
> broth; he has NO separate mouth — the open visor simply shows the soup, and the
> visor IS the face; he wears short black pants (mostly hidden under the belly) and
> fully black, flat, minimalist boots

- **Belly emblem (verbatim, reveal-gated):** *a large red Chinese dragon on a blue
  background, scales and claws detailed, tail wrapping around the belly.* This is
  **withheld in P1–P3** and **fully revealed in P4** (see reveal timeline). Reuse
  this exact wording in P4 and P5.
- **The visor IS the face / mouth.** He has no mouth. In P3 the boiling-broth jet
  fires from the **open visor**, not from any separate mouth. Guard against drawing
  a mouth in every panel.

### GastroDefense service corridor / interior & palette (verbatim from [[Visual Style Guide]])

The corporate antagonist's R&D campus, here on a service corridor rather than the lab
floor. Reuse for the corridor surfaces in this issue:

> a GastroDefense Inc. service corridor — beige institutional walls, grey linoleum
> floor, a strip of fluorescent ceiling light overhead casting flat even beige-tinted
> light, clean white-and-grey panels, cable runs and equipment along one wall

- **Palette (dry-corporate-comedy tone):** beige fluorescent — desaturated, washed-out,
  institutional beige-grey-white, cold flat overhead fluorescent light.
- **The "one warm pop" rule (P1–P4):** the reddish-orange pho broth is the ONLY
  warm/saturated element against the beige-grey corridor. P4 adds a **second** warm
  element — the red-dragon belly emblem at full saturation. Always add the negative

  warm-toned."

### The SLURP jet (Pho-boman's SFX, P3 only)

His canonical SFX from [[Pho-boman]] is **SLURP**. P3 is its first deployment — an
