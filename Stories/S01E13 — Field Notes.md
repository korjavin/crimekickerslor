# S01E13 — Field Notes

> **Logline:** The only human who watched them be born walks up on a street, hands over a notebook, and tells them the number four was always a count.

> **Stage:** Broad-strokes outline. Synopsis, continuity, and threads are locked to the [[Series Arc]] (Movement II). Panel breakdown, captions, and image prompts are a later production pass (panel-prompt-smith).

## Continuity / placement

**Movement II.** Follows [[S01E12 — Thermal Gradient]] (Primm located; 72-hour clock). Precedes [[S01E14 — Dead Reckoning]] (the rescue). The clock is under 48 hours by episode's end.

## Synopsis

The **Junior Engineer** returns — the one channel the drone net cannot track, because he has a human face and simply walks up to Tiebi on a street, outside HQ. He has been running his own investigation since the fracture (his own post-incident research, not corporate access — author-ratified): salvaged equipment, archive digging, a physical lab notebook annotated in pencil.

The notebook delivers the lineage: **AEGIS-1, AEGIS-2, AEGIS-3 each existed, each fractured, each scattered.** The "4" in Project AEGIS-4 was always a count, not a model name. Windman and Primm were not the first two fragments to walk out of a GastroDefense building. The notebook also logs an **older signal still active** at an address in the city — a different, prior signature (seed for E16). The Junior Engineer says he will keep watching from outside, and leaves; the net does not register him.

## Thread advanced

- **Twist 2 seed:** prior AEGIS iterations are real. The four are the latest, not the only.
- The Junior Engineer is established as the team's "person who knows" — recurring human ally and POV witness.

## Twist / suspense

- They are not the first. Disposal and reintegration have happened before, to others — the dread that will fully land in E16.
- The still-active old signal sits unaddressed: no time for it now, and it is not at the Origin Facility. (Beyond-20 seed planted here, decoded in E16, never resolved.)

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- The "older signal" is an AEGIS-3 primary fragment still warm in a disused facility — the reintegrated prototype's residual. The heroes will reach it in E16; the audience can already feel the implication that reintegration does not cleanly erase a signal.

## Differentiation / what's new

The first time the human world re-enters the story on the heroes' side. Until now every human (the lobby crowd, the maintenance worker, the Lunch Lady) was indifferent or oblivious. The Junior Engineer is the first human who *chose* them — delivered with no sentiment, as a man handing over a notebook because he cares about the thing he built.

## Open for drafting

- How much of the lineage the notebook spells out vs. implies is a drafting choice; keep the still-active signal under-explained.
