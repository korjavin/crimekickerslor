# S01E20 — Return to Origin Facility

> **Logline:** On the ninth day the two halves of one body do the thing the specification has no field for — and the cafeteria says one word about it.

> **Stage:** Broad-strokes outline. Synopsis, continuity, and threads are locked to the [[Series Arc]] (Movement IV close; the open turn). Panel breakdown, captions, and image prompts are a later production pass (panel-prompt-smith).

## Continuity / placement

**Movement IV close; the season's open turn (not a resolution).** Follows [[S01E19 — Consent Field]] (ten-day clock). The end of the twenty-episode movement; the un-make moral question is brought to maximum pressure and deliberately left unresolved.

## Synopsis

On day nine, Windman and Primm choose a **third option** — not un-make, not separation, not compliance: a deliberate, consensual use of the resonance field that has run since [[S01E15 — Partial Contact]], in a way the specification has no template for. The act requires both fragments in the same space, choosing to *engage* the resonance rather than merely carry it. What it produces is neither reintegration nor separation — it is a state the AEGIS-4 specification has no category for, because its consent field was deleted and Appendix C was never written.

**It costs something real, on two registers** (author-ratified): **structural** — the resonance field is permanently altered — and **personal** — the act costs one of the four something specific (left to the E20 drafter; reserved). AEGIS-5 logs `ANOMALOUS STATE — SPEC MISMATCH` and escalates to a target the series has not revealed. The injunction is now contested by a procedural anomaly no court has ruled on; the ten-day clock has not expired; nothing is resolved.

In the Origin Facility cafeteria — GastroDefense catering, present because it is a Tuesday (author-ratified staging) — the **Lunch Lady** observes the result, lowers her ladle toward it the way she lowered it toward Pho-boman's helmet in E01, and says her second word of the season: *"Sufficient."*

The four step out onto the same kind of street where E08 showed them stop a truck. Four figures, same direction. Behind them, a GastroDefense database field reads: *PROJECT AEGIS-4 — STATUS: [FIELD PROCESSING].* The field has been processing for eleven seconds. The season ends with it still processing.

## Thread advanced

- The third option creates a new procedural situation the series cannot yet resolve — the heroes' strongest act and the most open ending.
- AEGIS-5's escalation opens a beyond-20 vector.

## Twist / suspense

- **Twist 5 — the third option is not in the specification.** The act has no template; AEGIS-5 cannot file it.
- The close is a **turn, not a resolution**: the un-make question is open, the resonance is changed but not closed, the four are still four, the system is still processing.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- The third option changes the resonance *state*; it does **not** change the underlying mechanic — reintegrating W and P would still un-make all four. The dilemma survives the act. This is a deferral with cost, not an escape.
- The series does not evaluate whether the cost was right. That is the final moral question, reserved beyond the twenty.

## Reserved / beyond-20 seeds

- Whether the third option forecloses or merely defers reintegration (system still processing).
- The personal cost — which member, and what — reserved for the drafter and the series' future.
- AEGIS-5's escalation target; the law-enforcement client's enforcement mechanism beyond the injunction; the "still-warm" AEGIS-3 signal; the contents of the never-written Appendix C.

## Differentiation / what's new

The finale-that-isn't: a season-scale turn that withholds its own resolution. The series' grammar (four figures, same direction, facing outward) returns from E08 and E09 — but now it points at an unanswered question rather than a recovered calm. The Lunch Lady's single word closes the movement the way "Settle" opened it: minimal intrusion, maximum weight.
