# S01E13 — Field Notes

> **Logline:** The only human who watched them be born walks up on a street, hands over a notebook, and tells them the number four was always a count.

---

## Continuity / placement

**Movement II.** Follows [[S01E12 — Thermal Gradient]] (Pho-boman confirmed Primm's location; 72-hour transfer clock set). Precedes [[S01E14 — Dead Reckoning]] (the rescue). By this episode's close the clock is under 48 hours.

**E12 closed** on Pho-boman walking back out of GastroDefense HQ with intelligence and a deadline — the rescue now has a target (sublevel 3) and a hard window. The three free members know where Primm is; they do not yet know what they are.

E13 arrives between the intelligence-gathering and the rescue: a pause, low-kinetic, outside the building, outside HQ surveillance range, where something the heroes could not have found by infiltrating any terminal finds them instead. The Junior Engineer does not wait to be looked for. He walks up.

E14 opens immediately from this episode's close — the clock is running, the notebook is in hand, and the rescue begins.

> **Seam test for any future edit:** does E13 open with three heroes (Windman, Tiebi, Pho-boman) in a city location outside GastroDefense HQ drone coverage, with Primm still held and the 72-hour clock already running, and does it close with the four unit codes confirmed as a lineage count rather than a model designation, the two red plants (GD-Standard-7 entry; tab-7 marker) planted but undecoded, and the Junior Engineer departing without ceremony? If Primm appears free, if the drone net registers the Junior Engineer, if either red plant is explained or decoded on-page, or if the clock does not close to under 48 hours, the E13–E14 seam is broken.

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page** (one page; five panels is the correct budget — this is a handoff issue, not an action issue; it earns its stillness). The kinetic register of E12 is behind it; the ensemble action of E14 is ahead. E13 is the quiet moment in between: one human, one notebook, five panels.

The visual space is a **city street, midday, exterior** — not institutional, not underground, not a GastroDefense facility. This is the first episode since the diaspora issues to be set entirely in ordinary urban space with no corporate building in frame. Daylight. Foot traffic in the background, distant. The heroes are standing still for once, in public, not hiding — because the Junior Engineer walked up to Tiebi on the street and the most neutral response was to stand still and listen.

The **notebook** is the episode's primary object, as the truck was E08's primary object and the recall specification document was E09's primary object. It is physical, pencil-annotated, held in two hands, handed across. The handoff is the issue's single kinetic moment. It is visibly a worked-over human object — eighteen months of revision visible in the chart layers, erased ghost-branches, patched dates, pages drawn on the backs of discarded GastroDefense schematics. The antidote to four episodes of cold corporate fonts and screens.

The **crosswalk countdown timer** in the background across all five panels is the episode's quiet pulse — a mundane seconds-counter that runs alongside the 48-hour rescue clock without being named as a parallel. When it reaches zero in P5 and WALK appears, the encounter ends. The audience holds the rhyme (seconds and hours; both clocks ticking) without the episode pointing at it.

Captions are the dry case-file narrator throughout. Dialogue is minimal — two lines across the whole issue: one to the Junior Engineer ("The 'four' was always a count"), one to Tiebi ("He'll keep watching"). The others say nothing on-page. Panel 4 has no captions — the chart and the three bodies carry it entirely.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- **The older signal is not background noise.** The red-pencil tab-7 marker at the notebook's page-edge — barely visible in Panel 5 — is an address: a disused former GastroDefense facility in the city where something the Junior Engineer's instruments registered but could not identify is still active. He logged the location and the reading and wrote beside it, in his own handwriting, that he does not yet know what it means. The heroes do not know either. When they follow the address — in E16 — the archive they find still running in that building will tell them two things at once: that disposal has been executed before, under GD-Standard-7, and that the truck cargo from E08 was the reagent for their own reintegration. The audience cannot yet hold all of this — but the signal exists, and the notebook contains its address. Neither plant is decoded on-page in E13; neither hero understands either.
- **AEGIS-3's reintegration was completed.** The chart's AEGIS-3 column, visible in Panel 4, shows "reintegration — completed" and a red GD-Standard-7 notation beside a struck-through entry. The heroes see this but have no context for GD-Standard-7 beyond what E09's recall specification named: it covers "disposal per Product Liability / Unscheduled Outputs." Pho-boman sees the red line cross his own reflection without reading it as a precedent for his situation — he is looking at a struck entry near his own code, not comprehending what it preceded. The connection is confirmed in E16, from the building's archive.
- **The "PEOPLE WHO WALKED OUT" rewrite is the Junior Engineer's allegiance declaration.** The heroes can read it; the audience can read it; the Junior Engineer wrote it. No hero comments on it on-page. It is in the chart.
- **The drone net has no entry for the Junior Engineer.** GD-Standard-15 covers asset-recovery escalation thresholds for anomalous signatures. A human face walking an unmarked block does not meet the flag criteria. This is not a gap in the net — it is the net working as designed. The net was built to find AEGIS-4 unit signatures. The Junior Engineer is not one.
- **The clock.** The heroes know the clock is under 48 hours. The audience has held the 72-hour countdown since E12. This is the last pause before the rescue.

## Differentiation / what's new

Every prior encounter with information in this arc has been mediated by a corporate system: a terminal, a document, a drone feed, a status update. The heroes have read their situation off screens and specifications, always in the corporation's language, always in passive voice, always in a font.

This is the first time information arrives in pencil, by hand, from a human who chose to bring it.

The Junior Engineer is the first human in the series who *acts on their behalf* — not a bystander (the lobby crowd, the maintenance worker), not a force of nature (the Lunch Lady), not an institutional system. A person who looked at what happened, spent eighteen months cross-referencing it, and walked across a city block on a Tuesday to hand the result to the one hero with a recognizable face. The series has been building toward a "person who knows" since E01 Panel 2 (the Junior Engineer at the bench, mid-whisper to the headless chassis). This is where that character completes its first arc.

What is new here:
- **The first human ally on the heroes' side.** The distinction from every other human in the series — indifferent, oblivious, or institutional — is delivered by his presence, not announced by the narrator.
