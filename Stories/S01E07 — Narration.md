# S01E07 — Narration (Voiceover)

Spoken-word narration voiceover for [[S01E07 — Specific Heat]], written for ElevenLabs
text-to-speech to play as background narration over a slideshow / motion-comic of the
5 panels (see [[S01E07 — Panel Prompts]]) and the print [[S01E07 — Captions]].
Written for the ear. Dry case-file narrator voice — same family as [[S01E01 — Narration]]
through [[S01E06 — Narration]], now running two simultaneous bureaucratic clocks in one
track: Pho-boman's broth thickening and Primm's surface inventory, interleaved without
either addressing the other. No direct dialogue between characters in this issue — it is
narrated in near-silence throughout. The DRIFT and SLURP SFX are in-panel lettering.
~215 words, ~86 seconds at 150 wpm.

## Clean script (paste into ElevenLabs)

> Everything in this block is spoken narrator text only — no labels, nothing that
> shouldn't be voiced. Paste as-is. No character dialogue exists in this issue.
> The DRIFT— and SLURP— SFX are lettered in-panel and are not voiced here.

One of them was following a smell.

One of them was following a mass displacement anomaly in the refrigeration unit of a GastroDefense Inc. logistics truck.

The brick had been there for approximately four hours. It was about to have a different opinion.

The broth had opinions about minus eighteen degrees Celsius. The opinions were accumulating. He tried the wall. The rack was twelve centimeters of steel. He tried the floor. The floor had a steel subframe.

He tried phasing. He tried the wall behind it. Also steel. He tried the floor again. Concrete over a steel subframe. He tried a blink. Every vector passed through steel. Metal took the field, the phase, and the blink. One rule, not three. He filed this under: a problem. The broth was beginning to queue in a way that concerned no one but the broth.

The broth went weightless. The face went away for a moment. He looked at it. It felt like a word in a language he had not yet learned.

The gap between the racks was a meter and a half. He ran it. The door opened. That was the whole plan. Outside, the broth settled. The face came back. Neither of them said anything about it.

## Runtime

- **Word count:** ~215 words.
- **Runtime:** ~86 s at 150 wpm; ~92 s at a more deliberate 140 wpm. With the longer
  pauses in Panel 3 (the inventory staircase) and the deliberate silence after Panel 4's
  emotional beat, it plays nearer **~100–105 s** in practice — the longest episode
  narration yet, which is correct: this is the quietest issue, and the silence is the texture.
- "He filed this under: a problem." and "Neither of them said anything about it." are the
  two landing lines — both earn their pauses.

## ElevenLabs delivery notes

- **Voice character:** the same dry, mid-to-low documentary narrator as [[S01E01 — Narration]]
  through [[S01E06 — Narration]] — maintain the same voice for series continuity. Flat
  affect, unhurried, low register. This issue wants the narrator even stiller than usual:
  the freezer is the silencing environment; the narrator matches it.
- **No character dialogue in this issue.** E07 is the series' near-wordless counterpart
  to E06's first dialogue. Neither Primm nor Pho-boman speaks. All text is narration.
- **DRIFT— and SLURP— SFX are in-panel lettering, not voiced.** The track does not
  include either SFX. Note the SLURP in Panel 5 is the episode's warmest and loudest
  beat in print — on the voiceover track, the narrator's warming shift on "Outside, the
  broth settled" carries that emotional register instead.
- **FIELD READ caption (Panel 2) is in-panel lettering, not narrated.** It has a
  distinct machine-register typographic treatment in print. On the audio track, the
  narrator reads the captions on either side of it and pauses where the FIELD READ sits
  — the pause carries the weight of the absurdity without voicing the readout.
- **The staircase panels (2 and 3):** two interleaved clocks (broth-thickening,
  surface-inventory) running without addressing each other. Read them as a flat
  diagnostic list — one observation at a time, no escalation in the voice, even when
  the situation is escalating. The comedy is in the register staying level. The Panel 3
  staircase in particular ("He tried phasing… Also steel… He tried the floor again…")
  should land like a checklist being worked through methodically.
- **"He filed this under: a problem."** is flat, not ironic in delivery. Same register
  as the inventory lines above it — not an announcement, a notation.
- **Panel 4 is the still centre of the episode.** "The broth went weightless. The face
  went away for a moment. He looked at it. It felt like a word in a language he had not
  yet learned." — read each line with genuine space after it. The 1.8 s break after the
  final line of Panel 4 is the longest silence in the track. Let it be real silence.
- **"That was the whole plan."** is the humor payoff of the episode. Flat delivery,
  no lift. The compression is the joke. The pause after it is the only beat of triumph
  available before the button.
- **"Neither of them said anything about it."** is the quietest possible closing note.
  Same principle as E06's "Neither of them said the word 'team.'" — name the absence,
  not the presence. Softest register available. Let it close without announcement.
- **Settings:** expressiveness ~25–35 %, stability ~75 %. Even stiller than earlier
  episodes — the flatness is higher because the stage is colder and quieter. Match the
  voice used for E01–E06; this narrator does not change.
- **Voice archetypes:** "George," "Harry," "Arnold," or whichever dry documentary /
  low-affect voice was chosen for E01–E06. Avoid enthusiastic / warm / heroic / young.
