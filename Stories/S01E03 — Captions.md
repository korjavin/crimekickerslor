# S01E03 — Captions

Print-ready narration captions for [[S01E03 — Momentum Not Included]] — to letter onto the
comic page beside each picture. Unlike [[S01E02 — Captions]] (deliberately spare, lots of
silence), **E03 is caption-DRIVEN**: the dry "case-file" **staircase captions** are the
comedic engine, so the issue runs caption-rich by design. There is **no SFX and no dialogue**
anywhere in the issue — every line on the page is a narration caption in the same deadpan
register established in S01E01/S01E02. Canon-accurate to the 6-panel script. Pairs with
[[S01E03 — Panel Prompts]].

## Lettering notes

- **Everything on the page is a caption.** E03 has no SFX and no dialogue bubbles — the dry
  case-file narrator voice carries the whole issue. (Contrast: E01 had SFX + dialogue; E02
  had one SFX and one offscreen voice.)
- **The staircase is the joke — letter the beats as separate stacked boxes**, not run
  together. Panels 2, 3, and 5 each land a short caption, beat, short caption, beat. If a
  letterer merges them into one block the comic timing dies. The rhythm IS the comedy (and,
  in Panel 5, the triumph).
- **Two staircases rhyme on purpose:** Panel 3 ("He fell, and on the way down, he got big. /
  This was the right idea. / He did not move. / This was the problem.") is the comedy version;
  Panel 5 ("Sprint first.
  Then grow. / In that order.") is the triumphant version of the same four-beat rhythm. Keep
  their lettering identical in weight and spacing so the echo reads.
- **Panel 2's fourth caption is the bridge:** "There was a way out. It was down." is the last
  beat of Panel 2 — dry, and it sets up the fall: its "down" echoes straight into Panel 3's
  "He fell, and on the way down…". Letter it as the quiet button on the closet beat, then let
  Panel 3 pay it off.
- **"T." (Panel 6)** is the smallest, most offhand caption in the issue — the name beat,
  bottom-right, quiet. Same family as E02's "Windman.": observed, not announced. (It is a
  parking-stall letter that happens to be his name; don't punch it up.)
- **Caption count is high by design.** This is not the E01/E02 "5–10 sentence" narration cap
  — E03's captions are the script's primary text and its comedic spine, so the issue is
  intentionally caption-rich. Each box is short; the density is the point.
- **Register:** dry, corporate, deadpan, present/past-tense "case-file" voice — identical to
  the narrator family of E01/E02. No exclamation, no hero-speak.
