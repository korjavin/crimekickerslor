# S01E15 — Partial Contact

> **Logline:** Two days after the rescue, the two halves of one body realize the thing between them never went back to sleep.

---

## Continuity / placement

**Movement III opener.** Follows [[S01E14 — Dead Reckoning]] (rescue succeeded; the four are together again; the reintegration chamber's ambient prep field contacted both fragment signatures during the extraction — Primm's in person, Windman's via the air-pulse routed through the ductwork — unnoticed). Precedes [[S01E16 — Prior Art]] (the four follow the old signal to the disused facility; that episode's double decode requires the resonance to already be active before they walk in). The un-make question moves from document (E09) to lived sensation here.

**E14 closed** on the four together again — the rescue beat, the success, the hidden cost. Pho-boman and Primm had to pass through the staging chamber on sublevel 2; its preliminary alignment field was running ambient. The protocol escalated to a `[REDACTED]` recipient. Neither of those facts is visible to the heroes yet. What is visible — two days later — is that something in the air between the two fragments has changed.

**E16 opens** on the four arriving at the disused GastroDefense processing facility — the "still-warm" AEGIS-3 signal from the Junior Engineer's notebook. The resonance must already be active and carrying before the double decode of that episode lands with its full weight.

> **Seam test for any future edit:** does this episode open exactly two days after the rescue (not later, not the same day), involve no new antagonist action or corporate escalation on-page (that comes in E17), and close on the resonance still running — active, constant, unresolved — with the heroes facing outward toward E16 rather than inward toward a decision? If the resonance is named or resolved, if the corporate net appears as a subject rather than ambient background, or if any hero articulates the un-make question as a choice, the Movement III opener is broken.

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page** — one page, one image per panel, tight captions, minimal dialogue. Five panels is the correct budget for a cost episode: the kinetic pressure of E14 has released; this is the still air after a rescue, and the stillness is the subject. An extra panel would push toward explanation; five panels lets the resonance carry weight without being diagnosed. The sixth panel (the ensemble action close) is deliberately absent — this episode does not end on a convergence, it ends on a separation that is also a signal.

The visual space shifts twice across the episode: it opens on a **day-lit street** (the city two days later, unremarkable, the heroes moving through ordinary space), moves into a **nondescript interior** — a room of no consequence, possibly above a shopfront, the kind of space four people who are not yet sure what they are to each other occupy by default — and closes on **two separate thresholds**: Windman and Primm each at their own door, the resonance between them measurable in the frame even without either figure present in the other's panel.

The split-register structure used in E09 and E14 is not deployed here — this episode earns its silence by having only one story running, not two. No corporate screen. No drone in a corner. The net is forming offscreen. This episode does not show it. That discipline is the episode's form.

Captions are the dry case-file narrator throughout — boxed, deadpan, observational, the house register. Dialogue is minimal: two exchanges, each under ten words. The narrator does the diagnostic work. The heroes are not yet sure what to diagnose.

> **Visual production:** image-generation prompts for each panel to be built against the [[Visual Style Guide]] by the panel-prompt-smith agent.

## Panels

### Panel 1 — Two Days Later

**Image:** Day. A street-level wide shot, ordinary city, no emergency. The four are together but not in formation — the geometry of people who have been spending time near each other without having arranged it. Windman is slightly ahead, fan folded at his side, bandaged face tipped at the angle of someone reading ambient pressure. Tiebi is to his left at half-a-step's distance, beret straight, mustard-gold shirt catching the flat daylight. Pho-boman is slightly behind, broth resettled and warm, noodle-brows in the attentive configuration. Primm is to the right, one step behind, mini-orbs in deliberate orbit — not the tight orbit of "I have the answer," not the motionless stillness of Panel 4 in E09; the cautious, slightly-wider orbit of someone tracking something just outside the edge of their field.

The city is ordinary. The city does not know what happened two days ago in a building with a sublevel 2. No drones visible in this panel. No corporate signage. Just a street, and four people on it.

What is visible only in the ghost-line / field-distortion registers that the series has used since E06: Windman's pressure ghost-lines are picking up three distinct signatures. The one to his left — Tiebi — reads in the familiar irregular-mass bracket. The one nearest him on his right — Primm — reads as the field-register always has, occupying the same gravitational coordinate Windman has no category for. But there is something different about how it reads now. It is not faint. It is not intermittent. It reads at low but constant amplitude — the way a signal reads when it is not spiking but also not off. Windman has not said this aloud. He does not have the word for it. He has been tracking it for forty-three hours.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *Two days after the rescue, the city had returned to its previous behavior.*
- CAPTION (below): *Three of the four had also returned to their previous behavior.*
- CAPTION (below): *Windman's field-read had not.*

**Why it lands:** The episode's key register is established in the opening captions: something has changed in the field-read, and the narrator names this in the driest possible formulation while declining to explain it. "The city had returned to its previous behavior" is the series' case-file narrator at its best — noting the return to normalcy as a fact, immediately undermined by the third caption's quiet exception. The ghost-line register carrying the changed Primm-read is visible to the reader in the image without being flagged by the narrator — the field is doing the work before the caption catches up. The four-figure composition rhymes with E09 Panel 1 ("loose four, voluntary, not yet shoulder to shoulder") but the palette is day rather than night — this is not the urgency of the E09 to E14 arc; this is the aftermath.

### Panel 2 — Not a Blank

**Image:** Close-in on Windman — not a full portrait, but the angle that shows the fan in his right hand, the ghost-line field extending forward, and the field-read bracket visible in the air at the edge of his perception. This is the same visual format used in E08 Panel 2 (top-left vignette): the FIELD READ bracket, established as the series' notation for what his pressure-sense returns. The bracket's positional grammar is unchanged from E08 and E09 — one slot per signature his field returns: Tiebi, the Primm position, Pho-boman.

The bracket now reads:

*[FIELD READ: irregular-mass (known, echo: T-04) / P-03 (low amplitude, constant) / broth-type (warm, 62 kg)]*

The middle entry — the one that has always returned a blank, the one that has been a dash-placeholder since E08 because Windman has no category for two fragments of one thing in proximity — is no longer a dash. Where the bracket used to carry a bare *—* it now carries a reading:

*P-03: low amplitude / constant / not spiking / not off*

Below the bracket, in the same field-read font, a second annotation that has never appeared before in the series' field-read register. It is in a different weight — lighter, less certain, as though the field is filing a report about itself:

*[W-01 echo: present / constant / source: not external]*

Windman's expression — what is visible above and below the bandage, the set of his jaw, the angle of his fan — is the expression of someone who has received a field-return they have no framework for. Not alarmed. Not certain. The expression of a body reading data it does not have a column for.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The field had always returned a blank for that position.*
- CAPTION (below): *It was not returning a blank.*
- CAPTION (below): *He had checked forty-three times in two days.*

**Why it lands:** The FIELD READ bracket has been the series' precise instrument for the fragment-resonance asymmetry since E08 — the dash-placeholder for Primm's position was the most efficient single expression of what Windman's field cannot categorize. Here, the dash is replaced by an entry that is not a category, just a reading: *low amplitude / constant / not spiking / not off.* The new line — *W-01 echo: present / constant / source: not external* — is the episode's fused realization in bracket form: the field is reading Windman back to himself at low constant amplitude, which means the resonance is running in both directions. The narrator does not explain this. The "forty-three times in two days" is the comedy register of the series applied to an experience that is not funny — the precision of the count is exactly the register Windman uses for everything, applied here to something he cannot solve with precision.

### Panel 3 — What One Body Feels Like

**Image:** Interior. The nondescript room — above a shopfront or similar, low ceiling (not a problem at current scale), neutral walls, a window letting in flat city light. The four are present but not in conference: Tiebi is near the wall, beret straight, looking at nothing in particular; Pho-boman is to one side, broth warm and unhurried, noodle-brows in the attentive configuration; Windman is standing, fan open but not aimed, at the angle of someone who has stopped pretending to do something else.

Primm is at the window. Not looking at the street — looking at his gloved hand, palm up, where one of the orbiting mini-orbs has stopped. Not the motionless stop of Panel 4 in E09 (no answer, the document doing everything) — a different stillness, the stillness of a body that has registered something from the inside and is examining the inside. The light bending slightly at his arm's reach, the DRIFT field running at low passive amplitude even though he is not using it. It has been running at low passive amplitude for forty-three hours.

This is the panel that does not use the FIELD READ bracket. This is Primm's panel, delivered from the inside rather than Windman's outside field-read: the mini-orb that has stopped is at the position closest to where the resonance registers, and Primm is looking at the space around it the way a person looks at a word that has been used correctly in a sentence they have never heard before.

Between the two fragment-figures in the frame — Windman on one side of the room, Primm at the window — the residual field distortions of both men are faintly visible in the air between them. Not firing, not aimed. Present. The ghost-lines and the light-bend in the same space, not entangled the way they were in E08 Panel 5 (the involuntary flutter), but running in parallel at low constant amplitude. The reader has seen this air before. The air has changed.

Tiebi has been watching this for approximately four minutes. He has not said anything. His beret is exactly where it always is.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *His field had been running since the building.*
- CAPTION (below): *Not the way it ran when he was using it.*
- TIEBI (flat, to no one specific, the observation of someone who has been waiting to confirm a fact before stating it): *It's not stopping.*

**Why it lands:** This is the "fused realization" panel the series uses for its strongest beats — two canon facts collapsing into one meaning. The DRIFT field running at low passive amplitude is: (a) Primm's power, running without use, and (b) the resonance expressing itself through the only instrument Primm's body has. The prep field contacted the AEGIS-4 fragment signature and the signature is now carrying back through the field. Tiebi's line — *"It's not stopping"* — is the episode's only line of dialogue in the middle three panels, and it is delivered flat, as a filed observation, not as alarm. This is correct: Tiebi is the only person in the room who was human before any of this happened, and his register for observations he cannot explain is the same register he uses for bridge clearances and access codes. He has four words. He uses four words.

### Panel 4 — A Word in a Language Not Yet Learned

**Image:** Close-in on Pho-boman. Not a portrait — the angle that shows the visor and the noodle-face from approximately eye-level with his midsection, the belly-dragon at this angle fully visible: the red Chinese dragon on blue background, scales and claws, tail wrapping. The broth is warm in the helmet, noodle-brows in a configuration that has appeared once before in the series — the lower-compressed configuration from E09 Panel 4, the one the series vocabulary had no name for yet. But it is not identical: the E09 configuration was the response to the document, to disposal, to a filed fact about what he is made of. This configuration is different in register. The E09 one was: *I have received information I did not have a framework for.* This one is: *I am receiving an absence I did not know was an absence until now.*

What the field-distortion register shows, visible at the visor's edge: nothing. No FIELD READ bracket, because Pho-boman carries no incident-signature — his thermohydromorphic presence produces a thermal signature, a broth-temperature reading, a weight in the world. But the incident-signature field between the two fragments — the low-constant-amplitude running that Windman's field-read is tracking and that Primm's DRIFT is carrying — does not include Pho-boman. It runs past him, around him, between the other two. He is aware of this. He is aware of it the way a person becomes aware of a frequency they have never been able to hear, by noticing that others respond to something they cannot locate.

The cilantro nose, the noodle-brows in the new configuration, the broth very still in the helmet — the face of a being who has just discovered, for the first time, that there is a room he is not in.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *He could feel what the air was doing between the other two.*
- CAPTION (below): *He could not feel any part of it.*

**Why it lands:** This is the panel the audience has been holding since E09 — the asymmetry between the three who carry the incident-signature mark and the one who was born complete, without it, is now visible to Pho-boman himself for the first time. The E09 field-read confirmed his absence in the bracket; that was the reader's knowledge. Here the absence becomes Pho-boman's knowledge, delivered as a felt-quality rather than a data point. The narrator uses the series' minimum-true-thing register: he can feel what the air is doing between the other two; he cannot feel any part of it; he noticed the difference. Three captions. No drama. The noodle-brow configuration and the belly-dragon in frame are the visual anchors — the dragon's warmth against the episode's quiet register. The broth going still in the helmet is the series' visual vocabulary for a Pho-boman moment of stillness; it has appeared before; it means: *he is not filing this one yet.*

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- **The cause:** the reintegration chamber's ambient prep field contacted both AEGIS-4 fragment signatures during the E14 extraction — but by two different routes. Primm passed through the sublevel-2 staging area in person; the prep field brushed his signature directly for the 1.2 seconds it took him to clear the threshold. Windman never entered the building: the second contact came through the air-handling system, where the pressure-pulse he routed into the ductwork from the loading dock carried his fragment-signature down into the chamber's prep-field radius. Two signatures, one chamber, only one of the two fragments physically present. The preliminary alignment sequence logged both at low amplitude — no reintegration occurred, but the contact woke the resonance from its E08 dormant-flutter state to an active, constant signal. The heroes have felt the change for two days and have no diagnosis. The reader, who saw the chamber, the amber lamp, and the ductwork pulse in E14, has had the diagnosis since the moment the log recorded two signatures.
- **The structural fact:** the resonance running at constant low amplitude is the two true fragments being partially re-entangled — not reintegrated, not un-made, but placed in a state the reintegration chamber's preliminary sequence was designed to establish before full integration. They are, in biological terms, staged. The recall's process has advanced without executing. They walked out of the building carrying the first step of their own un-making.
- **Tiebi's lesser echo:** the prep field touched only the two fragment signatures; Tiebi's lesser incident-signature echo was not contacted. But the shift in the resonance field has made his echo slightly more legible to Windman's field-read — a side-effect of the field's recalibration toward the two primary signatures. Tiebi is not aware of this. It may become relevant.
- **Pho-boman's absence:** the prep field passed through Pho-boman without contact — no AEGIS-4 signature to find. For the first time in the series, Pho-boman is aware of his own absence from the resonance field, rather than simply carrying it as a structural fact. The audience has tracked this asymmetry since E09's field-read bracket. Now Pho-boman carries it as knowledge. What he does with that knowledge is reserved.
- **The escalation:** E14's `[REDACTED]` escalation is still processing offscreen. The net is forming. This episode does not show it. The audience knows it is happening.

## Differentiation / what's new

The rescue arc (E10–E14) was structured around action, clock, and consequence: capture, investigation, infiltration, rescue. The consequence was hidden in the rescue's final beat.

E15 is the cost episode — the series' first episode whose entire subject is something invisible, felt rather than shown, impossible to act on. It is a quiet episode after five episodes of escalating action, and the quiet is structural: the resonance does not produce SFX, does not have a corporate document, does not escalate on a 45-minute timer. It simply runs. This is the correct register for a cost that the heroes will live with for six more episodes.

The split-frame close (Panel 5) is new to the series: the four-figures-same-direction grammar of the convergence close is inverted again, but differently from E09 (alley, compression). Here the separation is the form and the signal is the content. Two frames, two separate locations, one thing running between them. The geometry of the episode's final image is not reunion — it is the structure of a signal that works across distance, which is a different kind of closeness than the series has shown.

What is new here:
- The FIELD READ bracket updated with an active Primm entry — no longer a dash-placeholder.
- The *W-01 echo: source not external* annotation — the resonance running in both directions, Windman reading himself in the field.
- Pho-boman's felt-awareness of his own absence from the resonance — new register for the series' longest-running structural asymmetry.
- Tiebi's lesser echo becoming more legible as a side-effect — planted, not named.
- The split-frame close: two bodies, one signal, the city as the gap it runs across.
- No SFX callout in the closing panel: not the empty-box grammar of E09 Panel 4 (presence of silence), just the absence of the callout format. The resonance is not an event.
