# S01E05 — Panel Prompts

Image-generation prompts and episode-specific visual canon for
[[S01E05 — Hold Your Own Weight]] (Primm's origin). Built by the
**panel-prompt-smith** agent.

> Each prompt is **self-contained** — image models have no memory, so every prompt
> restates the art style, the character, and the props in full. The global art style
> and recurring descriptors live in [[Visual Style Guide]]; reuse the wordings below
> **verbatim** across panels for consistency.

This issue is **one comic page resolved across five images** (P1–P5). It is
**vertical** — Primm falls *down* through the GastroDefense Inc. tower, one floor per
panel, phasing through every surface he tries to grip, until he works out (alone, in a
sub-floor) that the problem was never the phasing. **Primm is alone** the entire page —
no teammate appears, and the only other human is one pair of legs at extreme background
distance in P3, oblivious, already gone. The seam: **P1 continues**
[[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 4's bottom-left quadrant (the corridor-wall
phase-out), and **P5 hands off into** [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 5's
overhead compass-spoke shot (the purple coat descending a stairwell to the street).

fully solid figure, and NOT full invisibility. Reuse this wording wherever it applies:

### Ghost-image / size-contrast technique (P4)

Used in P4 to show the eureka (the instant before and the instant after he turns his own
weight back on) as ONE frame instead of two sequential panels. Rendered as two
clearly-staged instances of the same character within one panel (one slightly desaturated
"before" instance, one full-color "now" instance), NOT a blurry double-exposure and NOT a
whole-frame motion blur — so it stays inside the clean hand-inked flat-color look. Reuse
this block **verbatim** in P4:

> a SINGLE-PANEL "ghost-image" sequence showing one continuous action as TWO
> clearly-staged instances of the SAME character in one frame, both cleanly drawn in the
> flat cel style (NOT a blurred double-exposure, NOT a whole-frame motion blur): an
> EARLIER instance rendered slightly DESATURATED and tonally cooler (the "before" beat),
> and a LATER instance rendered in FULL saturated color (the "now" beat), the two read
> left-to-right as one motion across the panel. Keep the character's identity cues
> identical in both instances so they read as the same person; vary only their STATE and
> pose to show the physics.
