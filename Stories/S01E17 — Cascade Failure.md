# S01E17 — Cascade Failure

> **Logline:** The thing the rescue escalated to turns out not to be a person — it is the next product, and inside it Windman hears a field the corporation used to have and deleted.

---

## Title

**"Cascade Failure"** — the engineering failure mode in which one component's fault triggers a chain that none of the downstream components can interrupt, because each was designed to assume the prior step would complete successfully. The AEGIS-4 reintegration chamber failed to complete its procedure; the failure escalated to the system whose design assumed that completion; the system auto-notified its successor's specification because the successor cannot be finalized without the predecessor's data; a client deadline closes. Each step was within spec. The title names the structure. The episode is the structure arriving as a form.

## Differentiation / what's new

The prior Movement-closing reveal episodes (E09 opened on the specification; E11 decoded the contract) each arrived at their reveal through a document the heroes found and read. E17 is the first reveal that arrives through perception: Windman hears something that is not there. The form confirms what he heard; the form does not precede the perception.

This is the episode where the corporate antagonist's nature completes its inversion. The big bad of the series is not AEGIS-5 — AEGIS-5 is a successor product line that cannot be finished without a data input. The big bad is the specification revision that removed Appendix C in Rev. 3. The closest thing to a villain in the back half is a document management decision made before the heroes existed.
