# S01E17 — Cascade Failure

> **Logline:** The thing the rescue escalated to turns out not to be a person — it is the next product, and inside it Windman hears a field the corporation used to have and deleted.

> **Stage:** Broad-strokes outline. Synopsis, continuity, and threads are locked to the [[Series Arc]] (Movement III close). Panel breakdown, captions, and image prompts are a later production pass (panel-prompt-smith).

## Continuity / placement

**Movement III close.** Follows [[S01E16 — Prior Art]] (lineage decoded). Precedes [[S01E18 — Resonance]]. Resolves the `[REDACTED]` escalation recipient from [[S01E14 — Dead Reckoning]].

## Synopsis

E14's escalation resolves: the recipient is **AEGIS-5** — not a person, not a villain, but the successor prototype program. It is auto-notified of any AEGIS-4 recovery failure because the AEGIS-5 specification depends on AEGIS-4 reintegration data to finalize its own design parameters. AEGIS-5 cannot be completed if AEGIS-4's reintegration data is not delivered. **AEGIS-5 is a specification, not a character** (author-ratified): it communicates only by form and hardware interrupt, never by speech. GastroDefense does not send emissaries; it sends forms.

Near the AEGIS-5 development lab, Windman's aerokinesis perceives the facility's air-circulation signature, and within it the legacy AEGIS-4 specification still running in AEGIS-5's firmware as a design reference. The original spec, **Revision 1, contained a consent field** — Appendix C, "Voluntary Compliance Protocol" — removed in Revision 3 before completion (the version the heroes read in E09 was Rev. 7: *"[Field not applicable — units were not designed to provide consent]"*). Appendix C was never written. But consent was, once, a design parameter. Windman hears it as a pressure-difference: the thing the specification used to hold and no longer holds.

## Thread advanced

- AEGIS-5 introduced — as a procedural dependency and beyond-20 engine, not an antagonist.
- The consent field reveal reshapes the un-make question: the fragments were *designed* to have a say, and the design was changed before they existed.

## Twist / suspense

- AEGIS-5's "negotiation" is a compliance notification served via Tiebi's legal identity, carrying a client deadline. "Alternative enforcement" is noted as a contract option — undefined. (Sets up E19's injunction.)
- The removed consent field is the emotional fuse for Movement IV: consent was deleted, not absent.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- AEGIS-5 needs them not out of malice but as a data dependency: their un-making is a prerequisite for the next product. The corporation's indifference is total — they are an input.
- The deleted Appendix C is a beyond-20 seed: what the Voluntary Compliance Protocol would have said is never recoverable.

## Differentiation / what's new

The antagonist "reveal" that refuses to be a reveal: the big bad is a successor product line and a deleted document field. The tone rule holds — the most chilling beat in the series is a specification with a missing appendix.

## Reserved / open threads
