# S01E08 — Four-Body Problem

> **Logline:** A driverless GastroDefense refrigerated truck rolls through a city block at night with nobody at the wheel; four strangers converge in two loose pairs from opposite ends of the block, each following a different signal, and accidentally stop it together — while a recall notice on its side quietly lists four unit codes nobody has time to read.

---

## Title

**"Four-Body Problem"** — the generalization of the two-body physics problem, the case where the number of interacting masses grows beyond what any single equation can cleanly solve. It rhymes precisely with E06's "Two-Body Problem": the same dry physics-term family (Momentum Not Included / Specific Heat / Two-Body Problem), the same double meaning underneath. Two bodies was already complicated. Four is the problem the rest of the arc is built on. The title names the mathematical fact; the episode is the fact.

---

## Continuity / placement

This is **Issue 8, the close of Act III ("Reunion").** It follows [[S01E06 — Two-Body Problem]] (Windman + Tiebi first meeting) and [[S01E07 — Specific Heat]] (Primm + Pho-boman first meeting) and opens the seam toward Act IV.

**E06 closed** on Windman and Tiebi as a loose pair on a night street — two figures facing the same direction, "neither of them said the word 'team'; they walked the same direction anyway." By E08, they have been walking that direction. The city has been presenting itself to them as a series of objects.

**E07 closed** on Primm and Pho-boman at the threshold of a GastroDefense cold-storage freezer doorway, facing outward toward the loading dock and the city beyond — two figures, neither speaking. They have been moving since.

The two pairs have not crossed paths. E08 opens with both of them independently converging on the same city block — not because they have been looking for each other, but because the same loud object is the loudest signal in the city tonight and each of them, by their own sensors, has noticed it.

The **incident-signature payoff** — the strongest signal between the two true AEGIS-4 fragments, Windman ↔ Primm — has been withheld across E06 and E07 and lands here for the first time. These two have never been in the same physical space. What the audience holds: two literal pieces of one original body, in the same frame, with no shared language for what they are to each other.

E08 feeds forward to **Act IV**: GastroDefense Inc.'s recall protocol is already filed, already named, already on the side of a truck that did not arrive at the processing center. E09 opens on the corporation noticing.

> **Seam test for any future edit:** does this episode close on exactly four figures — Windman, Tiebi, Pho-boman, Primm — facing the same direction, the truck stopped behind them, the recall notice on the truck's side panel legible to the reader and unread by any character, the team not named, no corporate antagonist speaking? If any figure leaves alone, if the team is named, or if GastroDefense becomes an active speaking presence, the Act III close is broken.

## Format

This episode is a **6-panel comic page** (one page; the extra panel over the Act III standard is justified by the scale — this is the first time all four are in frame together, the convergence mechanic requires a setup panel, the kinetic centerpiece earns its own panel, and the resonance payoff needs room to hold). Six panels is the correct budget for what has to happen here without rushing any of it.

The visual space is a **sloped city block at night** — one block, one object, no enclosure. This is the correct setting for this cast: no low ceiling (Tiebi's vulnerability would otherwise become the episode's subject), no steel racking (Primm's vulnerability would similarly overwhelm), enough open sky for Windman's pressure-map to read distance without obstruction. The coldness of the refrigeration unit is present but contained to the truck — it is a plot tool, not a trap, which is correct for E08's register (the trap episodes are E07; this one is convergence).

Where E06 had one warm color pop (the streetlamp against a dark palette) and E07 had two (Pho-boman's reddish-orange, Primm's cyan against institutional grey-blue), E08 is the first time all four canonical signature colors are in the same frame simultaneously: Windman's blue compression-rings, Primm's violet/cyan DRIFT light-bend, Pho-boman's reddish-orange broth-rope, Tiebi's mustard-gold growing body. The four-color frame in Panel 4 is a deliberate visual event — the palette has been building toward this, one color per issue, and here they arrive together.

Captions are the dry case-file narrator throughout — boxed, deadpan, observational. Dialogue is minimal. The narrator does most of the work. The team-feeling is delivered by the narrator naming, in the driest possible register, the minimum true thing.

### Panel 4 — Four-Body Problem

**Image:** The climax panel. The kinetic centerpiece. This panel is the one frame in the series where all four SFX fire simultaneously. The truck has reached the bottom of its stopping-arc — this image catches the exact moment of stopping: the four-way interlock visible in one frame.

**Windman:** fan completing a controlled lateral sweep aimed at the front wheel-gap — concentric blue HWUF pressure-rings rolling under the cab, pushing against the rolling direction precisely at the gap between the front wheels. Not a blast; a compression wave, aimed and steady, the fused perception-and-power that E02 established now deployed at an eight-metric-ton problem. The blue rings are dense, deliberate, locked to the rolling direction like a key in a lock.

**Primm:** gloved palm extended toward the front axle at arm's reach — violet/cyan DRIFT light-bend wrapping the axle, lifting the front fraction of the truck's weight. Not enough to stop the truck alone. Approximately eighteen percent of eight metric tons. The field is holding it. The caption will note the percentage with the same inventory precision as E05's cable-tray inventory, arriving at a different conclusion.

**Tiebi:** planted in the truck's rolling path, at maximum architecturally-viable size — the size that fits under the railway bridge at 2.4 meters clearance with what the caption will call a compromise. Wardrobe-sized. The STOMP ground-ripple visible beneath his feet where he has planted himself on the pavement. The bridge arch is visible above him — not quite grazing him. He has calculated this precisely. The beret is exactly where it always is. The bridge has been informed of his plan. The bridge is not impressed. The bridge has allowed it anyway.

**Pho-boman:** rear of the truck, noodle-rope from the SLURP cinched around the rear axle — reddish-orange rope tight, broth steaming, maintaining distance from the cold exhaust vent but not enough distance to stop the bind from holding. His noodle-brows are in the configuration of a being that has identified the contents of a vehicle and is now wrapping part of that vehicle around its axle and is still not entirely sure these are separate decisions.

All four SFX in the panel:

*HWUF —*
*DRIFT —*
*STOMP —*
*SLURP —*

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The truck stopped.*
- CAPTION (below): *This took four of them.*
- CAPTION (below): *None of them said anything about that.*

**Why it lands:** The four SFX landing together in one panel is the "first time they feel like a team" beat, delivered as a mechanical fact. The truck stopped because four different power-sets arrived at the same eight-metric-ton object, two pairs converging from opposite ends, and their applications interlocked without coordination: pressure at the wheel-gap, axle-lift at the front, a body-sized obstacle dropped into the path of a truck already losing speed to the other three, a bind at the rear axle. This is what the series' competence comedy has been building toward — not sentiment, but the dry notation that the thing worked. The caption staircase says the absolute minimum true thing: the truck stopped, this took four of them, nobody said anything about it. Three lines. The joy is entirely in the geometry. Tiebi's bridge calculation paying off with a "compromise" is the slapstick peak of the episode — he solved the ceiling problem with precision and the result is a wardrobe-sized man under a bridge, beret on, which is exactly right. And it is the panel where his Panel 3 failure is redeemed: alone he was bigger, not heavier, and the truck shrugged him off; here the same planted body holds, because the other three are bleeding the truck's momentum at the same instant. The four-color frame — all four signature colors at full saturation in one frame for the first time in the series — is the visual climax the palette has been building toward since E02.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- Windman and Primm are two fragments of one original body — Project AEGIS-4. They have never been in the same physical space since the fracture. The residual field-entanglement in Panel 5 — the involuntary overlap of ghost-lines and DRIFT light-bend — is what two halves of one original body do when they share air for the first time. Neither has the vocabulary for this. The audience does.
- The recall notice on the truck lists them by unit code. W-01 is Windman. P-02 is Pho-boman. P-03 is Primm. T-04 is Tiebi. The corporation filed this paperwork before any of them knew the others existed. It dispatched the truck. The truck did not arrive. This fact has not yet registered anywhere in GastroDefense's operational systems. It will, in E09.
- The truck's cargo manifest — visible through the cab window if you look — reads: FROZEN PHO BROTH (BULK). ORIGIN: GASTRODEFENSE CATERING DIVISION. DESTINATION: RECALL PROCESSING CENTER. The broth in the cargo hold is the same lineage of broth that made Pho-boman. Pho-boman located the truck by scent. He found it personally relevant. The audience knows why it is personally relevant. Pho-boman has filed the relevance without having access to its cause.
- Tiebi's incident-signature — the lesser echo, the transformed-human mark — registered in Windman's FIELD READ in Panel 2 as "irregular-mass (known)": already categorized from E06, already filed. The dash-placeholder for Primm is the signal that has never been categorized, never been filed, the one that returned blank because Windman has no category that fits two fragments of the same thing at close range. He still does not. He has decided this is acceptable.
- Pho-boman's absence of incident-signature is confirmed again here in the FIELD READ bracket: "broth-type (warm, 62 kg)." No resonance. No echo. Born-complete. The difference between the three who carry the mark and the one who was born without it is legible to the audience in the bracket's logic; it is not available to any of the four.
- Four beings just stopped a truck by interlocking four different power-sets around one object, two pairs converging from opposite ends, with zero coordination and zero communication. The corporation that built two of them as an integrated prototype could not have designed a more efficient team. It did not design this team. Nobody did.

- **Panel 2:** the split-register panel runs four diagnostic clocks simultaneously without ever touching each other. The comedy is in the parallel register — four people doing their own math, arriving at their own conclusions, about the same object. The FIELD READ bracket is the tonal peak of the issue's humor as a single image: the dash-placeholder column does the work a paragraph cannot. Tiebi's bridge-clearance caption is the setup.

## Differentiation / what's new

The diaspora issues (E02–E05) were each a solo power-discovery — one person, one problem, alone. Each one was about a body learning what it is.

The first reunion issues (E06, E07) were each a two-person encounter — one incident, two powers accidentally fitting together, one narrator button naming what neither of them said. Each one was about a body discovering it is not the only one.

E08 is the convergence — four people, one object, no coordination, and the mechanical fact that it worked. The power-sets interlock not because anyone planned it but because the object was present and each of them arrived with the right angle. The joy of the team-feeling is delivered entirely as competence: the truck stopped, this took four of them, nobody said anything about that. The audience gets the warm beat; the narrator names the count. The heroes face outward.

The slapstick has scaled up across the arc as promised: E06 had one street-level incident with two powers. E07 had a full power-neutralization inside a steel cage with two powers used in one application. E08 has an eight-metric-ton problem solved by four applications, two pairs converging from opposite ends, with no communication. The escalation is correct.

What is new here:
- The four-color frame. The palette has been building toward this. It arrives in Panel 4 and holds through Panels 5 and 6.
- All four SFX simultaneously for the first time: HWUF — DRIFT — STOMP — SLURP in one panel.
- The fragment-to-fragment resonance payoff — Windman ↔ Primm — delivered not as a named event but as a residual field-entanglement in the still air of Panel 5. The caption says the minimum. The audience holds the rest.
- The first Tiebi/Pho-boman exchange. Four words. Filed.
- The recall notice. The corporation's name for them, on a truck they just stopped, in small type nobody is reading.
- The count: "There were four of them now." The series' first acknowledgment that all four are present. Not a name. A number.
