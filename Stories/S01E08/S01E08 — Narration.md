# S01E08 — Narration (Voiceover)

Spoken-word narration voiceover for [[S01E08 — Four-Body Problem]], written for ElevenLabs
text-to-speech to play as background narration over a slideshow / motion-comic of the six
panels (see [[S01E08 — Panel Prompts]]) and the print [[S01E08 — Captions]]. Written for
the ear. Dry case-file narrator — deadpan, observational, the minimum true thing. ~160
words, ~64 seconds at 150 wpm.

Note: E08 carries a single STOMP in Panel 3 (Tiebi's failed solo attempt) and all four SFX
(HWUF, DRIFT, STOMP, SLURP) firing together in Panel 4, plus one line of dialogue (Tiebi,
Panel 5: "You were in the freezer."). These are lettering, not narration. They do not appear
in this script.

## Clean script (paste into ElevenLabs)

> Everything in this block is spoken text only — no labels, nothing that shouldn't be
> voiced. Paste as-is.

The truck had been unattended for eleven minutes.

Four different people noticed this for four different reasons. The city did not comment.

The mass was moving. Something on its far side was not the mass.

The metal was approximately one freight truck. The other signal was approximately nothing he had a framework for.

The bridge clearance was two point four meters.

The smell was familiar. The smell was also behind a wall. He found this interesting.

One of them tried it alone. Eight metric tons declined. The math required the others. Nobody had asked them.

The truck stopped.

This took four of them.

None of them said anything about that.

The air between them had not decided what to do with itself.

None of them said the word.

There were four of them now.

GastroDefense Inc. operated fourteen field-survey drones in the district.

## Runtime

- **Word count:** ~160 words.
- **Runtime:** ~64 s at 150 wpm; ~68 s at a more deliberate 140 wpm. With the tuned
  pauses — especially the long silences after "The truck stopped." and "The air between
  them had not decided what to do with itself." — the track plays nearer **~80–85 s**.
- The three isolated lines of Panel 4 ("The truck stopped." / "This took four of them." /
  "None of them said anything about that.") are the comedic peak; the pauses between them
  are load-bearing. Do not compress them.

## ElevenLabs delivery notes

- **Voice character:** same dry, mid-to-low documentary narrator as [[S01E01 — Narration]]
  and [[S01E02 — Narration]] — deadpan, unhurried, flat affect. The narrator does not
  celebrate the truck stopping. The narrator notes it. Match the same voice used for
  prior episodes to maintain series continuity.
- **Panel 2 register:** the four split-vignette captions read as four separate, equally-
  weighted dry observations. No one vignette is more urgent than any other. The comedy is
  in the parallel flatness. Hold the same unruffled tone across all four.
- **Panel 4 — the peak:** "The truck stopped." lands as a rubber stamp, not a triumphant
  declaration. Each of the three lines gets its own beat of silence after it. "None of
  them said anything about that." is the button — deliver it the quietest of the three.
- **Panel 5 — the still:** "The air between them had not decided what to do with itself."
  is the emotional center of the episode, delivered in the narrator's quietest register.
  The long break after it is not dead air; it is the episode's held breath. Let it sit.
- **Panel 6 — the close:** "There were four of them now." is the count, not a declaration.
  Flat and final. "GastroDefense Inc. operated fourteen field-survey drones in the
  district." is the Act IV plant — deliver it in the same inventory register as the
  "eight metric tons declined" line in Panel 3: dry, precise, not alarming.
- **SFX and dialogue are lettering, not narration:** the lone STOMP (Panel 3), HWUF, DRIFT,
  STOMP, SLURP (Panel 4), and Tiebi's "You were in the freezer." (Panel 5) are in-panel
  elements. Do not voice them.
- **Settings:** expressiveness ~30–40%, stability ~70%. The deadpan is the performance.
- **Voice archetypes to audition:** documentary / corporate / narrator with dry, low-affect
  character — "George," "Harry," "Arnold," or series-established equivalent. Avoid
  enthusiastic / warm / heroic.
