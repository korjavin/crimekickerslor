# S01E03 — Panel Prompts

Image-generation prompts and episode-specific visual canon for
[[S01E03 — Momentum Not Included]] (Tiebi's size-shift diaspora origin).

> Each prompt is **self-contained** — image models have no memory, so every prompt
> restates the art style, the character, and the props in full. The global art style
> and recurring descriptors live in [[Visual Style Guide]]; reuse the wordings below
> **verbatim** across panels for consistency.

This issue is **one comic page resolved across six images** (P1–P6). It is MOBILE —
four distinct spaces (elevator lapel, office coat closet, marble lobby, revolving door,
parking-garage ramp, parking-garage deck). **Tiebi is alone** the entire page — no other
hero appears. The Executive is an oblivious background giant in P1–P2 only; all other
humans are unnamed, indifferent crowd. The seam: **P1 continues**
[[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 4 (lapel pin, elevator), and **P6 hands off into**
[[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 5's overhead compass-spoke shot.

## Episode visual canon

### Tiebi — appearance (verbatim from [[Tiebi]], with mustard accent rendered)

[[Tiebi]]'s character page is the single source of truth for his look. He is the
**only hero** in the issue. Reuse this descriptor block **verbatim** in every panel
(scaled to camera distance and to his current size — full block at full height; the
same identity cues, never abbreviated, at micro scale):

> a humanoid man, male, ~175–180 cm tall at normal size, medium build, slightly lean
> but healthy, realistic human proportions; an ordinary human head, a neutral and
> friendly medium-skin-toned face, no facial hair, normal expressive eyes (eyes clearly
> visible, NOT covered or blindfolded); his signature feature is a classic French beret,
> slightly tilted, dark navy, snug and soft with a small nub at the top center; he wears
> a simple fitted shirt/tunic in MUSTARD-GOLD (his signature accent color), plain dark
> trousers, and simple flat dark shoes.

- **Mustard accent:** his shirt/tunic is **MUSTARD-GOLD**, his signature warm color — the
  recurring warm pop across every panel of the issue against the grey/fluorescent baseline.
  Reuse the exact phrase **"shirt/tunic in MUSTARD-GOLD"** every time.
- **Identity-across-scales rule (the two non-negotiable cues):** at every scale, the two
  cues that make him read as the SAME person are the **dark-navy tilted French beret** and
  the **mustard-gold shirt**. They must be present and legible at postage-stamp scale
  (P1, P2, P5 ghost A) exactly as at full height (P3, P4, P5 ghost B, P6).

### Scale-rendering register

> Tiebi is the project's size-shifter, so the same character must read as the SAME person at
> **postage-stamp / micro scale** and at **full human height** within one issue. Two rules:

- **Micro scale (world-as-giant from his POV):** when Tiebi is postage-stamp-sized, do
  NOT shrink the scene — render the world AROUND him at giant scale so HE reads as tiny by
  contrast: dinner-plate-sized props, towering giant body-parts of other people cropped
  out of frame above him, a marble floor that recedes like a plaza. He stays a perfectly
  formed, perfectly proportioned, fully human miniature (NOT a chibi, NOT a child, NOT
  distorted) — just very small. His **dark-navy tilted beret** and **mustard-gold shirt**
  stay legible even at this scale.
- **Full scale (normal staging):** when Tiebi is full-sized (~175–180 cm), stage him as an
  ordinary-height man in a normal human environment, same beret and same mustard shirt, no
  scale tricks. The *contrast* between the two registers across panels is the storytelling.

### Ghost-image / size-contrast technique

> Used in P5 to show a single physical action (micro-sprint → full-size roll) as one frame
> instead of two sequential panels. Rendered as two clearly-staged instances of the same
> character within one panel (one slightly desaturated "earlier" instance, one full-color
> "later/now" instance), NOT a blurry double-exposure and NOT a motion-blur smear of the
> whole frame — so it stays inside the clean hand-inked flat-color look. Reuse this block
> **verbatim** in any panel that needs it:

> a SINGLE-PANEL "ghost-image" sequence showing one continuous action as TWO clearly-staged
> instances of the SAME character in one frame, both cleanly drawn in the flat cel style
> (NOT a blurred double-exposure, NOT a whole-frame motion blur): an EARLIER instance
> rendered slightly DESATURATED and tonally cooler (the "before" beat), and a LATER instance
> rendered in FULL saturated color (the "now" beat), the two read left-to-right as one motion
> across the panel. Keep the character's identity cues identical in both instances so they
> read as the same person; vary only their SIZE and pose to show the physics.
