# S01E10 — Half-Life

> **Logline:** A department that has not run in a year executes its retrieval protocol, and the one of them who is half of the original body walks into a steel box to check whether it has an exit — and finds out it does not.

> **Stage:** Broad-strokes outline. Synopsis, continuity, and threads are locked to the [[Series Arc]] (Movement II, midpoint). Panel breakdown, captions, and image prompts are a later production pass (panel-prompt-smith).

## Continuity / placement

**Movement II opener; series midpoint and largest turn so far.** Direct seam from [[S01E09 — Variance Report]]: E09 closed on `SCHEDULING RETRIEVAL` behind frosted glass. E10 opens as that status becomes `RETRIEVAL IN PROGRESS`. Precedes [[S01E11 — Closed Loop]].

## Synopsis

The Retrieval Division runs its standard asset-recovery protocol — inelegant, form-based, budget-constrained. **Primm is captured.** Not through tactical cleverness and not because GastroDefense understands his weakness: the protocol's containment unit is a steel-frame holding structure standard for industrial equipment storage, and Primm phases into it to confirm whether an exit exists on the far side. The steel subframe constrains his gravity field. The door closes. A capture confirmation is filed. This is within spec — the corporation is not being clever, it is being procedural, and the procedure happens to be exactly wrong for him.

The other three are not captured. Pho-boman's incident-signature null means the drone sweep correlates only three unit codes against recovery targets; he walks out undetected. Tiebi shrinks into a ventilation gap; Windman reads an exit route the drones have no sensor coverage over. The three remaining have no word for what just happened. The ticket updates: `ASSET RECOVERED 1/4 — REINTEGRATION SCHEDULED, PENDING SECONDARY ASSET RECOVERY`.

## Thread advanced

- The retrieval specification becomes lived stakes: "Return to Origin Facility" is now a place Primm is in.
- The team is split for the first time since converging — three outside, one inside a wall.

## Twist / suspense

- Windman feels the absence where the fragment-resonance signal used to be. He cannot name what he is missing. He files it where he can reach it. (First lived consequence of the W↔P resonance, delivered as loss.)
- The episode ends before reintegration is attempted: it is on hold until Windman is also in custody (paid off in E11). The clock is running.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- Primm was captured by the metal-mass vulnerability established in [[S01E05 — Hold Your Own Weight]] / [[S01E07 — Specific Heat]] — but GastroDefense did not target it. It is incidental. The corporation is soulless, not menacing.
- The "gap in the air" Windman feels is the dormant half of his own original body being moved out of range.

## Differentiation / what's new

The first time the team loses. After the convergence (E08) and the recall read (E09), this is the turn that splits them and makes the recall real. The register shifts from corporate dread to active emergency — but the antagonist is still a workflow, not a villain.
