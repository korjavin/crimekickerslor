# S01E07 — Captions

Print-ready narration captions for [[S01E07 — Specific Heat]] — to letter onto the comic page beside each picture. 5 panels, 20 caption boxes plus two SFX. The driest issue in the series: near-wordless by design, with no direct dialogue between characters. Voice is the same case-file narrator as E01–E06, now running two simultaneous bureaucratic clocks — Pho-boman's broth thickening and Primm's surface inventory — interleaved in a single staircase without either clock addressing the other. The FIELD READ caption in Panel 2 and "that was the whole plan" in Panel 5 are the tonal peaks. Pairs with the art in [[S01E07 — Panel Prompts]].

## Lettering notes

- **SFX — DRIFT** (Panel 4): Primm's canonical SFX. Small and quiet here — integrated into the light-bend distortion that wraps Pho-boman's body. This is not the full field deployment; it is a contained, precise application. Do not letter it large. The SLURP that follows is the episode's contrast point.
- **SFX — SLURP** (Panel 5): Pho-boman's canonical SFX. The episode's loudest and warmest beat, lettered in contrast to the near-silence of the freezer across Panels 2–4. Bold, warm register. It lands at the threshold as gravity returns and the face reconstitutes — the punctuation of the exit. Let it breathe.
- **FIELD READ caption** (Panel 2): this line has a distinct typographic register — it is Primm's field returning data, formatted as a diagnostic readout. The brackets and all-caps label are intentional. Set it differently from the standard case-file caption boxes: monospace or scan-register lettering to distinguish the machine-voice from the narrator. The content — "(warm, 62 kg, broth-type)" — is the issue's tonal peak; the lettering style should let the absurdity of "broth-type" land without editorializing.
- **Caption staircase rhythm** (Panels 2–3): two interleaved clocks running without ever addressing each other. The broth captions and the surface-inventory captions share the column in alternation. Do not let them merge visually — they are two separate trains of thought. If the lettering layout permits, a subtle spacing break between the broth-clock lines and the Primm-inventory lines helps the reader track which clock is ticking.
- **"He filed this under: a problem"** (Panel 3): the narrator button for Primm's register. Flat, diagnostic, not ironic in tone even though it is ironic in content. Same register as the inventory lines above it — do not inflate it.
- **"Neither of them said anything about it"** (Panel 5): the closing button, quietest possible register. It covers the dissolved face, the beat-too-long stillness, the broth-queue, and the meter and a half of heroism. Same principle as E06's "Neither of them said the word 'team'" — name the absence, not the presence. Let it whisper.
- **No direct dialogue in this issue.** E07 is the silent counterpart to E06's first dialogue. Neither Primm nor Pho-boman speaks. All text is narration captions, one FIELD READ readout, and two SFX.
- **Deliberate near-silence throughout.** The freezer is quiet for five panels. The SLURP is the only sound that breaks it. Resist any impulse to add ambient SFX to the freezer interior — the silence is the episode's texture.
- **Open thread:** the FIELD READ caption is the series' first instance of a machine-register caption (as opposed to the case-file narrator). The lettering style for it is unresolved — flag for the author whether it stays within the standard caption box with a typographic shift, or sits in a distinct box shape (scan-panel / HUD register).
