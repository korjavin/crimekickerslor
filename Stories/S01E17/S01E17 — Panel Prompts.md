# S01E17 — Panel Prompts

Image-generation prompts and episode-specific visual canon for
[[S01E17 — Cascade Failure]] (Movement III close — the consent-field reveal and the
compliance notification). Built by the **panel-prompt-smith** agent.

> Each prompt is **self-contained** — image models have no memory, so every prompt
> restates the art style, every character in the frame, and the props in full. The
> global art style and recurring descriptors live in [[Visual Style Guide]]; reuse the
> wordings below **verbatim** across panels for consistency.

This issue is **one comic page resolved across six images** (P1–P6). It is the
**Movement III close**: the first back-half episode in daylight, a perceptual reveal
delivered through Windman's aerokinesis, two full-frame document panels, and a
compliance notification that does everything the episode's antagonist never appears
to do. The visual space is **two environments in sequence**: an **exterior near a
GastroDefense development annex** (edge-of-district, morning light, open air) and
briefly the **interior of a non-descript relay point** (copiers, cable trunks, nothing
else). AEGIS-5 never appears. GastroDefense never sends an emissary. A form does
everything.

### The FIELD READ bracket format (verbatim, P2)

In P2, Windman's ghost-lines return a FIELD READ bracket — the same institutional
bracket format introduced in E08 P2 and E09. The dash-placeholder format `—` marks an
absent field (not an absent being). Render as legible field-text in small plain
monospace-style lettering inside square brackets:

> [FIELD READ: circulation signature — GD-ANNEX-SUBLOT-7 / firmware layer: AEGIS-4 spec (Rev. 1, design reference, deprecated) / field delta: — / Appendix C: void — 0.000 Pa]

The `field delta: —` line uses the same dash-placeholder as E08's unreadable Primm
signature. Here it marks not an absent being but an absent field: the pressure-cavity
where a design parameter was removed. **Guard against filling the dash with a word.**
