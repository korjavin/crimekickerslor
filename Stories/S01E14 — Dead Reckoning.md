# S01E14 — Dead Reckoning

> **Logline:** Four powers, one building, one shoulder of luck — they get him out, and they do not yet know what the room they walked through did to two of them.

---

## Title

**"Dead Reckoning"** — navigation by known speed, heading, and elapsed time, without external reference points. A method for finding where you are when you cannot see landmarks. It is also a nautical idiom for acting on the best available information while the ground is uncertain. The rescue is exactly that: three people moving through a building they have partially mapped, on a timeline they are counting down, toward a position Pho-boman confirmed two days ago and which may have shifted. There is no real-time update. There is a route, a clock, and the math of how long 72 hours takes to run.

This two-signature contact is the cause E15 names when the resonance wakes as a constant, bidirectional signal ("source: not external"), so the count must read **two** on both sides of the seam.

> **Seam note (mechanic — already reconciled across E14/E15; flagged for author confidence):** The two-signature count depends on a *soft mechanic*: Windman never enters the building (his locked role is the exterior loading-dock distraction), so his fragment-signature reaches the sublevel-2 prep field only by *riding the compressed-air pulse he routes through the ductwork in Panel 2* — the same pulse that serves as his directional relay to Pho-boman. The incident-signature is canonically a property of a *remade body*, not of moved air, so "a signature carried on a pulse of air" is an extension of the mechanic, not a direct application of it. This is **already the ratified resolution** in [[S01E15 — Partial Contact]] (its "what the audience knows" states the same two-route mechanism, and the bidirectional resonance / "source: not external" payoff is built on it), so E14 and E15 now agree and the seam is internally consistent. It is surfaced here only so the author can confirm they are comfortable with the air-carries-signature extension; if not, the fix spans both files (downgrade to single-fragment contact in E14 *and* E15, with the bidirectional feeling justified by the two being halves of one body) and cannot be made in E14 alone.

> **Seam test for any future edit:** does this episode open with the three (not yet four) positioned for the rescue, confirm Pho-boman enters first and unseen, show Primm phasing out of the holding structure, and close with four figures together — while also showing the reintegration chamber on sublevel 2, the prep field running ambient, and the escalation auto-filed? If Primm escapes alone, if the chamber is not seen, if the escalation does not fire, or if the prep-field contact is made explicit to the heroes, the seam to E15 is broken.

## Format

## Tone targets

- **Panel 1:** wide, dry, establishing — three positions, one absent fourth, a precise clock. The humor is in the precision: fewer than four hours left of seventy-two. The warmth is in the third caption: Primm has been waiting longer than the others, and the narrator names this without sentiment.
- **Panel 2:** the episode's slapstick peak. Tiebi at the loading dock, macro form, HWUF and STOMP simultaneously, drones rerouting. The comedy is in the geometry — Tiebi solving the ceiling problem by deploying horizontally — and in "three drones had already rerouted; this was the correct number," the narrator confirming a plan that was never stated. Kinetic and exterior.
- **Panel 3:** quiet and institutional. Pho-boman in the corridor, drone present, drone blind, GD-SURVEY notation routing him to food-safety compliance. The joke is the drone's indifference reused in a new context. The straight line — "this had always been true and was now, for the first time, useful" — is the episode's best single caption.
- **Panel 4:** efficient and kinetic. One bolt, one noodle-rope, one step, the field returning. The DRIFT light-bend snapping back on is the visual payoff. SLURP and DRIFT landing quietly against the exterior's HWUF-STOMP.
- **Panel 5:** the coldest panel. The amber lamp. The shimmer. The narrator's three-line statement of what the protocol logged, with no editorial. This is where the episode earns its title: the route was correct, the heading was right, and what they navigated through is only visible as a notation in a staging-area log. The tone is procedural dread in the case-file narrator's flattest register.
- **Panel 6:** the warm close, with cold underneath. The four outside. The city ahead. The terminal behind them, too small to read from where they stand. "This was within spec" as the final beat. The belly-dragon visible at eye-level. Four colors in one frame, same as E08 Panel 4, but not kinetic — still, facing outward, the job completed.

## Differentiation / what's new

The full-cast ensemble action has happened before — E08 was the four-way convergence, the kinetic peak of the reunion arc. E14 is structurally similar in scope but inverted in almost every mechanical detail:

- E08 was accidental convergence on a problem nobody planned to share. E14 is deliberate coordination of a plan that was designed precisely.
- E08's four SFX fired simultaneously in one panel as a joy beat. E14's four SFX fire in sequence, in two registers — exterior and interior — and the interior pair is quieter.
- E08 succeeded at no cost the episode named. E14 succeeds at a cost the episode names only in the narrator's flattest register, and the heroes leave without knowing it was incurred.
- E08 ended on the recall notice in small type that nobody read. E14 ends on an escalation filing and a preliminary alignment log in small type that nobody reads. The parallel is deliberate and should be visible.

What is new here:
- The spatial relay: Windman's air-handling field-read functioning as directional guidance to Pho-boman inside the building, two heroes collaborating without being in the same physical space.
- The Tiebi geometry beat: macro form deployed along a horizontal axis rather than a vertical one, the ceiling limit managed rather than exploited.
- Primm's field returning as a visual event — the DRIFT light-bend snapping back on after two issues of absence.
- The staging-area panel: the cost beat delivered entirely in procedural terms, with the amber lamp and the "logging only" notation doing the work the narrator does not editorialize.
- The `[REDACTED PER COMPLIANCE PROTOCOL]` escalation: the first appearance of the vector that will resolve in E17 as AEGIS-5. Visible to the reader, invisible to the heroes, consistent with the series' established grammar for things in the frame that nobody is reading.
