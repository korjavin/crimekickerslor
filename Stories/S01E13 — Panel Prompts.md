# S01E13 — Panel Prompts

Image-generation prompts and episode-specific visual canon for
[[S01E13 — Field Notes]] (the handoff — the Junior Engineer walks up on a
city street and gives the team a notebook). Built by the **panel-prompt-smith**
agent.

> Each prompt is **self-contained** — image models have no memory, so every prompt
> restates the art style, every character in the frame, and every prop in full. The
> global art style and recurring descriptors live in [[Visual Style Guide]]; reuse
> the wordings below **verbatim** across panels for consistency.

This issue is **one comic page resolved across five images** (P1–P5). It is a
**low-kinetic handoff episode**: three free heroes (Windman, Tiebi, Pho-boman)
standing on an ordinary city street at midday, receiving a physical lab notebook
from a young civilian who was present at the AEGIS-4 incident. No powers fire.
No SFX deploy. The episode earns its stillness. The notebook — and what it
contains — is the episode's primary object. The drone net is absent from this
block; the Junior Engineer chose the location.

### Windman's pressure-perception overlay — ambient crowd-reading register

Per [[Visual Style Guide]] Airmaster pressure-perception overlay (RATIFIED 2026-06-15),
reuse the canonical wording verbatim wherever the overlay appears:

> a "pressure-map / ghost-line" perception overlay rendered in a DISTINCT visual
> register, clearly separate from the lit, visible scene: tighter finer ghost-line
> linework, slightly desaturated and tonally cooler, semi-transparent. In this overlay,
> objects are NOT drawn as normal lit visible objects — instead they appear as
> air-displacement silhouettes and concentric pressure-rings / contour-lines that trace
> their shape in moving air, like a blind man's sonar drawn in wind. This overlay is
> "what HE perceives," layered over or behind "what the camera and audience see"; the
> two layers must read as clearly different so the model does not simply draw a normal
> lit scene.

**E13 register note:** in this episode the overlay is at **low ambient range** —
the crowd-reading register, not the targeting register. Ghost-lines extend outward
at a short radius, softly tracing the mass-profiles of ordinary pedestrians in the
background and the approaching/present Junior Engineer. Concentric rings are
**looser and lighter** than the tight diagnostic rings used against a truck or in a
combat read; this is the register of someone who has catalogued the block and found
nothing alarming. The Junior Engineer reads as a single unguarded human mass with
elevated respiration — nothing else.

**FIELD READ bracket (P2 only):** render as legible field-text near Windman's
folded fan, in small plain monospace-style lettering inside square brackets:
`[FIELD READ: single human-mass (elevated respiration) — unaccompanied — no device mass above 450g]`
