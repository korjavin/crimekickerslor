# S01E04 — Captions

Print-ready narration captions for [[S01E04 — What Is a Ladle For]] — one panel-header per
panel, to letter onto the comic page beside each picture. **12 captions plus one SFX**
across five panels. Voice is dry case-file narrator, deadpan-existential: the same register
as E01–E03 but slower and warmer, the comedy of a being with no prior framework for anything.
This issue is caption-driven — every panel except the action beat (Panel 3) carries three
captions in a staircase rhythm. Panel 3 is SFX-only. Pairs with the art in
[[S01E04 — Panel Prompts]].

## Captions

**Panel 1 — Still In The Cauldron**
> He had been awake for approximately no minutes.
>
> He was in a cauldron. He was also, somehow, a cauldron.
>
> He had no thoughts about this yet. The thoughts were queuing.

**Panel 2 — The Ladle Test**
> The ladle had been used near him. Not on him. Near him.
>
> He could not determine why.
>
> He had begun to believe this was the main question.

**Panel 3 — SLURP**
*(no narration caption — SFX only: "SLURP—", thick lettering integrated into the jet.
The involuntary boiling-broth discharge and the very wide noodle-eyes carry the beat.
See lettering notes.)*

**Panel 4 — Contents Under Pressure**
> The broth had come out of him.
>
> It had, apparently, gone where he felt afraid.
>
> He filed this under: himself.

**Panel 5 — PHO-BOMAN**
> Pho-boman.

## Lettering notes

- Panels 1, 2, and 4 each carry **three captions in a descending staircase rhythm** —
  each line is shorter or lighter than the one above it. Let the staircase breathe; do not
  compress the three boxes into a tight stack.
- **Panel 3 carries no narration caption — SFX only.** The action is involuntary and
  instantaneous; a caption would puncture the beat. The SFX *SLURP—* is the canonical
  first deployment of Pho-boman's power sound. Letter it thick, integrated into the jet
  — not a box, not a label, part of the image.
- **Panel 5 caption: smallest and most offhand line in the issue.** Bottom-right, dry-
  corporate register. The name arrives by inventory sticker, not ceremony; the caption
  observes what is already true. Let it whisper, not announce — same treatment as E02's
  *"Windman."* and E03's *"T."*
- **Non-caption in-panel lettering** (locked in [[S01E04 — Panel Prompts]], not part of
  the narration track): Panel 1 — the handwritten masking-tape label on the cauldron reads
  **PHO**; Panel 5 — two labels side by side on the cauldron: the masking-tape label
  **PHO** and a small red inventory sticker **MAN**. These are image/art details; the
  letterer should treat them as background text embedded in the panel art, not as caption
  boxes.
- **Deliberate silence — do not fill Panel 3.** The Lunch Lady is wordless throughout
  this episode. No dialogue or word is given to her in any panel; do not add any.
- **Voice continuity:** the numeric-deadpan formula in Panel 1 ("approximately no minutes")
  is the same register as E02 Panel 1 ("approximately four minutes"), E03 Panel 1
  ("approximately six minutes"), and E05 Panel 1 ("approximately one second") — kept
  consistent so all four diaspora issues read as one show.
- **Open thread:** the belly-dragon emblem on Pho-boman's round belly must remain fully
  withheld in Panels 1–3 (the art in those panels hides it by angle, submersion, or
  partial obstruction) so that Panel 4's full-reveal lands. This is load-bearing for the
  letterer only in that no caption should describe or reference the emblem before Panel 4.
