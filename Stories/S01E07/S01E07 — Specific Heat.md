# S01E07 — Specific Heat

> **Logline:** A soup entity follows a smell into a corporate freezer and seals himself in. The one hero who comes after him is a gravity manipulator — and the freezer is the single room on Earth where every power he has stops dead at the wall. The exit is a meter and a half away, and he has to carry the soup to reach it.

---

## Title

**"Specific Heat"** — the thermodynamic property describing how much energy a substance requires to change temperature. Pho-boman has a very high specific heat (soup does not cool quickly, but when it does, it commits). Primm has a specific heat of zero (he does not change register regardless of circumstances). The term is dry, physics-adjacent, and fits the house title family without explanation: "Momentum Not Included" / "What Is a Ladle For?" / "Hold Your Own Weight" / "Two-Body Problem."

---

## Continuity / placement

This is **Issue 7, Act III, the second reunion episode.** It follows [[S01E06 — Two-Body Problem]] (Windman + Tiebi first meeting) and precedes [[S01E08]] (full four-way convergence). Neither Windman nor Tiebi appears here.

**Pho-boman** closed E04 riding the Lunch Lady's rolling food cart out the loading bay toward the street, looking forward, not back. He has been drifting the city since. He has been warm. The city has been various. By E07 he is the sort of being who follows a smell into a building without first asking what the building is.

**Primm** closed E05 descending the GastroDefense fire-stairwell toward street level — mini-orbs in tight orbit, posture exact, looking like a hero who always knew how to use stairs. He has been drifting the city since. He has not mentioned the falling to anyone, because there has been no one to mention it to. By E07 he is the sort of being who, finding an anomaly on a quiet dock, investigates it because anomalies get investigated.

The episode is **sequential, and the order is the point.** Pho-boman arrives first, alone, by scent. Primm arrives second, drawn off a different errand by the sound of a door doing something a door should not do at this hour. One of them walks into the trap; the other walks in after him. The city led them here by two entirely different routes, the same as ever — but this time the routes are a few minutes apart, and that gap is the whole plot.

> **Seam test for any future edit:** does this episode end with exactly two figures — Primm and Pho-boman — in the freezer doorway, facing the same outward direction, Pho-boman's broth resettled, Primm's mini-orbs in tight orbit, neither having named a team, no third hero appearing, GastroDefense named only as a logo and never as an active speaking antagonist? If one figure leaves alone, or if the team is named, or if a third hero appears, the Act III second-reunion beat is broken.

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page** (one page; standard Act III budget). Each panel is one image with tight captions and minimal dialogue. Iconography over blocking.

The visual space is a **single contained stage**: a GastroDefense Inc. logistics cold-storage loading dock and the walk-in industrial freezer behind it. The five panels bracket the room. **Panel 1 is exterior** — the small one walks into the trap. **Panel 2 is the threshold** — the second one seals himself in after him. **Panels 3–4 are inside** the freezer, eight by six meters of floor-to-ceiling steel racking — a room that turns out to neutralize one specific person completely. **Panel 5 is the threshold again** — both of them out. The palette is dark institutional grey-blue, frosted metal, cold vapor. Two warm color pops against the grey: Pho-boman's reddish-orange broth (always) and Primm's cyan visor-glow. The DRIFT light-bend appears but stops dead at steel surfaces rather than propagating; the visual grammar of the field being blocked is the episode's recurring VFX beat.

Captions remain the dry case-file narrator throughout — boxed, deadpan, observational, never editorial. The narrator's register in this issue runs two simultaneous gag clocks: Pho-boman's broth thickening described as a bureaucratic process, and Primm's power-failure listed as a surface inventory in the exact diagnostic register he established in E05 but arriving now at a different conclusion.

## Panels

### Panel 1 — One of Them Was Following a Smell

**Image:** Night. The loading dock of a GastroDefense Inc. cold-storage logistics warehouse — wide concrete apron, a parked refrigerated truck to one side with the GastroDefense logo visible on the cab door, cold vapor bleeding faintly from the truck's refrigeration unit. At the far end of the dock, the heavy insulated freezer door stands slightly ajar, propped open a careful few inches by a brick at its base; cold fog rolls faintly through the gap.

Foreground, the focus of the frame: Pho-boman's small spoon-shaped silhouette stepping **into** the gap — head cocked toward the cold, noodle-brows in the attentive configuration, drawn by the bloom of frozen-pho smell escaping the propped door. He is following the scent with his whole body, unhurried, nose-first, one flat black boot already across the threshold. That boot is about to clip the brick. The image catches the instant before: the brick at the base of the door, the small foot swinging past it, the whole trap latent in two objects that have not yet touched.

Deep background, small and peripheral and facing the wrong way: a tall purple silhouette at the far edge of the dock, near the truck, one hand held slightly forward into the faint light-bend of the DRIFT field — a gravity-sense reading the truck's refrigeration mass. His back is to the door. He has not registered the door, the fog, or the small figure stepping through it. He is forty feet and one errand away.

The brick is the smallest thing in the frame. It is also the most important, which the frame does not say.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *One of them was following a smell.*
- CAPTION: *One of them was reading a mass displacement anomaly in the refrigeration unit of a GastroDefense Inc. logistics truck, with his back to everything that mattered.*
- CAPTION: *The brick had been there for approximately four hours.*
- CAPTION: *It had not been consulted about the boot.*

**Why it lands:** The asymmetry is the new architecture of the episode, and it is established in one image. Pho-boman is the protagonist of this panel — sensory, forward-body, walking into the trap nose-first. Primm is present but peripheral, occupied, facing away: the diagnostic hero whose attention is on the technically interesting thing (the truck's mass) rather than the thing that matters (the small being and the door). The reader sees the whole causal chain before either character does — the brick, the boot, the self-closing door — which is the dramatic irony the rest of the issue pays off. The GastroDefense logo is atmosphere only. The two warm pops are present and small: the broth in the visor, the cyan of Primm's distant visor. The brick's "opinion about the boot" keeps the episode's voice — E05's railings had opinions, now bricks do — and makes the causality visible rather than coincidental: this trap has one cause, and it is a brick and a boot.

### Panel 2 — He Went In After It

**Image:** The threshold. We have jumped a beat: the door has already swung shut and re-opened. In the foreground, Primm stands at the now-open freezer door, gloved hand on the **exterior** handle — he has crossed the dock and worked the latch from outside, the only side that works. His head is angled into the cold interior, the diagnostic narrow focus from E05 Panel 3. The brick lies on the concrete by his boot, displaced, no longer doing its job.

Through the doorway, deeper in the frosted steel racking: Pho-boman, small, well past the threshold, **not coming back out.** He has gone toward the smell, into the cold, and stopped — his broth already shifting, the reddish-orange settling lower in the visor, the first stiffening. He is not at the door. He is at the back, by the frozen tubs, where the scent led him, and he has begun to slow.

Primm is mid-step across the threshold — one boot in, weight committing inward. He is going in after the warm thing that did not leave. Behind him, unheld, the heavy door has begun to swing closed again; the exterior latch is a hand's width from seating. The image holds the exact moment the help becomes the trap.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The door slammed.*
- CAPTION: *His field read a dense metallic mass where the manifest listed none — and a warm one, sealed behind it.*
- CAPTION: *He opened the door. The warm thing did not leave. It had gone deeper, after the smell.*
- CAPTION: *So he stepped in after it.*
- CAPTION: *The door had no handle on the inside.*

**Why it lands:** This is the panel the whole rewrite turns on, and it does three things at once. It supplies the causality the episode was missing — one being walked into the trap, the other walked in after him, and we watched both happen. It honors the diagnostic character: Primm does not feel a heroic pull, he reads a mass anomaly (a warm body sealed in a cold box is a wrong state), and he resolves wrong states. And it is the help — "so he stepped in after it" — said in the flattest possible register, with no word for help in it. The reader supplies the word. The final caption is the trap closing on the rescuer: there is no handle on the inside. The gravity manipulator who came to get the small thing out is now exactly as sealed in as the small thing. The door is silent set dressing; GastroDefense's box does what GastroDefense's box does.

### Panel 3 — He Filed This Under: A Problem

**Image:** Inside the freezer, the door shut behind them — the latch-lock visible in the closed position, a fact the image presents without editorial. Interior: floor-to-ceiling steel racking on all four walls and most floor space, commercial catering tubs of frozen broth on every shelf, frosted metal, ice-crystal bloom, breath-fog. Temperature: -18°C. The room is approximately eight by six meters of continuous steel mass.

Primm runs the whole inventory in one held survey. He stands one step from the nearest rack column, right hand flat against it — the DRIFT light-bend visible around his palm, ending **flat** at the steel, bouncing back, not propagating through. A second attempt at lower left: a boot pressed to the concrete floor, the light-bend ending the same way, because the floor has a steel subframe. The field's grazing edge sweeps the center of the room as he scans and incidentally washes over Pho-boman — registering him as a data point alongside the rack column, a warm 62-kilogram nothing the system has no category for. His mini-orbs orbit slightly wider than they should — running right but running fast, the one cue that something has changed.

At center: Pho-boman, broth now visibly thicker, body slightly hunched, leaning away from the nearest rack with the careful distance of a being that has learned something about metal and cold in combination. He has found the exact center of the available non-metal floor space and occupied it. His noodle-eyes have contracted toward each other; the cilantro nose is rigid.

Between two rack columns at the far end, visible in the background: a gap. One meter and a half wide, running from the center of the room to the exit door. It is the only non-steel corridor in the room. The image makes it available without pointing to it; the reader sees it before Primm does.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The broth had opinions about -18°C. The opinions were accumulating.*
- CAPTION: *He tried the wall.*
- CAPTION: *The rack was twelve centimeters of steel.*
- CAPTION: *[FIELD READ: NO DETECTABLE SIGNATURE (warm, 62 kg, broth-type)]*
- CAPTION: *He tried the floor. Concrete over a steel subframe.*
- CAPTION: *He tried phasing. He tried a blink. Every vector passed through steel.*
- CAPTION: *Metal took the field, the phase, and the blink. One rule, not three.*
- CAPTION: *He filed this under: a problem.*
- CAPTION: *The broth was beginning to queue in a way that concerned no one but the broth.*

**Why it lands:** This is the single inventory panel — the entire steel-cage neutralization compressed to one staircase instead of spread across two slow surveys. The caption column runs both diagnostic clocks at once: Pho-boman's thickening broth (bureaucratic symptom-accumulation) and Primm's surface-failure (the exact diagnostic register from E05, arriving now at a different conclusion). The FIELD READ is the issue's funniest line — the field grazes Pho-boman mid-scan and returns a readout that is clinical and completely absurd; "broth-type" is not a category Primm has a framework for, so he files it and continues the inventory. "Metal took the field, the phase, and the blink. One rule, not three." answers the only real continuity question the room raises — why steel stops his phase and his blink when plaster did not in E05 — by naming the vulnerability as a single rule: metal grounds all three. And the panel mirrors E05 Panel 2's architecture-opinion captions, but inverted: in E05 the building's opinions were the building's problem; here Primm is the one running the inventory, the competent-on-purpose hero listing surfaces that disagree with him and arriving, in his flattest voice, at "a problem."

### Panel 4 — The Face Goes Away For A Moment

**Image:** Close-wide. This is the twist panel, the emotional beat, and the cooperative act in one frame.

Primm has crossed to Pho-boman and placed one gloved hand — silver-tipped fingers, the same hand that has been pressing against steel walls — against the side of the diving helmet. His palm is pressed across the visor face, covering the open gap: hand flat, fingers spread, the black glove sealing the opening. The DRIFT field fires from that point of contact: the light-bend distortion wraps Pho-boman's entire body in a single contained field, enveloping him from helmet to boots. Not the walls. Not the floor. Pho-boman. This is an established capability — objects within arm's reach — applied to a new target, not a discovery. Primm's expression does not change register. He made a calculation; this is the result.

Inside the transparent visor, visible to the reader through the visor's glass above and around Primm's covering palm: the broth goes weightless. The reddish-orange lifts free of the visor's lower bowl. The noodle-eyes drift apart from their positions, each one floating independent. The cilantro nose rises from center and hovers. The face — the whole dynamic construction that is Pho-boman's face — dissolves into a cloud of floating soup particles and suspended noodles, dispersed across the interior of the helmet in zero gravity. Primm's palm across the visor face keeps everything inside; nothing escapes outward. But inside the helmet: no face. A face that is not yet a face, suspended.

Pho-boman's arms float slightly upward. His boots are no longer planted — he has become effectively weightless, a small figure held in Primm's grip at the helmet, feet barely above the floor. He is not panicking. He is, if anything, experiencing the mild bewilderment of a being that files things. This is a new thing. He will file it.

Primm looks at the dissolved face through the visor, his palm still flat against the glass. The mini-orbs are in tight orbit — not erratic, tight, the way they were at E05 Panel 5 when he had the answer. He has not moved. He is holding a weightless soup entity by the helmet in an industrial freezer, and he has gone still for exactly one beat too long before the run.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- SFX (quiet, integrated into the light-bend field wrapping Pho-boman's body, smaller lettering than the SLURP that follows): *DRIFT—*
- CAPTION: *The broth went weightless.*
- CAPTION: *The face went away for a moment.*
- CAPTION: *He looked at it.*
- CAPTION: *It felt like a word in a language he had not yet learned.*

**Why it lands:** The zero-g dissolved face is made possible by Primm's gloved palm covering the open visor gap — the same hand that has been pressing against steel walls all episode now physically seals the one opening the broth would otherwise escape through. This requires no new anatomy; his hand is the seal. The warmth of the broth stays interior and the dissolved-face image stays contained where it can be read clearly against the cold grey freezer.

The emotional beat is positioned with precision. Primm reacts to the *visual image* of the dissolved face — a face that is not yet settled, a being temporarily without edges — and the caption names his internal response as a linguistic gap, not a physics detection. "A word in a language he had not yet learned" is not the incident-signature that Windman felt in Tiebi's pressure-anomaly in E06. There is no uncanny-familiar flicker, no echo of the shared mark — Pho-boman was born of the broth, not remade by the fracture, and Primm's mass-field has already confirmed this (the FIELD READ in P3 returned broth-type, not resonance). What Primm experiences is not recognition. It is rhyme. He has also been, briefly, a self without edges — one piece of a fractured thing, inherited into a body he did not choose — and the image of a face that dissolves into its components and waits, unhurried, for gravity to come back, rhymes with something he has no word for. The captions do not explain this. The panel holds the image and the stillness and lets the reader do the rest.

Pho-boman experiences nothing that corresponds to this on his side. He is waiting, weightless, for whatever happens next. He will file it.

### Panel 5 — That Was The Whole Plan

**Image:** Overhead-and-slightly-behind register — the same compositional grammar established in E06 Panel 6's two-figure ending. A wide image of the freezer's far end: the gap between two rack columns (one meter and a half of non-steel corridor running from center to the exit door), and through that gap, the exit door, now forced open, cold vapor flooding outward through it onto the loading dock beyond.

The action has already happened. We catch the frame at the threshold: Primm is in the doorway, Pho-boman tucked under his left arm (still under the DRIFT field, feet barely touching, weight effectively nullified — a tactical choice, not a discovery; he needed to carry the mass he could carry through the gap he could use). They are crossing the threshold. As they do: gravity returns to Pho-boman. The DRIFT field ends at the doorframe.

Inside the helmet, the broth crashes back down into the visor's lower bowl — and as it does:

**SFX** (bold, warm, the episode's loudest sound, in contrast to the freezer's silence): *SLURP—*

The face comes back. The noodle-eyes drop into position. The cilantro nose falls back to center. The reddish-orange blooms at the visor's lower rim, fluid again. The broth has settled.

Both figures are in the doorway, half-in and half-out, cold vapor dissipating around them. They face the same direction: outward, toward the loading dock, toward the city. Primm's mini-orbs are in tight orbit. Pho-boman's visor-face is back and upright. Neither is looking at the other. The freezer is behind them. They are not looking at it.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The gap between the racks was a meter and a half.*
- CAPTION: *He ran it.*
- CAPTION: *He hit the door with his shoulder.*
- CAPTION: *That was the whole plan.*
- CAPTION: *Outside, the broth settled.*
- CAPTION: *The face came back.*
- CAPTION: *Neither of them said anything about it.*

**Why it lands:** The comedy payoff is purely geometric: the great tactical effort of DRIFT-on-a-body plus a full sprint covers one meter and a half and ends with a shoulder. The gravity manipulator's solution to a complete power-neutralization is to run and hit a door — and the door opens not because of any power but because his own weight came back at the threshold and a self-closing cam-latch is no match for a running body. The escape uses the one thing the room could not take from him: his mass, restored the instant the field stops mattering. The captions deliver this with the same flat inventory register Primm used for his wall-testing in P3 — "he ran it / he hit the door with his shoulder / that was the whole plan" — and the compression is the joke. There is no triumph. There is a door that opened. That is what doors do.

The final three captions shift register slightly — not to sentiment, but to the narrator noting what has been restored and what neither of them has said. "Neither of them said anything about it" covers everything: the dissolved face, the beat too long, the broth-queue, the meter and a half of heroism. The house rule holds: neither of them said the word "team." Neither named the other. They are in a doorway, facing outward, same direction. The series grammar established in E06 Panel 6 — two figures, not one, facing the same direction outward — is honored and continued in its own form. Every diaspora episode ended on one figure walking alone, away. Both reunion episodes end on two figures at a threshold, facing the same direction, not walking away.

The SLURP at the threshold is the episode's warmest beat and its loudest sound, which is correct: the soup is back, the face is back, and the cold is behind them.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- Primm is one of two AEGIS-4 fragments. Pho-boman is not a fragment — he is a wholly new being born when pho broth filled the prototype's diving helmet at the moment of fracture. The incident-signature that Windman detected in Tiebi in E06 — the shared mark the AEGIS-4 fracture left on the bodies it remade — does not exist between this pair: Pho-boman was *created* by the incident, not *remade* by it, so he carries none of it. Primm's gravity field has already confirmed this: FIELD READ returns broth-type, not resonance. There is no signal to decode.
- Primm came in after Pho-boman — that is as close to "help" as his register permits. He read a warm mass sealed in a cold box, registered a wrong state, and went to resolve it; he did not decide to rescue anyone. The audience reads the act as rescue. Primm would file it under: an exit problem he then had to solve twice, once for the body he was carrying. The help is real and it is practical; it is never sentimental, which is exactly why the emotional beat in P4 lands one-directionally.
- What Primm felt in P4 — the beat-too-long stillness, the dissolved face, the word in a language not yet learned — is not recognition. It is structural rhyme. He was a self without edges once, in a different way, for different reasons (fracture, not dissolution). The image of a face that temporarily is not there rhymes with something he carries about a body he did not choose. He has no vocabulary for this. The audience has the vocabulary and can see it. He cannot.
- Pho-boman has no corresponding internal experience during the zero-g moment. He was waiting, weightless, for whatever happened next. He has filed it under: that happened. He is not missing information. He is complete.
- GastroDefense Inc. has logged all four as "Recalled." Two of them just exited one of their logistics freezers via a shoulder-barge. The corporation does not know. The brick's original purpose — propping the door — was someone's logistics error, not a plan. The freezer having no interior handle was also not a plan; it was a non-compliant box in a corporation that does not audit its own boxes. The captions did not investigate who left the brick, and neither will the corporation.
- The cooperation that just occurred — DRIFT on a body, one corridor, a shoulder — was Primm solving an exit problem he had walked into on purpose, and it happened to defuse Pho-boman's cold crisis along the way. The help was structural even though the entry was chosen. The audience can see what this looks like when it becomes fully deliberate and mutual. Neither of them can see that yet.
- Primm will not mention the freezer. He will not mention the walls, or the inventory of surfaces, or the meter and a half, or that he went in after a smell-following soup on purpose. These are private facts. He is, constitutionally, not going to bring them up.

## Differentiation / what's new

E06 is the first reunion. E07 is the second. They must not be the same episode.

**E06's meeting mechanic** was uncanny-familiar: Windman detected Tiebi as an anomaly in his pressure-field — a mass-signature that "did not stay one size" — and the dramatic irony was that both of them felt something they could not name, because both were remade by the same incident — Windman split from the prototype in it, Tiebi transformed where he stood — and carry the same faint mark of it. The emotional core was mutual and symmetric, even if neither could articulate it.

**E07's meeting mechanic** is the structural negative of that, and the new structure sharpens it. There is no shared signal. Pho-boman is sealed in first; Primm comes in after him; Primm's field reads Pho-boman as broth-type — 62 kg, warm, no detectable signature — and finds none of the mark the fracture left on the bodies it remade. Pho-boman does not scan: he was born of the broth, an entity the incident *created* rather than *remade*, so he carries no echo of it. He is born-complete; there is no recognition to be had. The remade-versus-born asymmetry means the emotional beat, when it arrives, is entirely one-directional: it lives in what Primm sees in a dissolved face, not in what either of them detects in the other.

**E06's cooperation** was complementary-by-design: Windman sensed, Tiebi acted, they were accidentally a sensor-actuator pair. The cooperation had the shape of two powers fitting together.

**E07's cooperation is the inverse — a defeated rescuer.** Primm walks in *on purpose* to get the small freezing thing out, and the room then takes every tool he carries: the field, the phase, the blink, all grounded by steel. The one being who came prepared to solve the room is the one being the room is built to neutralize completely. He wins anyway — not because his powers fit Pho-boman's, but because warm broth weighs sixty-two kilograms he can briefly carry, his own legs still work, and the corridor is exactly wide enough. The cooperation has the shape of a helping instinct outliving every power that was supposed to serve it, and succeeding by the dumbest available means.

**E06 ended on dialogue** — Tiebi and Windman's first words to each other, flat and precise. The comedy was in what was said and not said.

**E07 ends in silence** — "neither of them said anything about it." The comedy is in the distance. The meter and a half. The shoulder.

The slapstick scales up: E06 had one street-level incident (a runner, a gust, a momentum-roll). E07 has a complete power-neutralization inside a steel cage, with a rescuer who sealed himself in to attempt it. But the lonely-funny register of the diaspora arc still hums underneath both reunion episodes — these are not yet people who have found each other; they are people one of whom went in after the other and then had to be carried out of the consequences. They are in the doorway. They are facing the same direction. That is what the series has.
