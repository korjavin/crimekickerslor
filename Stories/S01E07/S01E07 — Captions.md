# S01E07 — Captions

Print-ready narration captions for [[S01E07 — Specific Heat]] — to letter onto the comic page beside each picture. 5 panels, 29 caption boxes plus two SFX. The driest issue in the series: near-wordless by design, with no direct dialogue between characters. Voice is the same case-file narrator as E01–E06. The episode is sequential — one hero follows a smell into the trap (P1), the other steps in after him and seals himself in too (P2) — and from P3 the narrator runs two simultaneous bureaucratic clocks: Pho-boman's broth thickening and Primm's surface inventory, interleaved in a single staircase without either clock addressing the other. The FIELD READ caption in Panel 3 and "that was the whole plan" in Panel 5 are the tonal peaks. Pairs with the art in [[S01E07 — Panel Prompts]].

## Captions

**Panel 1 — One of Them Was Following a Smell**
> One of them was following a smell.
>
> One of them was reading a mass displacement anomaly in the refrigeration unit of a GastroDefense Inc. logistics truck, with his back to everything that mattered.
>
> The brick had been there for approximately four hours.
>
> It had not been consulted about the boot.

**Panel 2 — He Went In After It**
> The door slammed.
>
> His field read a dense metallic mass where the manifest listed none — and a warm one, sealed behind it.
>
> He opened the door. The warm thing did not leave. It had gone deeper, after the smell.
>
> So he stepped in after it.
>
> The door had no handle on the inside.

**Panel 3 — He Filed This Under: A Problem**
> The broth had opinions about -18°C. The opinions were accumulating.
>
> He tried the wall.
>
> The rack was twelve centimeters of steel.
>
> [FIELD READ: NO DETECTABLE SIGNATURE (warm, 62 kg, broth-type)]
>
> He tried the floor. Concrete over a steel subframe.
>
> He tried phasing. He tried a blink. Every vector passed through steel.
>
> Metal took the field, the phase, and the blink. One rule, not three.
>
> He filed this under: a problem.
>
> The broth was beginning to queue in a way that concerned no one but the broth.

**Panel 4 — The Face Goes Away For A Moment**
> *(SFX, quiet, integrated into the light-bend field wrapping Pho-boman's body — smaller lettering than the SLURP that follows: DRIFT—)*
>
> The broth went weightless.
>
> The face went away for a moment.
>
> He looked at it.
>

**Panel 5 — That Was The Whole Plan**

## Lettering notes

- **SFX — DRIFT** (Panel 4): Primm's canonical SFX. Small and quiet here — integrated into the light-bend distortion that wraps Pho-boman's body. This is not the full field deployment; it is a contained, precise application. Do not letter it large. The SLURP that follows is the episode's contrast point.
- **SFX — SLURP** (Panel 5): Pho-boman's canonical SFX. The episode's loudest and warmest beat, lettered in contrast to the near-silence of the freezer across Panels 2–4. Bold, warm register. It lands at the threshold as gravity returns and the face reconstitutes — the punctuation of the exit. Let it breathe. SLURP fires **only here** — it is the one big sound in the issue, so resist lettering any soup sound earlier (the noise that draws Primm in Panel 2 is the metallic slam of the door, not a SLURP).
- **FIELD READ caption** (Panel 3): this line has a distinct typographic register — it is Primm's field returning data, formatted as a diagnostic readout. The brackets and all-caps label are intentional. Set it differently from the standard case-file caption boxes: monospace or scan-register lettering to distinguish the machine-voice from the narrator. The content — "(warm, 62 kg, broth-type)" — is the issue's tonal peak; the lettering style should let the absurdity of "broth-type" land without editorializing.
- **"So he stepped in after it"** (Panel 2): the quietest load-bearing line in the issue. It is the help, said in the flattest possible register — no warm word in it. Do not inflate it, do not italicize it for emphasis. Let it sit in the staircase exactly as level as the lines around it; the reader supplies the warmth the narrator withholds.
- **Caption staircase rhythm** (Panel 3): two interleaved clocks running without ever addressing each other — Pho-boman's broth-thickening and Primm's surface-inventory share the column in alternation. Do not let them merge visually; they are two separate trains of thought. If the lettering layout permits, a subtle spacing break between the broth-clock lines and the Primm-inventory lines helps the reader track which clock is ticking.
- **"He filed this under: a problem"** (Panel 3): the narrator button for Primm's register. Flat, diagnostic, not ironic in tone even though it is ironic in content. Same register as the inventory lines above it — do not inflate it.
- **"He hit the door with his shoulder"** (Panel 5): the comedy payoff. The gravity manipulator's escape from a sealed steel room is a shoulder, because his own weight is the one thing the room could not take from him. Letter it as flat as the inventory lines — the deadpan is the joke; the "no handle on the inside" from Panel 2 already supplies the reason.
- **"Neither of them said anything about it"** (Panel 5): the closing button, quietest possible register. It covers the dissolved face, the beat-too-long stillness, the broth-queue, and the meter and a half of heroism. Same principle as E06's "Neither of them said the word 'team.'" — name the absence, not the presence. Let it whisper.
- **No direct dialogue in this issue.** E07 is the silent counterpart to E06's first dialogue. Neither Primm nor Pho-boman speaks. All text is narration captions, one FIELD READ readout, and two SFX.
- **Deliberate near-silence throughout.** The freezer is quiet for four panels. The door-slam (Panel 2) and the SLURP (Panel 5) are the only sounds that break it. Resist any impulse to add ambient SFX to the freezer interior — the silence is the episode's texture.
- **Open thread:** the FIELD READ caption is the series' first instance of a machine-register caption (as opposed to the case-file narrator). The lettering style for it is unresolved — flag for the author whether it stays within the standard caption box with a typographic shift, or sits in a distinct box shape (scan-panel / HUD register).
