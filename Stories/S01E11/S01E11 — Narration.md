# S01E11 — Narration (Voiceover)

Spoken-word narration voiceover for [[S01E11 — Closed Loop]], written for ElevenLabs
text-to-speech to play as background narration over a slideshow / motion-comic of the five
panels (see [[S01E11 — Panel Prompts]]). Written for the ear. Dry case-file narrator — the
same voice family as prior episodes. This is the decode episode: three survivors read the
corporation's active case file and learn three things — the recall is liability management,
the corporation has already named them, and the held one has time. The narrator carries the
case in the same flat tone the corporation files it in. ~225 words, ~95 seconds at 150 wpm.

Note: E11 has minimal **dialogue** — Tiebi speaks three short lines on the page as speech
balloons ("CRIME KICKERS." / "Not revenge." / "Undelivered merchandise."). Those balloons
are on-page lettering, not the narration track; the narrator conveys the same facts in the
narrator's own voice. Do NOT also voice the balloons. Windman and Pho-boman have no lines.
The on-screen institutional text — the HELPDESK buttons, the case-file header fields, the
unit roster (including *P-02 — DATA NOT PRESENT*), the contract section, and the
*SESSION: ACTIVE — ACCESS LOGGED* line — is in-panel institutional lettering; do not voice
it as narration.

## Clean script (paste into ElevenLabs)

> Everything in this block is spoken text only — no labels, nothing that shouldn't be
> voiced. Paste as-is.

Three of them, now. The fourth was somewhere that was not the city.

Tiebi still had his credentials.

The system had not reviewed them in fourteen months. The system had not reviewed much of anything.

The case file loaded in eleven seconds. It had a header field for operational designations. The field was filled — the database had needed a name, drawn one from the incident log, and filed it between two other lines.

Crime Kickers.

He read it the way a case number is read. They absorbed it, and used it.

The file could account for three of them. Where the fourth should have been, it returned no data. Pho-boman, who cannot read, pointed at the empty line. The document naming them could not see him standing in front of it.

Below the roster, a hold. Reintegration required both halves of the original body, and the division had one. While the other stayed outside, the held one had time.

Windman did not read this. He felt it — a cold, flat blank where something used to answer. He had no word for it.

Then the contract.

It was not revenge. It was undelivered merchandise.

The corporation was not hunting them. It was closing a ticket. Budget: standard.

They stepped back into the city. Behind them, the file noted that it had been read, and quietly opened a smaller file about that.

They called themselves the Crime Kickers now.

## Runtime

- **Word count:** ~225 words.
- **Runtime:** ~90 s at 150 wpm; ~95 s at 140 wpm. With the tuned pauses — the held beat
  after "Crime Kickers." and the break after "He had no word for it." — the track plays
  nearer **~100–105 s**. E11 is the densest narration of the run so far; let the pauses do
  the work rather than rushing the lines.
- "Crime Kickers." carries a held beat by design — but it is the held beat of a field value
  landing, not a title card. Flat, small, then move on. Do not let it swell.
- "They called themselves the Crime Kickers now." is the last line. The closing break is
  absent by design — the track ends on that phrase, the name filed and in use, no air after.

## ElevenLabs delivery notes

- **Voice character:** the same dry, mid-to-low documentary narrator as [[S01E01 — Narration]]
  through [[S01E10 — Narration]] — deadpan, unhurried, flat affect. The dryness is uniform.
- **"Crime Kickers." — the name, not a reveal:** the single most important delivery note.
  The name must NOT land as a triumphant beat. Read it the way you'd read a case number off
  a form — flat, small, slightly under the line before it. The whole point is that the team
  inherits its name bureaucratically; the voice must not editorialize the name into a moment.
- **"It was not revenge. / It was undelivered merchandise." — the twist:** delivered in the
  flattest register in the track. Do not punch it. The comedy and the dread both live in how
  ordinary it sounds — the menace is a budget line, not a villain. "Budget: standard." is the
  cold button; small and done.
- **"He had no word for it." — the loss:** flat, not grief. Windman is registering an absence
  his vocabulary does not have. The contrast lives in the words and the silence, not the voice.
- **The bureaucracy joke ("The system had not reviewed much of anything."):** dry, no wink.
  It is funniest delivered as a straight factual aside.
- **What not to voice:** Tiebi's three speech balloons ("CRIME KICKERS." / "Not revenge." /
  "Undelivered merchandise.") are on-page lettering — the narrator conveys the same facts, so
  do NOT also voice the balloons. All in-panel institutional text must NOT be narrated — the
  HELPDESK buttons, the case-file header fields, the unit roster (including *P-02 — DATA NOT
  PRESENT*), the contract section, and the *SESSION: ACTIVE — ACCESS LOGGED* line. None of
  these appear in the clean script above.
- **Pacing:** the two slowest moments are "Crime Kickers." (Panel 3 — the name landing as a
  field value) and "He had no word for it." (Panel 3 — the gap). Let both sit. Everything
  else moves at the series' standard unhurried pace.
- **Settings:** expressiveness ~30–40%, stability ~70%. Deadpan is the performance.
- **Voice archetypes to audition:** match the voice chosen for prior episodes — "George,"
  "Harry," "Arnold," or series-established equivalent. The narrator is one person across the
  series. Avoid enthusiastic / warm / alarmed / dramatic.
