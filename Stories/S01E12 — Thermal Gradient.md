# S01E12 — Thermal Gradient

> **Logline:** The one the sensors can't see walks into the building that wants them all, and comes back with a location and a clock.

---

## Title

**"Thermal Gradient"** — the temperature differential between two zones that drives a measurable flow of heat. Here it is literal (Pho-boman navigating cold-storage adjacencies inside GastroDefense HQ while routing toward warm corridors) and structural: information moves from inside to outside, from the corporation's database to the team's working knowledge, through the only channel the drone net cannot block. The gradient is the difference between what the building knows and what the three outside don't. Pho-boman is the medium by which that difference resolves.

One of them can walk into the building and come back out.

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page**. The solo-infiltration register is quieter than the four-body convergences of E08 and the corporate-parallel structure of E09; five panels is the correct budget for a solo run with one handoff. The episode has two visual registers that never share a panel: the **outside** (three figures at a street-level remove from GastroDefense HQ, night, waiting) and the **inside** (warm institutional corridors, cold-storage adjacencies, a staging-area terminal). Pho-boman carries both registers in his body — reddish-orange warmth inside a building that is all cold fluorescent.

The visual space is **GastroDefense HQ** and its immediate exterior — not the district substation from E09 but the main operational building, larger, more institutional, with a food-safety drone fleet perimeter and a loading-dock approach. The building does not monologue. It is a building with a drone perimeter and an unsecured staging-area terminal.

Captions are the dry case-file narrator throughout. Dialogue is minimal and flat. The comedy register is operational — the building does not know Pho-boman is inside because it does not have a category for him; the corporation's apparatus runs around him exactly as designed, finding nothing where a being is walking.

> **Visual production:** image-generation prompts for each panel to be built against the [[Visual Style Guide]] by the panel-prompt-smith agent.

## Panels

### Panel 1 — Perimeter Read

**Image:** Exterior, night. The three figures at a street-level remove from GastroDefense HQ — a wide, low-angle shot that frames the building's approach zone: a loading-dock forecourt, a chain-link perimeter fence, institutional lighting from wall-mounted floods, cold vapor drifting from an industrial vent on the building's lower face. The building is large and administrative in the institutional style — cinder-block and glass-panel upper floors, the loading dock a wide roll-door on the lower face, drone-parking housings mounted at the roof edge.

Two GD-SURVEY drones are visible in the mid-air above the forecourt, running their sweep in opposite directions — the overlap of their patrol arcs forms a visible criss-cross above the approach zone. Their sensor arrays point downward. Their callsigns on housing: *GD-SURVEY-PLAT-01* and *GD-SURVEY-DESSERT-02.* They are conducting their standard combined ambient-temperature-plus-asset-recovery sweep. The sweep is finding nothing of note in the forecourt.

The three figures are on the far side of the fence, in shadow: Windman slightly forward, fan open, blue pressure ghost-lines mapping the building's mass profile and the drone arcs through the air — he has been reading the sweep pattern for eleven minutes. Tiebi to his left, beret straight, one hand at his side, the other gesturing forward to trace a corridor layout in the air — the layout he carried in memory from two years of badge-in mornings. Pho-boman between them, small and spoon-shaped against the fence mesh, noodle-brows in the focused-attention configuration. Broth warm and visible in the visor. Dragon-belly forward. He is looking at the gap between the two drone arcs.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The building had a perimeter drone sweep on an eleven-minute rotation.*
- CAPTION (below): *One of them knew the interior floor plan. One of them could read the sweep pattern in the air.*
- CAPTION (below): *One of them the sweep could not find.*
- TIEBI (flat, tracing the air): *Sublevel access is through the freight junction. Cold storage is on your left going down. Stay right.*

**Why it lands:** The establishing panel delivers the operational problem and its solution in a single frame without exposition: the building, the drone net, and the one physical fact that makes the plan work — the gap between the arcs, which Pho-boman is already looking at. Tiebi's line is not a briefing; it is the floor-plan fragment from a man who walked the route three hundred times, delivered with no ceremony. The panel plants the cold-storage adjacency as a routing constraint — "stay right" is both an instruction and a concession to Pho-boman's vulnerability — without the episode making the vulnerability into drama. The cold vapor from the building's lower vent is visible in the frame and tells the audience everything it needs to know about sublevel 3's environment before Pho-boman is inside.

The arcs are visible above him in faint ghost-line register — not his ghost-lines, but the visual grammar the series has established for sensor sweeps. ### Panel 3 — Sublevel 3 (Routing Constraint)

**Image:** Interior. A wide corridor on sublevel 3 — institutional ceiling, bare concrete, wall-mounted cable conduit running at head height, strip lighting in recessed ceiling mounts. The corridor branches at a T-junction ahead. To the left at the junction: a heavy insulated door with a cold-storage seal, frost on the door's lower gasket, condensation shimmer in the air at the door's perimeter. A small placard in institutional type: *COLD STORAGE — SUBLEVEL 3 / FACILITY COMPLIANCE ZONE C.* To the right at the junction: a freight corridor, wider, no cold seal, a fork-lift charging bay visible at the end.

Pho-boman is at the junction, mid-frame, already committed to the right branch. He has not paused at the junction; the choice was made before he reached it, because he knew from Tiebi's floor-plan instruction which side was warm and which was cold. His body is turned right, the left-branch cold-storage door at his extreme left in the frame, the frost on the gasket at exactly the distance he has calculated as comfortable. His noodle-brows are in a configuration of deliberate attention — the look of a being who has noted something on his left and chosen not to interact with it.

The dragon-belly is fully visible from this interior angle — the reddish-orange glow of the broth the only warm color in the frame; the corridor is cold-white institutional, the cable conduit is grey, the insulated door is bone-white with a frost halo.

At the far end of the right corridor, half-visible in the dim freight-bay light: the steel-frame containment unit — a large industrial cage structure, bolted to the floor, floor-to-ceiling steel bar framing on four sides. A standard industrial containment unit for heavy equipment. Its mass is significant; Pho-boman's broth registers it as a dense-steel object in his scent-field. He has not reached it yet. He is looking at the staging-area terminal mounted on the freight-bay wall to its left — an asset-tracking screen, on and unattended.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The sublevel 3 corridor had two directions.*
- CAPTION (below): *He had been told which one.*
- CAPTION (below): *The other one was cold.*

**Why it lands:** The routing constraint lands as the driest possible comedy — the junction, the cold-storage door, and Pho-boman's path already chosen before the panel begins. "He had been told which one. / The other one was cold." is the series' operating register for Pho-boman's vulnerability: not a trap, not a crisis, a constraint he has already managed into the route plan before the scene starts. The panel simultaneously plants the episode's two key objects: the steel-frame containment unit at the far end of the corridor (Primm, absent-but-present as a structure) and the staging-area terminal (the intelligence payload). The containment unit is not labeled; it is recognized by its industrial mass and the reader's knowledge that this is where Primm is held. Pho-boman does not need to approach it. He needs the terminal to its left.

### Panel 5 — Outside, With a Clock

Pho-boman is through the fence, crossing the final meter of forecourt shadow back to where Windman and Tiebi wait. He is walking at Pho-boman speed. The drone arc overhead has completed another rotation — the two perimeter drones are at the far ends of their sweep paths, neither pointed at this corner of the forecourt.

The three figures come into a loose cluster at the fence line, the closest spacing of this episode. Windman is exactly as left: fan folded, bandaged face forward, pressure ghost-lines having tracked Pho-boman's warm mass signature through the building's ground floor and back. He has been reading the interior from outside, the way he reads a space — by the mass moving through it. He has a general picture; he needs the specific. Tiebi is to his right, beret straight, mustard-gold shirt catching the peripheral flood-light. His expression is the expression of a man who has been standing still for eleven minutes on the outside of a building he used to have a badge for.

Pho-boman faces them both. His noodle-brows are in a configuration not established before in this Movement — not the existential-weight configuration of E09's Section 4.4, not the focused-operational configuration of the infiltration. Something between: a being who has walked through the building that has him filed for disposal, read a screen that listed the fragment he came for and his own unit code in a cross-referenced sub-record, and walked out again with the information he went in for. **PHO-BOMAN:** *Sublevel three. Steel containment. Reintegration not started. Transfer in seventy-two hours.*

The final caption — "Seventy-two hours was a number they could work with" — is the series' narrator doing what the series' narrator does: naming the minimum true thing in the driest register possible, which here doubles as a rescue clock starting.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- Pho-boman walked through the same building whose specification document lists him for disposal under GD-Standard-7, and the building could not find him. The system that wants to dispose of him is unable to locate him because of the property that also makes him "non-specification" — he carries no incident-signature, so the incident-signature-based asset-recovery cross-reference returns null. The corporation's most important method of finding him is also the reason it cannot.
- The staging-area terminal listed CATERING-004A in the same sub-record as Pho-boman's unit code (P-02), cross-referencing the origin incident. Pho-boman read the screen. He did not speak the reference aloud. He kept it in order with the other facts.
- The GD-SURVEY food-safety compliance queue will, at some point, produce a complete exception report for the 94°C mobile thermal anomaly in Grid-F. That report will eventually reach a reviewer. The loop counter is at 218. It continues.
- The transfer clock — 72 hours from 03:14:07 — is a database field in an automated scheduling queue. It will not wait. It does not know the three outside are counting.

## Differentiation / what's new

The diaspora issues (E02–E05) each put one hero alone with one problem of self-discovery. E08 put all four together on one problem of convergence. E12 puts one hero alone inside an enemy structure — the first time the series runs a solo-inside-the-apparatus beat.

What is new here:
- **The tactical-infiltration register.** Every prior Pho-boman episode has been about what he is: E04 explored his powers; E07 established his asymmetry (born-complete, no incident-signature). E12 is the first episode where what he *is* becomes operationally decisive. The liability (no incident-signature = disposal-flagged) is now the asset (no incident-signature = invisible to the asset-recovery cross-reference). The series' most existentially burdened hero is the team's only viable infiltrator.
- **The longest Pho-boman dialogue in the run** — four sentences, delivered as a numbered list. The contrast between the weight of what he has just done and the register he uses to report it is the episode's emotional center.
- **Primm as mass and structure rather than character.** The containment unit at sublevel 3 is Primm's presence in this episode — a dense-steel object at the far end of Pho-boman's corridor, not approached, registered by scent-field, not spoken of. The episode keeps him absent while confirming he is real and located.
- **The food-safety compliance loop as running gag across a whole episode.** Introduced at iteration 1 in Panel 2, it is at 218 by Panel 5 — the corporation's apparatus running the wrong process correctly, for the entire duration of the infiltration, because the process design has no branch for a 94°C entity without a registration number.
