# S01E12 — Thermal Gradient

> **Logline:** The one the sensors can't see walks into the building that wants them all, and comes back with a location and a clock.

> **Stage:** Broad-strokes outline. Synopsis, continuity, and threads are locked to the [[Series Arc]] (Movement II). Panel breakdown, captions, and image prompts are a later production pass (panel-prompt-smith).

## Continuity / placement

**Movement II.** Follows [[S01E11 — Closed Loop]] (motive decoded; rescue clock set). Precedes [[S01E13 — Field Notes]]. Primm held offscreen throughout.

## Synopsis

Pho-boman's invisibility to calibrated drone sensors — established in canon as a property of being born of the broth rather than remade by the fracture — goes from observed fact to tactical tool. He infiltrates GastroDefense HQ alone and undetected: the drone sweep covering the approach returns a null for his signature, and the asset-recovery cross-reference has no unit code to match. He gets inside.

What he brings back is not a weapon but intelligence: Primm is held on **sublevel 3**, in the steel-frame containment unit; **reintegration has not started**; the scheduled transfer to the chamber on sublevel 2 is in **72 hours**. He reads it off an unsecured staging-area terminal and carries it out in his memory, because he has no free hands. He routes around the building's cold-storage adjacencies (his canon cold vulnerability, managed as a constraint, not a trap).

## Thread advanced

- Pho-boman's invisibility demonstrated at scale — the team's single most important tactical advantage for the rescue.
- The rescue gains a target (sublevel 3) and a deadline (72 hours).

## Twist / suspense

- The drone net has **thickened**: the Retrieval Division pushed a firmware update bolting asset-recovery targeting onto the food-safety fleet. The net is denser than it was at E10 — but still blind to Pho-boman.
- 72 hours, counted from here, becomes the rescue clock.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- Pho-boman is walking unseen through the same facility whose specification lists him for disposal. The system that wants him cannot find him because of the very thing (no incident-signature) that also makes him "non-specification."

## Differentiation / what's new

The first episode where the team goes *first* rather than reacting. Pho-boman — the most existential, least tactical hero through the diaspora — becomes the operationally indispensable one. His liability (invisible, signature-less, disposal-flagged) is now his asset.
