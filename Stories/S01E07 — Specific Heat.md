# S01E07 — Specific Heat

> **Logline:** A soup entity and a gravity manipulator get locked inside an industrial freezer — one is seizing up by degree, the other discovers that every wall is the one thing his field cannot cross, and the only exit is a meter and a half away.

---

## Title

**"Specific Heat"** — the thermodynamic property describing how much energy a substance requires to change temperature. Pho-boman has a very high specific heat (soup does not cool quickly, but when it does, it commits). Primm has a specific heat of zero (he does not change register regardless of circumstances). The term is dry, physics-adjacent, and fits the house title family without explanation: "Momentum Not Included" / "What Is a Ladle For?" / "Hold Your Own Weight" / "Two-Body Problem."

---

## Continuity / placement

This is **Issue 7, Act III, the second reunion episode.** It follows [[S01E06 — Two-Body Problem]] (Windman + Tiebi first meeting) and precedes [[S01E08]] (full four-way convergence). Neither Windman nor Tiebi appears here.

**Primm** closed E05 descending the GastroDefense fire-stairwell toward street level — mini-orbs in tight orbit, posture exact, looking like a hero who always knew how to use stairs. He has been drifting the city since. He has not mentioned the falling to anyone, because there has been no one to mention it to.

**Pho-boman** closed E04 riding the Lunch Lady's rolling food cart out the loading bay toward the street, looking forward, not back. He had filed the broth-jet under: himself, and continued. He has been drifting the city since. He has been warm. The city has been various.

By E07, several weeks have passed for both of them. The episode opens on them already present at the same specific location. No orientation is owed — they are beings who have been operating in the city, and the city has led them here by two entirely different routes.

> **Seam test for any future edit:** does this episode end with exactly two figures — Primm and Pho-boman — in the freezer doorway, facing the same outward direction, Pho-boman's broth resettled, Primm's mini-orbs in tight orbit, neither having named a team, no third hero appearing, GastroDefense named only as a logo and never as an active speaking antagonist? If one figure leaves alone, or if the team is named, or if a third hero appears, the Act III second-reunion beat is broken.

## Format

The visual space is a **single contained stage**: a GastroDefense Inc. logistics cold-storage loading dock and the walk-in industrial freezer behind it. One exterior beat (loading dock, night), then four panels inside or at the threshold of a freezer that is eight by six meters of floor-to-ceiling steel racking — a room designed, as it turns out, to neutralize one specific person completely. The narrator's register in this issue runs two simultaneous gag clocks: Pho-boman's broth thickening described as a bureaucratic process, and Primm's power-failure listed as a surface inventory in the exact diagnostic register he established in E05 but arriving now at different conclusions.

## Panels

### Panel 1 — Two Different Smells

**Image:** Night. The loading dock of a GastroDefense Inc. cold-storage logistics warehouse — wide concrete apron, a parked refrigerated truck to one side with the GastroDefense logo visible on the cab door, cold vapor bleeding faintly from the truck's refrigeration unit. The heavy insulated freezer door stands slightly ajar at the far end of the dock, propped open a careful few inches by a brick at its base. Cold fog rolls faintly through the gap. The scene is seen from a slightly elevated angle, wide enough to hold both approaching figures without either seeing the other.

From the left: Pho-boman's small spoon-shaped silhouette approaches the freezer door at a slow angle, head cocked toward the gap — his noodle-brows in the attentive configuration, drawn by scent, tilted toward the bloom of frozen-pho smell escaping through the propped door. He is following something with his whole body, unhurried, nose-first.

From the right: Primm's tall purple silhouette approaching the same door from a different angle — but his attention is on the truck, not the door. The faint light-bend of the DRIFT field is visible in the air around his right hand, held slightly forward: a gravity-sense sweeping the dock. He has blinked to this location because his field detected anomalous mass displacement at range — the refrigeration unit's cooling machinery registers as a dense mechanical mass signature in motion, which is not the same as a stationary truck, and the anomaly is worth investigating. He is running a diagnosis. He has not registered the door or Pho-boman yet.

The brick is visible at the base of the door. It is the smallest thing in the frame.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *One of them was following a smell.*
- CAPTION: *One of them was following a mass displacement anomaly in the refrigeration unit of a GastroDefense Inc. logistics truck.*
- CAPTION: *The brick had been there for approximately four hours.*
- CAPTION: *It was about to have a different opinion.*

**Why it lands:** The asymmetry of the two arrivals is established before either character is focused on. Pho-boman's instinct is sensory and forward-body; Primm's instinct is diagnostic and precise. The brick's "opinion" callback establishes the episode's voice immediately — E05's railings had opinions, now bricks do. The GastroDefense logo is present as atmosphere only; it names the corporation visually without the captions naming it. Neither figure knows the other is there: two routes, same door, zero coordination. The cold fog through the gap is the first visual temperature cue. The warming pops are present but small: Pho-boman's reddish-orange broth color in the visor, Primm's cyan visor-glow.

### Panel 2 — The Opinions Are Accumulating

**Image:** Inside the freezer. The heavy door has swung shut behind them — the latch-lock mechanism is visible in the closed position, a fact the image presents without editorial. Interior: floor-to-ceiling steel racking on all four walls and most floor space, commercial catering tubs of frozen broth stacked on every shelf, frosted metal surfaces, the cold rendered as vapor-breath and ice-crystal bloom on every rack surface. Temperature: -18°C. The room is approximately eight by six meters of continuous steel mass.

Pho-boman stands center, broth in the helmet visibly changing — the reddish-orange has shifted slightly toward the visor's lower rim, thickening, the usual fluid slosh becoming something more deliberate and slower. His noodle-eyes have contracted toward each other. The cilantro nose is stiffer than usual, less supple. He is not panicking — that is not yet available to him in this register — but the broth has opinions, and the opinions are forming a queue.

To Pho-boman's right: Primm stands one step from the nearest rack column, right hand flat against it, head slightly tilted behind the visor — the posture from E05 Panel 3, the diagnostic narrow focus. The DRIFT light-bend is visible around his palm. At the steel surface, the distortion ends flat — it does not propagate through the rack. It does not find the far wall. It bounces back at him. The field's edge, while pressing against the wall, also reaches Pho-boman's position at center — the light-bend grazes the center of the room where Pho-boman stands, an incidental sweep that registers him as a data point alongside the rack column. His mini-orbs continue their orbit, uninterrupted, because the orbs are close and the field can manage close. The walls are another matter entirely.

A second DRIFT attempt is visible at lower left: his boot has been pressed against the concrete floor, the light-bend visible there too, ending the same way. The floor has a steel subframe. The floor has the same opinion as the walls.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The broth had opinions about -18°C.*
- CAPTION: *The opinions were accumulating.*
- CAPTION: *He tried the wall.*
- CAPTION: *The rack was twelve centimeters of steel.*
- CAPTION: *[FIELD READ: NO DETECTABLE SIGNATURE (warm, 62 kg, broth-type)]*
- CAPTION: *He tried the floor.*
- CAPTION: *The floor had a steel subframe.*

**Why it lands:** The caption staircase runs two simultaneous diagnostic clocks in one frame — Pho-boman's thickening broth (bureaucratic symptom-accumulation) and Primm's power-failure (surface inventory, exact diagnostic register from E05). The FIELD READ caption is the episode's funniest single line: Primm's gravity field grazes Pho-boman during the wall-scan and returns a readout that is simultaneously clinical and completely absurd. "Broth-type" is not a category that exists in any system Primm has a framework for. He files it. He continues the inventory. The two diagnostic registers (broth-thickening / surface-failing) are interlocked without ever addressing each other directly — they are sharing a caption staircase the way two people share an elevator, facing forward, having separate problems. The warm color pop of the broth against frosted grey is the palette anchor.

### Panel 3 — He Filed This Under: A Problem

**Image:** The same freezer interior, a beat later. Primm is elevated slightly — not floating, standing on the lower shelf of a rack to gain a wider view of the room, one hand on the rack's steel upright for balance (an irony the captions note without noting). He is surveying. His expression behind the visor is the narrow diagnostic look from E05 Panel 3: something that, in a human face, would be mild annoyance held at careful distance from any acknowledgment of mild annoyance. The DRIFT light-bend is visible in the air around him — and on all four walls simultaneously, the distortion bounces back. Every surface has the same opinion. The steel racking forms a complete cage, and the field knows it.

Mini-orbs: erratic. Not scattered the way they were in E05 Panel 2 when he was falling — this is a different kind of erratic. They are orbiting correctly but slightly wider than they should be, the way a clock runs correctly but slightly fast when something has changed.

In the mid-ground: Pho-boman, broth now visibly thicker, body slightly hunched, leaning away from the nearest rack with the careful distance of a being that has learned something about metal and cold in combination. He is not leaning against anything. He has found the exact center of the available non-metal floor space and occupied it. His noodle-brows are doing something complex. The cilantro nose is rigid.

Between the two rack columns at the far end of the freezer, visible in the background: a gap. One meter and a half wide, running from the center of the room to the exit door. It is the only non-steel corridor in the room. The image makes it available without pointing to it.

**Caption / dialogue:**

- CAPTION: *He tried the wall behind it.*
- CAPTION: *Also steel.*
- CAPTION: *He tried the floor again.*
- CAPTION: *Concrete over a steel subframe.*

**Why it lands:** The surface-inventory staircase mirrors E05 Panel 2's architecture-opinion captions ("The ceiling rail was not interested / The railing had the same opinion") but with a key difference: in E05, the building's opinions were the building's problem. Here, Primm is the one running the inventory. The competent-on-purpose hero is standing on a rack shelf, listing surfaces that disagree with him, arriving at "a problem" — a phrase that carries exactly the emotional weight of a man who does not say what he means about a problem. The final caption shifts back to Pho-boman's clock (broth-queue) in the driest possible register: the broth is concerned, no one else is involved in its concern. The gap between the rack columns is visible in the background; the reader sees it before Primm does.

### Panel 4 — The Face Goes Away For A Moment

**Image:** Close-wide. This is the twist panel, the emotional beat, and the cooperative act in one frame.

Primm has crossed to Pho-boman and placed one gloved hand — silver-tipped fingers, the same hand that has been pressing against steel walls — against the side of the diving helmet. His palm is pressed across the visor face, covering the open gap: hand flat, fingers spread, the black glove sealing the opening. The DRIFT field fires from that point of contact: the light-bend distortion wraps Pho-boman's entire body in a single contained field, enveloping him from helmet to boots. Not the walls. Not the floor. Pho-boman. This is an established capability — objects within arm's reach — applied to a new target, not a discovery. Primm's expression does not change register. He made a calculation; this is the result.

Inside the transparent visor, visible to the reader through the visor's glass above and around Primm's covering palm: the broth goes weightless. The reddish-orange lifts free of the visor's lower bowl. The noodle-eyes drift apart from their positions, each one floating independent. The cilantro nose rises from center and hovers. The face — the whole dynamic construction that is Pho-boman's face — dissolves into a cloud of floating soup particles and suspended noodles, dispersed across the interior of the helmet in zero gravity. Primm's palm across the visor face keeps everything inside; nothing escapes outward. But inside the helmet: no face. A face that is not yet a face, suspended.

Pho-boman's arms float slightly upward. His boots are no longer planted — he has become effectively weightless, a small figure held in Primm's grip at the helmet, feet barely above the floor. He is not panicking. He is, if anything, experiencing the mild bewilderment of a being that files things. This is a new thing. He will file it.

Primm looks at the dissolved face through the visor, his palm still flat against the glass. The mini-orbs are in tight orbit — not erratic, tight, the way they were at E05 Panel 5 when he had the answer. He has not moved. He is holding a weightless soup entity by the helmet in an industrial freezer, and he has gone still for exactly one beat too long before the run.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- SFX (quiet, integrated into the light-bend field wrapping Pho-boman's body, smaller lettering than the SLURP that follows): *DRIFT—*
- CAPTION: *The broth went weightless.*
- CAPTION: *The face went away for a moment.*
- CAPTION: *He looked at it.*
- CAPTION: *It felt like a word in a language he had not yet learned.*

**Why it lands:** The zero-g dissolved face is made possible by Primm's gloved palm covering the open visor gap — the same hand that has been pressing against steel walls all episode now physically seals the one opening the broth would otherwise escape through. This requires no new anatomy; his hand is the seal. The warmth of the broth stays interior and the dissolved-face image stays contained where it can be read clearly against the cold grey freezer.

The emotional beat is positioned with precision. Primm reacts to the *visual image* of the dissolved face — a face that is not yet settled, a being temporarily without edges — and the caption names his internal response as a linguistic gap, not a physics detection. "A word in a language he had not yet learned" is not the incident-signature that Windman felt in Tiebi's pressure-anomaly in E06. There is no uncanny-familiar flicker, no echo of the shared mark — Pho-boman was born of the broth, not remade by the fracture, and Primm's mass-field has already confirmed this (the FIELD READ in P2 returned broth-type, not resonance). What Primm experiences is not recognition. It is rhyme. He has also been, briefly, a self without edges — one piece of a fractured thing, inherited into a body he did not choose — and the image of a face that dissolves into its components and waits, unhurried, for gravity to come back, rhymes with something he has no word for. The captions do not explain this. The panel holds the image and the stillness and lets the reader do the rest.

Pho-boman experiences nothing that corresponds to this on his side. He is waiting, weightless, for whatever happens next. He will file it.

### Panel 5 — That Was The Whole Plan

**Image:** Overhead-and-slightly-behind register — the same compositional grammar established in E06 Panel 6's two-figure ending. A wide image of the freezer's far end: the gap between two rack columns (one meter and a half of non-steel corridor running from center to the exit door), and through that gap, the exit door, now open, cold vapor flooding outward through it onto the loading dock beyond.

The action has already happened. We catch the frame at the threshold: Primm is in the doorway, Pho-boman tucked under his left arm (still under the DRIFT field, feet barely touching, weight effectively nullified — a tactical choice, not a discovery; he needed to carry the mass he could carry through the gap he could use). They are crossing the threshold. As they do: gravity returns to Pho-boman. The DRIFT field ends at the doorframe.

Inside the helmet, the broth crashes back down into the visor's lower bowl — and as it does:

**SFX** (bold, warm, the episode's loudest sound, in contrast to the freezer's silence): *SLURP—*

The face comes back. The noodle-eyes drop into position. The cilantro nose falls back to center. The reddish-orange blooms at the visor's lower rim, fluid again. The broth has settled.

Both figures are in the doorway, half-in and half-out, cold vapor dissipating around them. They face the same direction: outward, toward the loading dock, toward the city. Primm's mini-orbs are in tight orbit. Pho-boman's visor-face is back and upright. Neither is looking at the other. The freezer is behind them. They are not looking at it.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The gap between the racks was a meter and a half.*
- CAPTION: *He ran it.*
- CAPTION: *The door opened.*
- CAPTION: *That was the whole plan.*
- CAPTION: *Outside, the broth settled.*
- CAPTION: *The face came back.*
- CAPTION: *Neither of them said anything about it.*

**Why it lands:** The comedy payoff is purely geometric: the great tactical effort of DRIFT-on-a-body plus a full sprint covers one meter and a half and ends with a shoulder. The gravity manipulator's solution to a complete power-neutralization is to run. The captions deliver this with the same flat inventory register Primm used for his wall-testing in P2 and P3 — "he ran it / the door opened / that was the whole plan" — and the compression is the joke. There is no triumph. There is a door that opened. That is what doors do.

The final three captions shift register slightly — not to sentiment, but to the narrator noting what has been restored and what neither of them has said. "Neither of them said anything about it" covers everything: the dissolved face, the beat too long, the broth-queue, the meter and a half of heroism. The house rule holds: neither of them said the word "team." Neither named the other. They are in a doorway, facing outward, same direction. The series grammar established in E06 Panel 6 — two figures, not one, facing the same direction outward — is honored and continued in its own form. Every diaspora episode ended on one figure walking alone, away. Both reunion episodes end on two figures at a threshold, facing the same direction, not walking away.

The SLURP at the threshold is the episode's warmest beat and its loudest sound, which is correct: the soup is back, the face is back, and the cold is behind them.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- Primm is one of two AEGIS-4 fragments. Pho-boman is not a fragment — he is a wholly new being born when pho broth filled the prototype's diving helmet at the moment of fracture. The incident-signature that Windman detected in Tiebi in E06 — the shared mark the AEGIS-4 fracture left on the bodies it remade — does not exist between this pair: Pho-boman was *created* by the incident, not *remade* by it, so he carries none of it. Primm's gravity field has already confirmed this: FIELD READ returns broth-type, not resonance. There is no signal to decode.
- What Primm felt in P4 — the beat-too-long stillness, the dissolved face, the word in a language not yet learned — is not recognition. It is structural rhyme. He was a self without edges once, in a different way, for different reasons (fracture, not dissolution). The image of a face that temporarily is not there rhymes with something he carries about a body he did not choose. He has no vocabulary for this. The audience has the vocabulary and can see it. He cannot.
- Pho-boman has no corresponding internal experience during the zero-g moment. He was waiting, weightless, for whatever happened next. He has filed it under: that happened. He is not missing information. He is complete.
- GastroDefense Inc. has logged all four as "Recalled." Two of them just exited one of their logistics freezers via a shoulder-barge. The corporation does not know. The brick's original purpose — propping the door — was someone's logistics error, not a plan. The captions did not investigate who left it. Neither will the corporation.
- The cooperation that just occurred — DRIFT on a body, one corridor, a shoulder — was Primm solving his own exit problem, and it happened to defuse Pho-boman's cold crisis as a byproduct. Neither of them chose to help the other. The help was structural. The audience can see what this looks like when it becomes deliberate. Neither of them can see that yet.
- Primm will not mention the freezer. He will not mention the walls, or the inventory of surfaces, or the meter and a half. These are private facts. He is, constitutionally, not going to bring them up.

## Differentiation / what's new

E06 is the first reunion. E07 is the second. They must not be the same episode.

**E06's meeting mechanic** was uncanny-familiar: Windman detected Tiebi as an anomaly in his pressure-field — a mass-signature that "did not stay one size" — and the dramatic irony was that both of them felt something they could not name, because both were remade by the same incident — Windman split from the prototype in it, Tiebi transformed where he stood — and carry the same faint mark of it. The emotional core was mutual and symmetric, even if neither could articulate it.

**E07's meeting mechanic** is the structural negative of that. There is no shared signal. Primm's field reads Pho-boman as broth-type — 62 kg, warm, no detectable signature — and finds none of the mark the fracture left on the bodies it remade. Pho-boman does not scan: he was born of the broth, an entity the incident *created* rather than *remade*, so he carries no echo of it. He is born-complete; there is no recognition to be had. The remade-versus-born asymmetry means the emotional beat, when it arrives, is entirely one-directional and entirely accidental: it lives in what Primm sees in a dissolved face, not in what either of them detects in the other.

**E06's cooperation** was complementary-by-design: Windman sensed, Tiebi acted, they were accidentally a sensor-actuator pair. The cooperation had the shape of two powers fitting together.

**E07's cooperation** is accidental in a different sense: Primm applies DRIFT to Pho-boman not as a rescue but as a logistics solution. He needed to carry a mass he could nullify through the corridor he could use. That Pho-boman's cold crisis was defused by the same action is a byproduct. The cooperation has the shape of two problems sharing one solution, not two heroes sharing one plan.

**E06 ended on dialogue** — Tiebi and Windman's first words to each other, flat and precise. The comedy was in what was said and not said.

**E07 ends in silence** — "neither of them said anything about it." The comedy is in the distance. The meter and a half. The shoulder.

The slapstick scales up: E06 had one street-level incident (a runner, a gust, a momentum-roll). E07 has a complete power-neutralization inside a steel cage. But the lonely-funny register of the diaspora arc still hums underneath both reunion episodes — these are not people who have found each other; they are people who have solved the same problem. They are in the doorway. They are facing the same direction. That is what the series has.
