# S01E02 — Feel the Room

> **Logline:** Flung from the split and still four minutes old, a blind man on a rooftop discovers that the wind answers when he panics — and that it has been answering his questions all along, drawing the city around him in pressure and air.

> **Status:** **Author-accepted canon — 2026-06-15.** Spark-drafted, Editor-reconciled
> (agreed 2026-06-14; E01↔E02 continuity seam fixed 2026-06-15), then reviewed and
> accepted by the author 2026-06-15. Logged in [[Canon & Sources]].

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page** (one page). Each panel is one image with tight captions / minimal dialogue — except **Panel 5, which resolves across two images** (beat A on the rooftop, beat B at street level: the hand-off into [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 5). Iconography over blocking. No other hero appears. No dialogue from any other character except one offscreen civilian in Panel 4 (ambient, not addressed to the protagonist).

## Working title

**"Feel the Room"** — double meaning: the literal act of pressure-sensing a physical space, and the idiomatic challenge of reading a situation you cannot see.


## Cast (this issue)

- **The rooftop stranger** — no name yet. Fragment of Project AEGIS-4. Body: 180 cm, moderately stout, chestnut hair, calm rounded face, blue W-monogram tee, brown knee-length shorts, fully black flat boots. Eye bandage covering both eyes completely — top half blue, lower half (above eyebrows) red. Multicolored three-blade fan in right hand (green / blue / red blades on a brown 25 cm handle), blades folded shut at first, snapping open mid-episode.
- **A pigeon** — involuntary straight man. Present Panel 2. No lines.
- **A city** — a rooftop three blocks east of the GastroDefense Inc. campus, a street six floors below that will become a compass spoke, a wobbling window-unit AC on the ledge of the adjacent building. The campus is visible on the horizon in Panel 1 and absent for the rest; the street appears in Panel 5. The city is the only interlocutor. Present throughout.
- **Offscreen civilian** — one distant shout in Panel 4. Never seen.

## Panels

### Panel 1 — Inventory

**Image:** A flat rooftop at dusk. Low horizon. Brick parapet in the foreground, water tower in the far background. The man sits on the graveled tar paper, knees drawn up, back against the parapet wall — the posture of someone who came to rest here, not someone who chose it. Both hands are on the ground beside him, palms down, fan lying closed against his right thigh. The eye bandage covers his face like a fact. His head is level, not tilted up toward the sky, not bowed. He is simply present, expressionless, in a body he did not choose. Three blocks to the east, barely visible above the parapet line, the blocky upper edge of a corporate building catches the last orange light — the GastroDefense Inc. campus, distant and indifferent. The city below is otherwise out of frame. He cannot see any of this.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION (dry, no box, small): *He has been awake for approximately four minutes.*
- CAPTION (below, same register): *He does not know what that means.*

**Why it lands:** The rooftop is not a mystery — the campus is right there in the background, three blocks east, establishing this as the tail of a trajectory that started at the split. The posture ("came to rest here") signals the same thing without stating it: he did not climb up, he arrived. The captions are unchanged — dry, case-file register, slightly corporate, which makes the loneliness funnier and sadder at the same time. The fan lying closed is a cocked gun. The GastroDefense building in the background is the cocked second gun: GastroDefense is watching (it isn't, yet), and the reader knows it.

---

### Panel 2 — Wrong Answer

**Image:** The stranger is on his feet now, slightly off-balance, both arms out for stability. He has clearly just tried to walk toward the parapet and reached for it — his right hand, still holding the fan (now snapped fully open by reflex, the three blades catching the last light), swings wide in a correcting gesture. The result is a **sudden involuntary burst of pressurized air** radiating from the fan — not aimed, not controlled, a flinch translated into wind. The evidence is everywhere: gravel skids off the rooftop in a low arc, a loose candy-bar wrapper cartwheels past the parapet, and a **pigeon** that was roosting six feet away launches vertically into the air, eyes wide, feathers blown comically sideways. The stranger has no idea the bird was there. His expression: the precise blankness of someone who did not expect what just happened and is reassessing. The fan blades, still spread, are the most vivid color on the page — green, blue, red against the flat rooftop grey.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- SFX (small, integrated into the gust trace): *HWUF—*
- *(no other dialogue)*

**Why it lands:** "Air comes when he panics" — delivered. The first gust is involuntary, untargeted, and immediately comic because the only witness is a pigeon. But something else happened in that same instant that the panel does not explain yet: the displacement of air he just created came back, and he felt it. The shape of the parapet wall, the gap to the next building, the bird — all of it briefly registered as pressure. He does not know what that means. The audience sees the fan open on its own and understands it is responsive to him. He does not know that yet either.

---

### Panel 3 — The City in Pressure

**Image:** A split-register panel — not two equal halves, but a foreground/background divide. **Foreground:** the stranger standing very still at the parapet edge, fan held loosely at his side, blades open but not swinging. He is not reaching out. His chin is slightly lifted, not in defiance — in attention. He is listening with his whole body. **Background (rendered in a different visual register — tighter linework, slightly desaturated, ghost-lines):** an overlay of the city as he is perceiving it, pressure-mapped. The adjacent building renders as a silhouette of air-displacement: the blocky outline of a window-unit AC sticking out from the wall, a cable line strung between rooftops, the faint signature of a bus passing six floors below on the street — all present as pressure-shapes, not as visible objects. The visual language signals clearly: this is not sight. This is something else. It has no name yet. It simply *is*.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *He stops trying to see.*
- CAPTION (below): *Something else is already there.*

**Why it lands:** This is the pivot — the moment where the episode stops being about blindness and starts being about perception. The visual split (what the audience sees vs. what he is reading) makes the distinction concrete without requiring any dialogue or explanation. The captions are deliberately ambiguous — "something else" could be the power, could be the city, could be both. The audience who read Panel 2 closely will realize: the pressure-map is what he felt when the gust came back. This is the same mechanism. He just has not named it yet.

---

### Panel 4 — The Test

**Image:** The window-unit AC on the ledge of the adjacent building is falling. It tipped — loosened by age or vibration from the bus passing — and is now mid-air, trailing a power cord, tumbling toward the alley six floors below. Time-suspended panel: the AC unit is frozen at the apex of its tilt, cord fully taut, bottom edge already past the ledge. Directly across the gap, the stranger's fan is arcing through a fast, deliberate stroke — **not a panic swing, a measured one**, the first controlled motion of the issue. The air pressure visible as a compression wave rolling across the gap between rooftops (shown as concentric pressure-rings in the visual language established in Panel 3) stops the AC unit, wraps around it like a hand, holds it at the ledge. The unit is not pushed back up — it is simply caught, suspended in the gap, power cord still dangling. Below, in the alley: tiny oblivious figures going about their day. One offscreen voice rises from somewhere across the street.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- DISTANT VOICE (from off-panel, very small lettering): *"Hey, is that—"*
- *(cut off; the panel does not answer)*

**Why it lands:** The control and the perception fuse here into one physical act: he could not have caught the AC unit without already knowing where it was and which way it was moving. He aimed the gust not toward where he heard it, but toward where the pressure told him it was going. For the audience the two powers — aerokinesis and pressure-sensing — collapse visibly into a single capability: he read the air and answered it in the same gesture. The civilian voice is cut off mid-sentence to keep the issue sealed — nobody completes that sentence, the city just returns to noise. He is still completely alone.

---

### Panel 5 — He Has a Name for It

**Image (beat A — parapet):** Evening, now fully dark above, city-glow below. The man stands at the parapet, facing outward over the city — the full expanse of the street grid spreading away from him. The fan is held up in his right hand, blades fully open, relaxed, not aimed at anything. The city below is rendered as a soft pressure-map in the same ghost-line visual register from Panel 3 — rooftops, signage, the distant water tower, a moving vehicle — all present to him, all shaped in air. His expression, for the first time in the issue, is not blank. It is quiet. Specifically: the expression of someone who just figured out the answer to a question they did not know they were asking. There is no audience for this.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION (bottom right of beat A, the smallest and most offhand line in the issue): *Windman.*

---

**Image (beat B — street level, handoff frame):** The same man, now at street level, seen from the side in the last light of the city-glow. He has just come down — the fire-escape stairs or a rooftop access door behind him, already dismissed. He is walking. Not toward the GastroDefense campus (that is behind him, three blocks east). Not toward anything in particular. One compass direction, one street, the grid stretching ahead. The pressure-map ghost-line register fades at the edges — the city is not rendered, just implied. He does not look back. His fan is at his side, blades folded shut. The panel is calm, not triumphant. He is four minutes old and walking.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- *(no caption — the name has already landed; there is nothing left to say)*

**Why it lands:** Beat A delivers the name as it should — quietly, as a caption, arrived at alone. Beat B delivers the handoff: he steps off the roof and onto the compass-spoke street, walking away from the campus, not facing the building or anything behind him. The next wide frame a reader encounters — [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 5's overhead shot — now reads as the immediate sequel: the tiny figure on one of four streets is him, and we just watched him get there. The ghost-line pressure-map fading out signals that perception is still running, the city still present in air, but the chapter of the rooftop is closed.

## What the audience knows that the hero doesn't

- He was one part of four — three came from one body, one from the broth. He has no idea any of the others exist.
- The fan in his right hand crystallized with him at split-time — it was not something he chose or carried in; it was simply there when he woke, as if it always had been.
- The eye bandage that makes him "blind" is also the reason his pressure-sensing works so clearly: without visual input competing, the pressure-map is the only signal. His equipment does not compensate for a disadvantage. It *is* the advantage.
- GastroDefense Inc. has already logged him as "Recalled." They know approximately where the fragments landed.

## Tone targets

- Panel 1: quiet, dry, near-empty. Palette: dusk flat grey, single warm smear above. The loneliness is the joke. No pity.
- Panel 2: the first pop of color and action — fan blades are the only saturated elements on the page. Comic timing via the pigeon.
- Panel 3: tonal pivot. Still quiet, but the visual register splits. A moment of hush before understanding.
- Panel 4: the only kinetic panel. Pressure-rings, suspended AC, cut-off voice. Short moment; do not over-extend.
- Panel 5: resolution + hand-off. Beat A (parapet): warm city-glow, dark sky, soft ghost-pressure map, the small "Windman" caption, big silence. Beat B (street level): he descends and starts down one compass-spoke street away from the campus, fan folded, no caption — the frame that flows straight into [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]] Panel 5.
