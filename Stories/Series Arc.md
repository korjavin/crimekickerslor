# Series Arc — Season One (20 Episodes)

The master architecture for Crime Kickers as **one continuous twenty-episode story** —
not twenty separate issues. Builds on [[Origin & Concept]] (canon). Individual episode
beats remain proposal until each issue is drafted and approved; the spine and structure
below are author-directed.

## Spine

> **Four beings who are one broken thing spend twenty episodes discovering what they are
> to each other — and whether the answer to that question is worth what it costs.**

Project AEGIS-4 fractured in a lunchtime accident and walked out as four people: two
fragments of one incomplete prototype, one soup-being born of the same moment, and one
transformed human who already knew who he was before the incident changed him.
[[The Bad Guys|GastroDefense Inc.]], the corporation that built the prototype, eventually
notices its recall truck never arrived. What follows is not a pursuit — it is a workflow
resuming after a variance ticket exceeded its auto-escalation threshold. The four slowly
learn what the workflow means for each of them. The series is about whether knowing the
answer to that question makes it possible to choose.

## The dramatic engine

**Why does GastroDefense recall now — eighteen months after the fracture?** Because a
law-enforcement client filed a contract performance notice (GD-C-4419) citing the
corporation's failure to deliver the completed AEGIS-4 prototype. The recall is a
compliance step against a liability, not a vendetta. GastroDefense would prefer to close
the file. The four are never hunted by a pursuing villain — they are pursued by a *system*:
a workflow, a specification, a budget line in a department that has not processed a ticket
since Q3 of the prior fiscal year. The threat is paperwork. The menace is indifference.

**The central dramatic irony.** The audience knows from [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]]
that Windman and Primm are two fragments of one original body. The heroes do not, for most
of the run. The fragments can sense each other (strongest [[Windman]] ↔ [[Primm]];
[[Tiebi]] carries the lesser incident-signature echo; [[Pho-boman]], born of broth, carries
none), but they have no language for it. So every act of alliance between the two fragments
is, to the audience, also a glimpse of what the recall would destroy. The series runs on
this irony for twenty episodes.

## Format

Each episode is a **comic issue of 4–6 panels** (one page, occasionally a two-page spread
for splash moments). One image per panel, tight captions, minimal dialogue. Iconography
over blocking. The diaspora issues are quiet and lonely-funny; slapstick scales up as the
team converges; corporate dread runs underneath from the recall onward.

## Movement structure

The twenty episodes form four movements of one unbroken story. There is no season break;
**E10 is the midpoint and the largest turn so far**, not a finale.

| Movement | Episodes | Shape |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **I — Scatter and Settle** | E01–E09 | Origin → diaspora → reunion → the recall is read. Ends on `SCHEDULING RETRIEVAL`. |
| **II — Run, and the Net Forms** | E10–E14 | Retrieval executes; Primm captured (midpoint); the rescue. |
| **III — What They Are** | E15–E17 | The rescue's cost; the AEGIS lineage decoded; AEGIS-5; the removed consent field. |
| **IV — The Boil** | E18–E20 | The un-make question brought to maximum pressure; the injunction; the third option. |

## Movement I — Scatter and Settle (E01–E09) — established foundation

These nine issues are the locked opening movement. Retroactively, they are the setup the
back half pays off.

- **E01 — [[S01E01 — Some Assembly Required]].** The origin incident: AEGIS-4 fractures
  (assembly-incomplete + the Lunch Lady's pho into the open helmet visor — a lucky/random
  event). Two true fragments (Windman, Primm), one being born of the broth (Pho-boman), one
  human transformed on-site (Tiebi). The four walk out in four directions, unaware of each
  other. Closes: *"Project AEGIS-4 — Status: Recalled."*
- **E02–E05 — diaspora.** One issue per hero discovering their power alone:
  [[S01E02 — Feel the Room]] (Windman) · [[S01E03 — Momentum Not Included]] (Tiebi) ·
  [[S01E04 — What Is a Ladle For]] (Pho-boman) · [[S01E05 — Hold Your Own Weight]] (Primm).
  Lonely-funny; each earns its name from an ambient detail in the environment.
- **E06–E08 — reunion.** They meet two at a time and then all at once:
  [[S01E06 — Two-Body Problem]] (Windman + Tiebi) · [[S01E07 — Specific Heat]]
  (Primm + Pho-boman) · [[S01E08 — Four-Body Problem]] (all four converge to stop a
  driverless GastroDefense recall truck; the recall notice is on its side; nobody reads it).
- **E09 — [[S01E09 — Variance Report]].** GastroDefense's automated systems notice the
  truck never arrived. The four find a terminal and read the recall specification:
  *"RETURN TO ORIGIN FACILITY"* means **re-integration = un-make** — irreversible, with
  no consent field. W-01 and P-03 are "specification outputs" (to be reintegrated); P-02
  and T-04 are "non-specification outputs" (disposal). The system schedules retrieval. The
  database auto-generates the label **CRIME KICKERS** from incident data — the heroes never
  see it.

## Movement II — Run, and the Net Forms (E10–E14)

### E10 — Half-Life *(midpoint)*

`SCHEDULING RETRIEVAL` becomes `RETRIEVAL IN PROGRESS`. The Retrieval Division runs its
standard, inelegant asset-recovery protocol. **Primm is captured** — not by tactical
cleverness but because the protocol's containment unit is a steel-frame holding structure
standard for industrial equipment, and its mass incidentally constrains his gravity field.
The door closes; a capture confirmation is filed; this is within spec. The other three
escape (Pho-boman is invisible to the drone net; Tiebi shrinks into a vent; Windman reads
an unsensored exit in the air). Windman feels the gap where the fragment-resonance used to
be and has no word for it. Ticket: `ASSET RECOVERED 1/4`.

### E11 — Closed Loop *(Twist 1: the recall is a contract dispute)*

Tiebi uses his former security-officer credentials to open the Retrieval Division's active
case file (a different document from E09's substation terminal). Its header already reads
**CRIME KICKERS** — the team inherits its name the way a case number arrives: bureaucratically,
without ceremony, and uses it functionally from here on. The same file reveals the recall is
being executed under contract performance notice GD-C-4419: this is liability management, not
malice. Crucially, reintegration is **on hold** until Windman (W-01) is also in custody — the
two fragments are reintegrated only together. While three stay free, Primm has time.

### E12 — Thermal Gradient

Pho-boman's invisibility to calibrated drone sensors goes from observed fact to tactical
tool: he infiltrates GastroDefense HQ alone and undetected. He confirms Primm is held on
sublevel 3, reintegration not yet begun, with a scheduled transfer to the chamber in
72 hours. The drone net has thickened — a firmware update bolted asset-recovery targeting
onto the food-safety fleet. The rescue now has a target and a clock.

### E13 — Field Notes *(Twist 2 seed: prior AEGIS iterations are real)*

The **Junior Engineer** returns — the one channel the net cannot track, because he has a
human face and simply walks up to Tiebi on a street, outside HQ. He has been running his own
investigation since the fracture; his lab notebook maps the prior iterations: **AEGIS-1, -2,
-3 each fractured and scattered.** The "4" in Project AEGIS-4 was always a count. The
notebook also logs an older signal still active at an address in the city. They are not the
first.

### E14 — Dead Reckoning

The rescue. Pho-boman enters first, unseen, and routes to sublevel 3; Windman and Tiebi run
a pressure-and-size distraction at the loading dock (Tiebi hits his low-ceiling limit as a
managed cost; Windman reads the route through the air-handling system). Primm phases out —
field weakened after 72+ hours in steel. The four are together again. **But:** the
reintegration chamber was in staging when Pho-boman and Primm passed through it, its
preliminary alignment field running ambient. The protocol has no branch for "recovered by
the subject's own team," so it escalates — to a `[REDACTED]` recipient.

## Movement III — What They Are (E15–E17)

### E15 — Partial Contact *(the cost)*

Two days later, Windman and Primm notice the fragment-resonance has changed: the involuntary
flutter from E08 is now an **ongoing signal** both fragments feel at all times. During the
extraction, both passed through the chamber's ambient prep field — no reintegration occurred,
but the preliminary alignment contacted both AEGIS-4 fragment signatures and woke the
resonance from dormant to active. The un-make question is no longer a document. It is a
sensation they now carry daily.

### E16 — Prior Art *(double decode: Twist 3 + Twist 6)*

Following the notebook's old signal, the four reach a disused former GastroDefense facility
where a "still-warm" AEGIS-3 fragment signal lingers in the walls. The prior reintegration
log decodes two things at once:

- **Twist 3 — disposal has been executed before.** AEGIS-3's reintegration was completed,
  and one of its non-specification outputs was disposed of under GD-Standard-7 — the same
  standard that covers Pho-boman. The log uses only the standard's language and **does not
  describe what the disposed entity was.** Disposal is not a threat. It is a filed,
  signed-off historical fact.
- **Twist 6 — the truck cargo was the reagent.** The same log references CATERING-004A and a
  frozen broth reagent. The cargo from the E08 truck — *FROZEN PHO BROTH (BULK), GASTRODEFENSE
  CATERING DIVISION, DESTINATION: RECALL PROCESSING CENTER* — was the reagent for **their**
  reintegration. The team-joy beat of E08 was the closest any of them came to being un-made,
  and Pho-boman's origin-broth and the un-making reagent are the same lineage.

### E17 — Cascade Failure

E14's escalation resolves: the `[REDACTED]` recipient is **AEGIS-5** — not a person, not a
villain, but the successor prototype program, which is auto-notified of any AEGIS-4 recovery
failure because it depends on AEGIS-4 reintegration data to finalize its own specification.
AEGIS-5 is a specification, not a character: it communicates by form and hardware interrupt,
never by speech. Near its development lab, Windman perceives in the firmware the original
AEGIS-4 spec (Rev. 1) — which contained a **consent field** (Appendix C, Voluntary Compliance
Protocol), removed in Rev. 3 before completion. Consent was once a design parameter; it was
deleted. A client deadline closes; "alternative enforcement" is noted as a contract option.

## Movement IV — The Boil (E18–E20)

### E18 — Resonance

The ongoing resonance becomes the subject rather than the background. Tiebi — the only one
who can phrase it — puts the specification's question to the fragments directly: reintegration
is irreversible and they will no longer exist as separate units; having felt proximity to that
state, can they describe it? They cannot, except that it does **not** feel like extinction —
it feels like a word they have no word for. Pho-boman says nothing: he is to be disposed of
whatever the fragments decide, and has known since E09.

### E19 — Consent Field *(Twist 4: the injunction is a revival)*

The client exercises "alternative enforcement": a legal **injunction**, served on Tiebi as
the only Crime Kicker with a legal identity. Ten days to present at the Origin Facility for
compliance. The case header's number shares a parent filing with the AEGIS-3 prior recall —
**the four were named in an active legal action before they had names.** *"Status: Recalled"*
in E01 was a legal status. The injunction does not ask for consent; the current spec (Rev. 7)
has no such field, and Appendix C was never written. The ten-day clock starts.

### E20 — Return to Origin Facility *(Twist 5 + the open turn)*

On day nine, Windman and Primm choose a **third option** — not un-make, not separation, not
compliance: a deliberate, consensual use of the resonance field that the specification has no
template for. It costs something real on two registers: **structural** (the resonance field
is permanently altered) and **personal** (the act costs one of the four something specific —
left for the E20 drafter, and reserved). AEGIS-5 logs `ANOMALOUS STATE — SPEC MISMATCH` and
escalates to a target the series has not revealed. In the Origin Facility cafeteria —
GastroDefense catering, present because it is a Tuesday — the **Lunch Lady** observes the
result and says her second word of the season: *"Sufficient."* Nothing is resolved: the
un-make question is open, the resonance is changed but not closed, the four are still four,
and a status field is still `processing`. The season ends on a turn.

## Twist ledger

Each twist is a collapse of facts already on the table, not a deus ex machina.

| # | Twist | Episode | Recontextualizes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | The recall is a contract dispute | E11 | The threat was always paperwork; every apparent menace was a workflow step. |
| 2 | Prior AEGIS iterations are real | E13–E14 | The "4" was a count; the four are the latest iteration, not the only one. |
| 3 | Disposal has been executed before | E16 | Disposal is not a threat but a filed historical fact. The asymmetry is precedented. |
| 4 | The injunction is a revival | E19 | The four were named in an active legal action before they existed. |
| 5 | The third option is not in the spec | E20 | The heroes' strongest act is also the series' most open ending. |
| 6 | The truck cargo was the reagent | E16 (planted E08) | The E08 team-joy beat was their closest brush with un-making. |

## Planted but reserved (beyond-20 seeds)

The un-make moral question is brought to maximum pressure and **not** resolved — it remains
the series' final moral question. The third option of E20 changes the resonance *state*; it
does not change the underlying mechanic (reintegrating W and P would still un-make all four).
Open threads carried past E20:

- Whether the third option forecloses or merely defers reintegration — the system is still
  processing.
- Whether the cost was worth it — the series does not evaluate this.
- AEGIS-5's escalation target — unrevealed.
- The "still-warm" AEGIS-3 fragment signal at the city address — never approached.
- The law-enforcement client's enforcement mechanism beyond the injunction.
- What Appendix C (the original Voluntary Compliance Protocol) would have contained — never
  written.

## Tone notes

- Comedic-absurd, per [[Origin & Concept]]; lonely-funny early, ensemble-slapstick mid,
  corporate-dread underneath from the recall onward.
- GastroDefense is **soulless, not menacing** — evil by quarterly report, never monologuing.
  Its agents are forms, deadlines, firmware, and departments resuming function. OCP from
  RoboCop is the reference. AEGIS-5 is a specification, not a dark lord.
- The Lunch Lady is **not** a villain. She is a force of nature with a ladle.

## Canon anchors

- Members: exactly four — Windman (W-01), Pho-boman (P-02), Primm (P-03), Tiebi (T-04).
- Origin: Windman and Primm are fragments of Project AEGIS-4; Pho-boman is born of the
  fracture incident; Tiebi is a transformed human (ex-soldier, ex-GastroDefense security
  officer), the only Crime Kicker with a human past. See [[Origin & Concept]].
- The fracture is a lucky/random event, not sabotage or design.
- The CRIME KICKERS name is not spoken by the team until they inherit it from GastroDefense's
  records (E11). It is never a triumphant adoption.
- The un-make dilemma is the series' final moral question — planted across the season,
  reserved beyond it.
