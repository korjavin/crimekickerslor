# S01E16 — Prior Art

> **Logline:** They follow an old signal to an empty building and read a log that tells them two things: this has been done before, and the soup that made one of them was shipped to un-make all of them.

---

## Title

**"Prior Art"** — the legal and technical term for existing work that precedes and informs a current specification: documented prior iterations that recontextualize everything filed after them. It sits comfortably in the house title family (dry institutional language, no drama). Here it names the episode's double discovery: the AEGIS lineage is prior art to the four's own existence, and the reagent that made Pho-boman is prior art to the reintegration procedure that would erase him. The term is cold, precise, and carries no alarm. GastroDefense would use it in a patent filing.

---

## Continuity / placement

**Movement III.** Follows [[S01E15 — Partial Contact]] (the rescue's cost named: the resonance is now an ongoing signal both fragments carry). Precedes [[S01E17 — Cascade Failure]] (AEGIS-5 introduced; the consent field found).

The episode pursues the "older signal still active at an address in the city" that the Junior Engineer logged in [[S01E13 — Field Notes]] and noted beside it: *don't know what this means yet.* Two earlier plants pay off here as one retro-charge: the cargo manifest in [[S01E08 — Four-Body Problem]] — *FROZEN PHO BROTH (BULK), GASTRODEFENSE CATERING DIVISION, DESTINATION: RECALL PROCESSING CENTER* — and the lot code CATERING-004A filed in [[S01E09 — Variance Report]] as P-02's own origin reference. The prior-art log names that lot as the reintegration reagent.

**E15 closed** on Windman and Primm carrying the ongoing resonance — the sensation the extraction cost them. The four arrive at this episode aware of the cost; the episode is not about the resonance. It is about what they find in an empty room, which is worse.

**E17 opens** on the AEGIS-5 escalation resolving. This episode plants the lineage knowledge that makes E17's reveal land harder: by the time the four see the successor program, they know they are the fourth iteration of a procedure GastroDefense has already run and completed.

> **Seam test:** Does this episode open with the four already moving together toward the old signal (not a new collision), deliver both twists through a paper record in an empty building (no antagonist present), leave the "still-warm" AEGIS-3 signal unresolved and unapproached, and close without a word from any character about what they have read? If anyone narrates the moral weight aloud, if GastroDefense speaks, or if the AEGIS-3 signal is investigated beyond detection, the Movement III double-decode is broken.

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page** (one page; five panels is correct for an issue whose weight is entirely in one document — the centerpiece Panel 3 is the double-decode, and it needs four panels of architecture around it without crowding any of them).

The visual space is a **disused former GastroDefense processing facility** — industrial bones of a building that has not run operations in years. Not dramatic ruins: institutional dormancy. The exterior is unremarkable. The interior is the inside of a building that was sealed and forgotten rather than demolished — terminals dark, fluorescent tubes cold, one emergency-power strip still drawing current from the grid because nobody filed the work order to cut it. The air is undisturbed. The four arrive and disturb it.

The palette is the coldest in the series to date: no warm color present until Pho-boman enters and his reddish-orange broth is the only source of warmth in the frame. That contrast is intentional and structural. Where E08 built toward a four-color arrival, this episode moves in the opposite direction: the colder the building, the warmer the broth reads, the more visually legible the cost of what the log says about it.

Captions are the dry case-file narrator throughout — boxed, deadpan, observational. Dialogue is zero to minimal; the log does all the talking. The Junior Engineer does not appear on-page — his notebook is represented only by what it pointed to.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- **The disposed AEGIS-3 non-specification output** is the historical precedent for Pho-boman and Tiebi's filed fates. GD-Standard-7 (Pho-boman's disposal category) has been executed before. The asymmetry the audience has tracked since E09 is shown to have already been executed — on someone, something, the log does not say what — as a routine closure step with a case number. The heroes now know this too. But the audience has been holding the asymmetry since E09; the heroes have held it since they read the specification. The weight is different.
- **The CATERING-004A reagent lineage** is a collapse the audience has been assembling across two prior episodes. In E08 the truck's cargo manifest — visible through the cab window — read *FROZEN PHO BROTH (BULK), GASTRODEFENSE CATERING DIVISION, DESTINATION: RECALL PROCESSING CENTER*; the audience knew the cargo, but the manifest carried no lot code. In E09 the recall specification gave that lot a name: Section 4.2 filed P-02's origin as *"Incident Log ref. CATERING-004A."* CATERING-004A is Pho-boman's own incident reference. Here, Section 6.2 reveals the same designation is the reintegration reagent across program iterations. The heroes learn what it means only now. Pho-boman's scent-identification of the truck in E08 ("he found this interesting; he was also not entirely sure these were two separate facts") is fully decoded: they were not separate facts. The lot that records where he came from is the lot that would un-make the four.
- **The "still-warm" AEGIS-3 signal** means that reintegration into a prototype does not fully consolidate a signal. The prior program's completion did not erase everything. The building holds something from the prior iteration. What this means for W-01 and P-03 if their own reintegration were completed — whether they would fully consolidate, or whether something would linger in walls somewhere — is reserved and never stated. The audience holds this quietly. It is not a question the series will answer.
- **What the disposed AEGIS-3 entity was** is permanently unspecified. The log uses only GD-Standard-7 form language. The audience knows the form covers non-specification outputs — the same category as Pho-boman. The audience does not know what the entity was. The series does not know. The series will not find out. The form is the record.
