# S01E03 — Captions

Print-ready narration captions for [[S01E03 — Momentum Not Included]] — to letter onto the
comic page beside each picture. Unlike [[S01E02 — Captions]] (deliberately spare, lots of
silence), **E03 is caption-driven**: the dry case-file staircase captions are the comedic
engine. There is **no SFX and no dialogue** anywhere in the issue — every line on the page is
a narration caption in the same deadpan register established in S01E01/S01E02. 6 panels total.
Pairs with [[S01E03 — Panel Prompts]].

## Lettering notes

- **Everything on the page is a narration caption.** E03 has no SFX and no dialogue bubbles —
  the dry case-file narrator voice carries the whole issue.
- **The staircase is the joke — letter each beat as a separate stacked box**, not run together.
  Panels 1, 2, 3, 4, and 5 each land short captions with a beat between. Merging them into
  one block kills the comic timing. The rhythm is the comedy (and, in Panel 5, the triumph).
- **Panel 2's closet comedy depends on sequence.** The beret-correction beat ("He straightened
  the beret. / He always straightened it.") lands last, after the ceiling-stop fails — the
  reflex is the punchline. Letter it as the quiet button on the closet sequence.
- **Panel 5 has a special mixed-text device:** between the two ghost-images, set apart in its
  own caption-quote box (distinct from the narration staircase boxes), the single word:
  *— "Breach."* — in quotation marks, with an em-dash prefix, signalling a half-spoken word.
  Then the four narration staircase captions follow below it. The quote box and the staircase
  boxes are both present in Panel 5; keep them visually distinct.
- **Two staircases rhyme on purpose:** Panel 3 ("He fell … / He had changed one variable. /
  He had changed the wrong variable first. / He opened a second file.") is the comedy version;
  Panel 5 ("Sprint first. Then grow. / In that order.") is the triumphant echo. Keep their
  lettering weight and spacing identical so the rhyme reads across the page.
- **"T." (Panel 6)** is the smallest, most offhand caption in the issue — name arrives by
  coincidence and color; same family as E02's *"Windman."*: observed, not announced. "He did
  not look back." is the quiet button beneath it. Both are bottom-right, the smallest boxes
  on the page.
- **Caption count is high by design.** E03's captions are the issue's primary text and its
  comedic spine. Each box is short; the density is the point.
- **Register throughout:** dry, corporate, deadpan, case-file voice — identical to the
  narrator family of E01/E02. No exclamation, no hero-speak.
