# S01E06 — Captions

Print-ready narration captions and dialogue for [[S01E06 — Two-Body Problem]] — to letter onto the comic page beside each picture. 6 panels, 14 caption boxes plus the first direct hero dialogue in the series. Voice is the dry case-file narrator established in E01–E05, now operating in a slightly different key: the pressure is social rather than physical, and the comedy is in what two beings do not say rather than in what one being discovers. Sparse flat dialogue appears alongside the narration for the first time; the narrator does most of the work and steps aside only when the silence cannot do it. Pairs with the art in [[S01E06 — Panel Prompts]].

## Lettering notes

- **SFX — STOMP** (Panel 5): Tiebi's canonical SFX, deployed in the field for the first time. Integrated into the arrival moment — bold and kinetic, carrying the momentum of the micro-sprint. This is the same technique as E03 Panel 5's eureka; the SFX is the same sound, the same physics, now against a real-world target. Not a macro-form ground-pound — a momentum arrival. Let the weight land in the lettering.
- **SFX — HWUF** (not in this issue): Windman's canonical SFX appears in E02; the compression wave in Panel 4 is a deliberate lateral sweep, not the startled-flinch HWUF of E02. No SFX is scripted for the Panel 4 gust — the ghost-line pressure-rings in the art carry the beat.
- **Dialogue register** (Panel 6): the first direct dialogue between any two heroes in the series. Both lines are flat, not speeches — Tiebi's repetition ("You can't even SEE") is emphasis, not rising emotion. Windman's reply is equally flat, factual, without expression. Do not let either line inflate. The comedy is in the deadpan.
- **"STOMP—"** in Panel 5 and the dialogue in Panel 6 are the only non-narration lettering in the issue. Everything else is the dry case-file caption boxes.
- **The narrator button** — "Neither of them said the word 'team'" — is the driest line in the issue. Smallest, most offhand register: it names the thing by naming its absence. Do not announce it. Let it whisper.
- **"They walked the same direction anyway"** is the closing caption, same register, same quietness. The visual beat (two figures, overhead, same direction) carries the weight; the caption is the understated confirmation.
- **Deliberate pause** (Panel 6): the gap between Windman's reply and the narrator button is a beat of silence — no caption, no dialogue. Do not fill it. The pause is structural.
- **Voice continuity:** the numeric-deadpan turn ("approximately four seconds" in Panel 3) is the same register as E01's "eleven months… eleven minutes" and E02's "approximately four minutes." Keep consistent.
