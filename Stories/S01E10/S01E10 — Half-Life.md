# S01E10 — Half-Life

> **Logline:** A department that has not run in a year executes its retrieval protocol, and the one of them who is half of the original body walks into a steel box to check whether it has an exit — and finds out it does not.

---

## Title

**"Half-Life"** — the physics term for the time it takes half of a radioactive substance to decay into something else; the point at which half is gone and what remains is a different quantity than what you started with. Here: the midpoint of the twenty-episode run. The capture of half the original body. The moment at which the team's wholeness decays into a new configuration — three free, one inside a wall. The term carries its register the same way the series' prior titles have: it is a physics fact that is also a description of loss without announcing itself as one.

---

## Continuity / placement

**Movement II, Episode 1; series midpoint and largest turn so far.** Direct seam from [[S01E09 — Variance Report]]: E09 closed on `SCHEDULING RETRIEVAL` visible through frosted glass behind the four figures, visible to the reader and unseen by any hero. E10 opens as that status becomes `RETRIEVAL IN PROGRESS`. Precedes [[S01E11 — Closed Loop]], in which the three remaining outside use Tiebi's credentials to open the Retrieval Division's active case file and learn both the corporate motive and the team's auto-generated name.

> **Seam test:** does this episode open with `RETRIEVAL IN PROGRESS` as a status field update visible inside a live physical image (not a dramatic announcement, not a pure text screen), execute the capture of Primm through a containment unit that incidentally constrains his gravity field (not through tactical cleverness against a known weakness), and close on three figures outside and one inside — with Windman's loss registering in his body and in the ghost-line gap, without him naming what he is feeling? If the capture is the result of GastroDefense targeting Primm's weakness specifically, or if Windman names what he is feeling, the midpoint seam is broken.

## Format

This episode is a **5-panel comic page**. The ensemble-split structure requires a setup panel, a kinetic capture panel, an escape sequence with three distinct hero beats that can be handled in one split-register panel, and the two closing panels — the separated close and the absence register. Five is correct: six would over-explain the escape; four would rush the capture.

Panel 1 opens on a live exterior image: the staging lot at pre-dawn, the cage being readied on the flatbed, a team member's tablet showing the status update. The status text is visible inside the image — one element, not the whole frame. This is the direct seam from E09's *SCHEDULING RETRIEVAL* delivered as a physical scene, not a screen dump.

The visual space traverses two locations: an **outer city district** (the retrieval operation's coverage zone, streets, low industrial facades, Retrieval Division unit on approach) and a **Retrieval Division containment staging area** (not the Origin Facility — the steel-frame holding structure is a field-deployment unit, a portable industrial-standard cage on a flatbed staging area in a fenced lot, chain-link perimeter, one floodlight, loading dock). The district geography follows from E09's service alley exit.

The parallel-register technique from E09 is used once, economically, for the three-way escape. The rest of the episode is single-register: one situation at a time, the camera moving between the field operation and its immediate consequences. The caption register is the dry case-file narrator throughout. GastroDefense does not speak. The Retrieval Division does not monologue. It executes a workflow.

Pre-dawn, one floodlight on the chain-link perimeter, casting harsh institutional white on the flatbed and what is now visible on it: the Retrieval Division containment unit — a portable steel-frame holding structure, six-face cage, each face a grid of welded steel members. Primm is inside the structure. He phased in through the nearest face — the faint glowing blue DRIFT trails are still dissipating in the air where he entered, the semi-transparency of phasing still fading from his outline. He phased in to determine whether an exit existed on the far side. He has determined this: the exit exists on the near face, which is now being closed.

The door — the structure's single access point, a hinged face on one side — is swinging shut. Not dramatically. A Retrieval Division field team member in GastroDefense coveralls is closing it with the same body language as someone completing a task on a checklist. Inside: Primm's mini-orbs have stopped. Not the "I have the answer" stillness from E08 Panel 5 — that was a different configuration. These have stopped because the gravity field has met the steel subframe on all six faces simultaneously and found no preferential exit. The field is present; it has nowhere useful to push. His katanas (ninjato-style straight blades) are on his back. There is not enough room to use them on the latch from inside, and the latch is a spring mechanism, not a lock. His phasing: he phased in. He does not currently have one. He has found out that the exit does not exist.

The field team member with the latch is already moving toward a tablet — standard GastroDefense issue — to file the confirmation.

**Caption / dialogue:**
- CAPTION: *The structure was rated for standard equipment.*

**Why it lands:** Two captions, clean staircase. The first is a specification fact; the second is the devastation. ## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- **The capture is incidental.** Primm was captured because the Retrieval Division's containment unit is a steel-frame industrial holding structure — standard equipment-recovery spec — and the steel enclosure incidentally constrains his gravity field and removes his phasing reference frame. GastroDefense did not target Primm's metal-mass vulnerability. The protocol did not know about the metal-mass vulnerability. The field team did not know about the metal-mass vulnerability. The containment unit was the standard tool in the standard kit. The capture is a coincidence between a general-purpose procedure and a specific weakness nobody was aiming for. GastroDefense is soulless, not clever.
- **The gap in the air.** The absence Windman registers in Panel 5's ghost-line field is the fragment-resonance signal — the dormant half of his own original body, the P-03 signature that has been running at the edge of his perception since E08, now moved inside steel and out of range. He cannot name it because he has never named it. He has never had a word for what P-03 is to him. The audience, who has had that information since E01, understands what the blank field means. The heroes do not.
- **Pho-boman's invisibility, confirmed as a tactical fact.** The drone net returned a null on P-02 in Panel 1 and processed the partial recovery without a branch for the null. It will return a null again in E12 when Pho-boman infiltrates the HQ. The team's key advantage in the rescue is already established here — they do not yet know it is an advantage. Pho-boman walked out because the system could not see him, not because he planned the walk-out as a tactic.
- **The hold clause.** The ticket update reads *BOTH FRAGMENTS REQUIRED BEFORE TRANSPORT.* This means Primm is not being transported to the Origin Facility until Windman is also in custody. While three stay free — while Windman stays free — Primm has time. The audience knows this. The heroes will learn it in E11 when Tiebi opens the active case file.

## Differentiation / what's new

The reunion issues (E06–E08) were about bodies discovering each other. E09 was the first voluntary four-body episode, the first episode where knowledge replaced action, the un-make reveal landing through a document in a corporate font.

E10 is the first time the team loses.

After the convergence (E08) and the recall read (E09), this is the turn that splits them. The register shifts from the corporate-dread-accumulation of E09 into active emergency — but the antagonist is still a workflow and the capture is still an accident. The Retrieval Division is not clever. It deployed standard equipment and the standard equipment happened to work. The corporation did not design a trap; it executed a procedure. This is colder than a trap.

What is new here:
- **The split configuration.** All prior episodes have ended with the four in the same space. This one ends with one inside a steel frame and three outside. The team-count is broken. The visual grammar of four-figures has been the series' closing grammar since E06; here the count is three and the absence is the subject.
- **The fragment-resonance as loss.** E08 delivered the resonance as a residual flutter, small and involuntary. E10 delivers it as an absence — the gap in the ghost-lines, the field that used to return something returning nothing. - **The hold clause as the rescue clock.** The ticket update — *BOTH FRAGMENTS REQUIRED* — is the mechanical fact that gives the series its next movement. Reintegration is on hold. Primm has time. The clock is ticking but the mechanism is not violence; it is a workflow waiting for its next input.
- **The null result as a confirmed tactical fact.** Pho-boman's invisibility to the drone net has been in the background since E09's split-register. Here it is confirmed operationally: the net returned a null on P-02, the protocol proceeded on three, Pho-boman walked out. The team's rescue advantage is established before the team knows they will need it.
- **The three-way escape as slapstick.** The three exits — beret-first micro-form, invisible walk, vertical pressure-gap — are the series' slapstick in compressed form: not the sustained kinetic centerpiece of E08 Panel 4, but three fast individual beats, each one funnier for being so consistent with what each hero is. Tiebi's beret-on-the-outside-first is the episode's comedy peak.
