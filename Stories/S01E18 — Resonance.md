# S01E18 — Resonance

> **Logline:** The thing the two of them have been carrying since the rescue stops being background, and the one of them who was a person before any of this asks the question out loud.

> **Stage:** Broad-strokes outline. Synopsis, continuity, and threads are locked to the [[Series Arc]] (Movement IV opener). Panel breakdown, captions, and image prompts are a later production pass (panel-prompt-smith).

## Continuity / placement

**Movement IV opener.** Follows [[S01E17 — Cascade Failure]] (consent field reveal; client deadline). Precedes [[S01E19 — Consent Field]]. The un-make question reaches its first direct articulation here.

## Synopsis

The ongoing resonance Windman and Primm have lived with since [[S01E15 — Partial Contact]] becomes the subject rather than the background. The four stop having cases and have the conversation they have been building toward — not a dramatic confrontation, but the driest possible version of it. Tiebi, the only one who can phrase it (the only one who was a person before the incident), puts the specification's own question to the fragments directly, the way he navigated the substation in E09: as a route that exists and needs to be walked.

The question is not "what do you want to do?" It is the specification's: reintegration is irreversible, and the fragments will no longer exist as separate units. Having felt proximity to that state, can they describe it? They cannot — except that it does **not** feel like extinction. It feels like the word they have no word for. Pho-boman says nothing. He is to be disposed of whatever the fragments decide. He has known since [[S01E09 — Variance Report]], and has been filing it where he can reach it.

## Thread advanced

- The un-make question is put to the fragments by the only member who can ask it. The answer is description, not resolution — the question is now fully alive.
- Pho-boman's silence carries the disposal asymmetry into Movement IV.

## Twist / suspense

- The fragments' inability to call the state "extinction" is the crack the third option (E20) will grow from — the audience feels it before the heroes name it.
- AEGIS-5's client deadline is closing offscreen; "alternative enforcement" has not yet been exercised.

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- "Not like extinction" hints at what the deleted Appendix C might have protected — and at why the corporation deleted it. Reserved.
- The audience, holding the W=P truth since E01, hears the conversation as two halves of one body trying to describe their own seam.

## Differentiation / what's new

The series' most dialogue-forward episode — but kept in its dry register. After two acts of action and decode, the engine becomes a question asked plainly. The slapstick recedes; the corporate dread and the character cost share the room.

## Open for drafting

- The exact phrasing of the description ("not like extinction… the word they have no word for") is the load-bearing beat; keep it consistent with [[S01E07 — Specific Heat]] and [[S01E15 — Partial Contact]].
