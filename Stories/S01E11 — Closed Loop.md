# S01E11 — Closed Loop

> **Logline:** Three of them open a case file to find out who is hunting them, and learn two things: the corporation isn't hunting them — it's closing a ticket — and the corporation has already given them a name.

> **Stage:** Broad-strokes outline. Synopsis, continuity, and threads are locked to the [[Series Arc]] (Movement II). Panel breakdown, captions, and image prompts are a later production pass (panel-prompt-smith).

## Continuity / placement

**Movement II.** Follows [[S01E10 — Half-Life]] (Primm captured; three outside). Precedes [[S01E12 — Thermal Gradient]]. Primm does not appear (held). This is a different document and access point from E09's substation terminal — the seam rule that the heroes never saw the E09 screen is preserved.

## Synopsis

The three need to understand what they are dealing with. Tiebi uses his former security-officer credentials to open the Retrieval Division's **active case file** on the recovery operation. Its header field already reads **CRIME KICKERS** — auto-generated from the E09 incident log, now the official label for the open ticket. Tiebi reads it aloud. Not triumphantly: the name arrives the way a case number arrives. They absorb it without ceremony and use it functionally from here on.

The same file reveals the recall is being executed under contract performance notice GD-C-4419: a law-enforcement client filed a notice citing GastroDefense's failure to deliver a completed AEGIS-4 unit. **This is liability management, not a vendetta** (Twist 1). The corporation would rather close the file than chase anyone.

## Thread advanced

- **Name inheritance:** the team takes on CRIME KICKERS, bureaucratically, off the page.
- **Corporate motive decoded:** the threat is a workflow and a contract, not malice.

## Twist / suspense

- **Twist 1 — the recall is a contract dispute.** Every apparent menace was always a workflow step.
- **The lever:** the file flags that W-01 and P-03 are reintegrated *together*. Reintegration is on hold until Windman is also in custody. Windman is both the reason GastroDefense needs Primm and the reason Primm is not yet un-made. While the three stay free, Primm has time. (Sets the rescue clock.)

## What the audience knows that the heroes don't

- The name in the case header derives from "kicking-type force (Unit T-04)" — Tiebi's STOMP. The team is named after the one of them who has a human past.
- GD-C-4419 was already visible in the E09 recall specification; the contract was always the engine.

## Differentiation / what's new

The series' first "decode" episode after the midpoint loss — knowledge replaces action. Learning the motive does not help them get Primm out; it reframes the whole pursuit as paperwork, which is colder and funnier than a villain would be.
